# E71.15 -- Proof attempt for the stable Riesz/Fredholm projection theorem

**Date:** 2026-07-07.
**Role:** attempt the proof requested for E71.14 and isolate exactly which step is still open.

## The theorem we want

E71.14 asks for intrinsic projections

```text
Pi_N^{phys}
```

defined from the CCM Weyl data

```text
(D_N, xi_N),       m_N(s)=sum_k xi_{N,k}/(s-d_{N,k}),
```

such that the physical determinant

```text
D_N^{phys}(s)
```

has only real zeros and converges locally uniformly:

```text
D_N^{phys}(s) -> Xi(s)/Xi(0).
```

If proved, E71.5 gives RH by Hurwitz.

## What can be proved now

There is a rigorous conditional theorem behind the persistence experiments.

### Theorem 15.1 -- compactness of a stable real divisor

Let `R_N` be finite subsets of `R` and let `S_N subset R_N` be selected by a rule depending only on
the finite data up to level `N`. Assume:

1. **local counting bound:** for every `T>0`,

```text
sup_N # (S_N cap [-T,T]) < infinity;
```

2. **gap at the origin:** there is `c_0>0` such that

```text
|r| >= c_0
```

for every `r in S_N` and every `N`;

3. **tail genus bound:** for every compact `K subset C`,

```text
sum_{r in S_N, |r|>2 sup_K |z|} sup_{z in K} |z^2/r^2|
```

is bounded uniformly in `N`;

4. **base normalization:**

```text
D_N(0)=1,
D_N(z)=product_{r in S_N, r>0}(1-z^2/r^2)
```

with the product finite at every `N`.

Then `{D_N}` is a normal family on `C`. Every locally uniform subsequential limit is an even entire
function with only real zeros.

### Proof

Fix a compact `K subset C`, and let `R_K=sup_{z in K}|z|`. Split the finite product into

```text
|r| <= 2R_K
```

and

```text
|r| > 2R_K.
```

The first part has uniformly bounded degree by the local counting bound. Hence it is locally bounded
on `K`: there are only uniformly many factors, the roots lie in a fixed compact interval, and the
origin gap prevents any factor `1-z^2/r^2` from blowing up on `K`.

For the tail, use

```text
|log(1-z^2/r^2)| <= C_K |z|^2/r^2
```

whenever `|r|>2R_K`. The tail genus bound gives uniform boundedness of the logarithm of the tail
product on `K`.

Thus `{D_N}` is locally bounded. By Montel, it is normal. Any locally uniform limit of real-rooted
even polynomials has only real zeros by Hurwitz: if a non-real zero appeared, a small disk around it
disjoint from `R` would have to contain zeros of `D_N` eventually, impossible. ∎

## What this proves for E71.14

If we can construct `Pi_N^{phys}` so that its finite spectrum `S_N` satisfies the two bounds above,
then we get:

```text
D_N^{phys} has subsequential locally uniform limits,
and every such limit has only real zeros.
```

This is the part of the theorem that is genuinely operator-theoretic and does not touch Weil
positivity.

## The missing identification step

The theorem still does **not** identify the subsequential limit with

```text
Xi(s)/Xi(0).
```

For that, one needs:

### Identification Lemma

Every subsequential normal limit `D_*` of the intrinsic physical determinants satisfies

```text
D_*(s) = Xi(s)/Xi(0).
```

Equivalently, one of the following must be proved from CCM data:

1. **log-derivative convergence**

```text
(D_N^{phys})'/D_N^{phys} -> Xi'/Xi
```

locally uniformly away from the divisor;

2. **trace/test convergence**

```text
sum_{r in S_N} h(r) -> sum_rho h(gamma_rho)
```

for a uniqueness class of entire test functions;

3. **canonical determinant convergence**

```text
D_N^{phys}(s) -> Xi(s)/Xi(0)
```

on a real interval with a normal-family extension.

This is the real content of CAND-1.

## Why persistence alone cannot prove the identification

The planted control from E71.12 is decisive.

For the planted off-line perturbation, the same zero-independent persistence rule finds a stable
cluster:

```text
25.001074
```

near the planted height, while the coherent zeta divisor disappears. This shows:

```text
persistence detects stable spectral data,
but persistence alone does not know that the stable data are Xi.
```

Therefore a proof that uses only "stable roots remain stable" cannot identify the limit with `Xi`.
It would also accept stable non-zeta divisors generated by perturbed input.

The identification must use the exact CCM Euler/Gamma construction, not just abstract stability.

## Attempt to prove the Identification Lemma

The natural path is to use the CCM explicit formula behind `QW`.

The finite data give:

```text
QW_N = arch_N - prime_N,
xi_N = ground vector of QW_N,
m_N(s)=sum_k xi_{N,k}/(s-d_{N,k}).
```

The desired conclusion would follow if one proves the strong convergence:

```text
m_N^{phys}(s) -> Xi'(s)/Xi(s) + entire_background'(s)
```

with a background whose integrated determinant cancels canonically.

But E71.11 tested the obvious backgrounds:

```text
all roots,
pole mesh,
sine comb,
```

and all failed. E71.9 tested the arch/free spectral quotient and it failed. Therefore the background
is not a simple static object.

The only remaining viable form is a Fredholm/scattering background tied to the finite-section
comparison maps:

```text
N -> N'.
```

This is exactly the intrinsic `Pi_N^{phys}` missing from E71.14.

## Exact open proposition

The proof of E71.14 is reduced to one proposition:

### Proposition 15.2 -- CCM Stable Divisor Identification

There exists a functorial finite-section comparison structure on the CCM Weyl data `(D_N,xi_N)` whose
stable Riesz divisor `S_N` satisfies:

```text
sum_{r in S_N} h(r) -> sum_rho h(gamma_rho)
```

for every test function `h` in a uniqueness class for even entire functions of order one.

If Proposition 15.2 holds, then:

1. Theorem 15.1 gives normal-family compactness and real-zero subsequential limits.
2. Proposition 15.2 identifies every subsequential limit with `Xi/Xi(0)`.
3. E71.5/Hurwitz implies RH.

## Why this is not yet a proof

Proposition 15.2 is not proved here.

And it cannot be silently assumed: it is precisely the operator-convergence frontier of CAND-1. In
the language of the NO-GO audit, this is where MW-2 could re-enter if the proof requires propagation
of Euler information into the critical strip.

So the honest status is:

```text
proved:  stable real divisors satisfying counting/tail bounds have real-zero normal limits;
open:    the intrinsic CCM stable divisor is Xi's divisor.
```

## Final verdict of the proof attempt

The requested theorem is not proved unconditionally in this document.

What is proved is the compactness/Hurwitz half. The remaining half is the exact mathematical core:

```text
construct Pi_N^{phys} intrinsically and prove its divisor converges to the Xi divisor.
```

That statement is equivalent in force to closing the CAND-1 convergence problem, and hence to RH via
E71.5.
