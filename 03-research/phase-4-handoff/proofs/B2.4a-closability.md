# B2.4a (continued) — Closability: the missing logical bridge

**Why this file exists.** Day 2 (`B2.4a-de-branges-norm.md`) found that a de Branges space $H(E)$ has
bounded strip evaluations, dissolving the Day-1 $L^2$ pathology, and jumped to "B-2 $\equiv$ chain
condition." The referee flagged that jump as premature and identified the **true immediate question**:

> **Is the Weil form $\mathfrak t$ closable in the archimedean de Branges space of $\gamma$?**

This is logically *prior* to constructing $E_{T_0,\sigma}$ or invoking a chain. Until it is settled, the
passage to $H(E)$ is not justified — it might silently change the problem. This file (a) states the
trivialization risk precisely, (b) gives the **sign/value invariance principle** that bounds how much
damage the norm can do, (c) reduces closability to one verifiable analytic input, and (d) lists the
prerequisite that must be checked *first*. **Rigor tags:** ✅ rigorous · ⚠️ unverified prerequisite ·
⟶ reduction.

---

## 0. PREREQUISITE that gates everything: the weight asymptotic ⚠️
The de Branges norm is $\|F\|_{H(E)}^2=\int_{\mathbb R}|F(x)|^2|E(x)|^{-2}dx$. The **entire downstream
analysis depends on the asymptotic of $|E(x)|^{-2}$**, and Day 2 asserted it without proof. Two candidates:

| Candidate weight $|E(x)|^{-2}$ | Source | Consequence for B-2 |
|---|---|---|
| $\sim e^{+\pi|x|/2}$ (exponential) | modulus $|\gamma(\tfrac12+ix)|^{-2}$ | norm $\ggg$ Weil energy ⟹ archimedean negligible ⟹ **trivialization risk REAL** |
| $\sim\log|x|$ (logarithmic) | de Branges density of states $\varphi'(x)\sim\tfrac1{2\pi}\log\tfrac{|x|}{2\pi}$ | norm $\approx$ Weil energy $\mathfrak a$ ⟹ **no trivialization; bottom $\approx$ Weil bottom** |

These are not the same object: $|\gamma|$ is the *modulus*, $\varphi'$ is the *phase derivative*. The de
Branges space weight is governed by the phase/density, but the Hermite–Biehler $E$ ties modulus and phase
together, so the honest answer requires actually writing $E$ for the archimedean factor and reading off
$|E(x)|$ on $\mathbb R$. **Action: settle this from de Branges/Lagarias before any further structural
claim.** If the weight is logarithmic, the referee's trivialization worry largely evaporates and B-2 may
be genuinely tractable in $H(E)$ with the *right* bottom; if exponential, §2's invariance principle is
what saves the RH content. Either way §0 is the first computation to do.

---

## 1. The trivialization risk, stated precisely (the referee's worry) ✅
Suppose the weight is exponential. Then for $g$ with $\widehat g$ concentrating at large $|r|$,
$$
\frac{\mathfrak a(g)}{\|g\|_{H(E)}^2}=\frac{\int|\widehat g|^2\,\Omega(r)\,dr}{\int|\widehat g|^2\,e^{\pi|r|/2}\,dr}\ \xrightarrow{\ \widehat g\to\text{high freq}\ }\ 0,
$$
i.e. the archimedean form becomes **negligible** relative to the norm. If, in addition, the prime form
$\mathfrak p$ were $H(E)$-bounded, then $\mathfrak t=\mathfrak a+\mathfrak q-\mathfrak p$ would be a
**bounded** form on $H(E)$, hence trivially semibounded — and B-2 would be *true for free*, as a property
of the chosen space rather than of $\zeta$. **That is the danger:** semiboundedness in a too-strong norm
is vacuous. So semiboundedness-in-$H(E)$ is **not** the prize; it could be empty content.

---

## 2. The sign/value invariance principle — what the norm CANNOT corrupt ✅
The risk in §1 is real but **bounded in scope**. Decompose the two properties of $\mathfrak t$:
$$
\underbrace{\inf\operatorname{spec}\ge0}_{\text{RH}}\iff\mathfrak t(g,g)\ge0\ \forall g\in\mathcal D\quad(\textbf{SIGN}),
\qquad
\underbrace{\inf\operatorname{spec}>-\infty}_{\text{B-2}}\quad(\textbf{VALUE/finiteness}).
$$
**Lemma (norm-invariance of the sign).** The condition "$\mathfrak t(g,g)\ge0$ for all $g\in\mathcal D$"
does not mention the inner product; it is the **same** statement in $L^2$, in $H(E)$, in any space with
the same form-core $\mathcal D$. Hence:
$$
\boxed{\ \text{sign}\big(\inf\operatorname{spec}\big)\ \text{is norm-independent}\ =\ \text{RH};\qquad \text{only finiteness/value is norm-dependent.}\ }
$$
**Consequence.** The norm change **cannot hide or fabricate RH**: it can only render the spectral
realization well-posed (finite bottom, self-adjoint operator) or not. The trivialization of §1 affects
the *value* (B-2 finiteness), never the *sign* (RH). So the faithful-ladder dream, which is about the
**sign of the limit**, is not endangered by choosing a strong norm — *provided the sign actually transfers
to the closure*, which is exactly closability (§3).

> **This reframes the whole flank honestly.** B-2 (semiboundedness) may indeed be *easy or even vacuous*
> in the right $H(E)$ — and that is acceptable, because B-2 was never the carrier of RH. The carrier is
> the **sign**, and the real deliverable of the "faithful ladder" is: *a well-posed self-adjoint
> realization whose bottom's sign equals RH.* Semiboundedness is the well-posedness wrapper; closability
> is what guarantees the wrapper preserves the sign.

---

## 3. Closability as the bridge, reduced to one input ⟶
A symmetric form is **closable** iff the zero element is the only "singular" limit: whenever $g_n\to0$ in
$\|\cdot\|_{H(E)}$ and $\mathfrak t(g_n-g_m)\to0$, then $\mathfrak t(g_n)\to0$. Decompose
$\mathfrak t=\mathfrak a+\mathfrak q-\mathfrak p$:

- **$\mathfrak a$ (archimedean).** Multiplication form by $\Omega\ge\Omega_{\min}$. If the weight is
  exponential (§0), $\mathfrak a$ is $H(E)$-**bounded** (§1) hence trivially closable; if logarithmic,
  $\mathfrak a\approx\|\cdot\|^2$ and is the closed form of a positive self-adjoint operator. **Either way
  $\mathfrak a$ is closable.** ✅
- **$\mathfrak q$ (pole).** Rank-2, bounded on $\mathcal D$ (strip evaluations bounded in $H(E)$, DB.3).
  Bounded perturbation ⟹ does not affect closability. ✅
- **$\mathfrak p$ (prime).** The only dangerous term. Closability of $\mathfrak t$ reduces to: **is
  $\mathfrak p$ relatively $H(E)$-bounded (or at least relatively closable) with respect to $\mathfrak a+\|\cdot\|^2$?**

So closability of $\mathfrak t$ in $H(E)$ $\Longleftrightarrow$ **the single analytic question**:
$$
\boxed{\ \exists\,a<\infty,\ b:\quad |\mathfrak p(g)|\le a\,\|g\|_{H(E)}^2+b\,\mathfrak a(g)\quad\text{on }\mathcal D\ ?}\qquad(\mathrm{C\text{-}bound})
$$
Note this is **KLMN relative to the $H(E)$ norm** — the very inequality that *failed* in $L^2$ (B2.3),
but now in a stronger norm where it may *succeed*. The mechanism: $\mathfrak p(g)$ is a weighted sum of
$(g\star\tilde g)(\log n)$, and $(g\star\tilde g)(t)=\frac1{2\pi}\int|\widehat g|^2 e^{irt}dr$; the
$H(E)$-norm controls $\int|\widehat g|^2|E|^{-2}$. So (C-bound) is an inequality between the prime measure
$\nu$ and the de Branges weight $|E|^{-2}$ — **rigorous, no distributions, no $\inf_r$.** This is the
honest, form-level successor to the (retracted) Day-1 multiplier picture.

**The clean sub-question to attack first (after §0):** for $g\in H(E)$, how fast does
$(g\star\tilde g)(\log n)$ decay in $n$? The $H(E)$ norm forces decay of $|\widehat g(r)|^2$ (rate set by
$|E|^{-2}$), which forces *smoothness/analyticity* of $g\star\tilde g$ — but smoothness gives **analytic
continuation, not real-axis decay**. So (C-bound) is delicate exactly where Day 1 was delicate; it is the
same difficulty, correctly posed. ⟶

---

## 4. Trichotomy — the three possible verdicts, all informative ✅
Once §0 (weight) and (C-bound) are settled, exactly one holds:

1. **$\mathfrak t$ closable & bounded below, non-trivially** (C-bound holds, weight not so strong as to
   trivialize). ⟹ a genuine self-adjoint bounded-below realization exists, its bottom's sign is RH (§2),
   and the faithful ladder is well-posed. **Best case** — proceed to construct $E_{T_0,\sigma}$ and the
   chain.
2. **$\mathfrak t$ closable but trivially bounded** (weight exponential, $\mathfrak p$ also $H(E)$-bounded).
   ⟹ B-2 is vacuous; *all* content is in the sign (§2). Honest but deflationary: the "weaker-than-RH"
   hope collapses on this flank, and the program reduces to the sign $=$ RH with no separation gained.
3. **$\mathfrak t$ NOT closable in $H(E)$** ((C-bound) fails). ⟹ $H(E)$ is the **wrong space**; the bottom
   would be a property of the completion, not of $\zeta$ (the referee's exact worry realized). ⟹ must find
   a different norm, or conclude no de Branges realization is faithful — itself a strong negative result
   locating the obstruction.

**None of the three is a dead end for the program’s *knowledge*** — each pins the obstruction somewhere
definite. But only case 1 keeps the faithful-ladder dream alive, so case 1 is what we are testing.

## 5. Status and the corrected priority order

| Step | Statement | Status |
|---|---|---|
| §0 | weight asymptotic of $|E|^{-2}$ (log vs exponential) | ⚠️ **unverified — do first** |
| §1 | trivialization risk is real *iff* weight is exponential | ✅ (conditional) |
| §2 | sign/value invariance: norm can't hide RH; sign transfers $\iff$ closable | ✅ |
| §3 | closability $\iff$ (C-bound): KLMN of $\mathfrak p$ in the $H(E)$ norm | ⟶ (reduced; analytic input open) |
| §4 | trichotomy of verdicts | ✅ (framework) |

**Corrected priority (per referee).** *Do not* build $E_{T_0,\sigma}$ yet. Order:
1. **§0** — fix the archimedean de Branges weight (log vs exp) from de Branges/Lagarias. One literature
   computation; gates everything.
2. **§3 (C-bound)** — decide closability: prove or refute the $H(E)$-relative KLMN bound on $\mathfrak p$,
   via the decay of $(g\star\tilde g)(\log n)$ for $g\in H(E)$.
3. Read **Bombieri**, *Remarks on Weil's quadratic functional* — he works the form on a space where these
   evaluations are bounded; his norm is almost certainly the answer to §0 and a model for §3.
4. **Only if case 1 (§4) holds**, construct $E_{T_0,\sigma}$ and the chain.

**Net (Day 3).** The referee's two corrections were both right and both improved the work. The `≡` was
downgraded to `⤳`. The deep worry ("strong norm trivializes the instability") is now precisely bounded by
the **sign/value invariance principle**: the norm can corrupt the *value* (B-2) but never the *sign* (RH),
and closability is exactly the guarantee that the sign survives completion. Closability itself reduces to
one rigorous, distribution-free inequality (C-bound) — the $L^2$-failed KLMN re-asked in the $H(E)$ norm —
gated by the still-unverified weight asymptotic (§0). The immediate, concrete next computation is §0.
