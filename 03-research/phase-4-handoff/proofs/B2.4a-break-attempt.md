## 0. CL.0 resolved — the weight is exponential (a double self-correction) ✅/◆
The de Branges norm is $\|F\|_{H(E)}^2=\int_{\mathbb R}|F(x)|^2|E(x)|^{-2}dx$ with $F=\widehat g$. Two
quantities were conflated across Days 2–3; both are now pinned and they are **different objects**:
- **Norm weight** $|E(x)|^{-2}$. With $E$ the archimedean structure function, $|E(x)|^2\asymp|\gamma(\tfrac12+ix)|^2\sim C|x|^{-1/2}e^{-\pi|x|/2}$,
  so $|E(x)|^{-2}\asymp|x|^{1/2}e^{+\pi|x|/2}$ — **exponential**. (Finiteness check: our Gaussian
  localizers have $|\widehat g|^2\sim e^{-(x-T_0)^2/\sigma^2}$, which beats $e^{\pi|x|/2}$, so
  $\widehat g\in H(E_\gamma)$. ✅ consistent.)
- **Density of states** $\varphi'(x)\sim\tfrac1{2\pi}\log\tfrac{|x|}{2\pi}$ — **logarithmic**. This
  governs real-axis *sampling*, not the norm.

> **Correction of the corrections.** Day 2 asserted "exponential" without proof; Day 3 over-hedged
> ("maybe it's log"). The truth: the **norm weight is exponential** (Day-2 instinct right), the
> **density is log** (Day-3 object real but it is not the norm). So we are in the **strong-norm regime**
> and the referee's trivialization worry is live — proceed to test it.

## 1. The break: $\mathfrak t$ is NOT semibounded in $H(E_\gamma)$ ◆ (scaling argument)
Strategy: exhibit a normalized family in $H(E_\gamma)$ on which $\mathfrak t\to-\infty$. Take
$\widehat g_\varepsilon$ a bump of width $\varepsilon\to0$ centered at a fixed height $T_0$ (so
$g_\varepsilon$ is **wide in $u$**, scale $1/\varepsilon$). Track the three pieces.

**Norm.** Since $\widehat g_\varepsilon$ is concentrated at $T_0$,
$$
\|g_\varepsilon\|_{H(E)}^2=\int|\widehat g_\varepsilon|^2 e^{\pi|x|/2}|x|^{1/2}dx\ \asymp\ e^{\pi|T_0|/2}\,\|\widehat g_\varepsilon\|_{L^2}^2 .
$$
**Crucially, the weight depends on the *location* $T_0$, not on the *width* $\varepsilon$.**

**Prime form.** $(g_\varepsilon\star\tilde g_\varepsilon)(t)=\tfrac1{2\pi}\int|\widehat g_\varepsilon|^2 e^{ixt}dx\approx \tfrac1{2\pi}\|\widehat g_\varepsilon\|_{L^2}^2\,e^{iT_0t}\,\Theta_\varepsilon(t)$,
where $\Theta_\varepsilon$ is a slowly-decaying envelope of width $\sim1/\varepsilon$ ($\Theta_\varepsilon(0)=1$).
Hence
$$
\mathfrak p(g_\varepsilon)\approx\frac{\|\widehat g_\varepsilon\|_{L^2}^2}{\pi}\,\mathrm{Re}\!\!\sum_{n:\,\log n\lesssim1/\varepsilon}\!\!\frac{\Lambda(n)}{\sqrt n}\,n^{iT_0}\,\Theta_\varepsilon(\log n)
\ \asymp\ \frac{\|\widehat g_\varepsilon\|_{L^2}^2}{\pi}\,\mathrm{Re}\!\sum_{n\le N}\frac{\Lambda(n)}{n^{1/2-iT_0}},\quad N=e^{1/\varepsilon}.
$$
By partial summation against PNT, $\sum_{n\le N}\Lambda(n)\,n^{-1/2+iT_0}\sim \dfrac{N^{1/2+iT_0}}{\tfrac12+iT_0}$,
**growing like $\sqrt N=e^{1/2\varepsilon}$** (the Chebyshev engine again). So
$$
\mathfrak p(g_\varepsilon)\ \asymp\ \|\widehat g_\varepsilon\|_{L^2}^2\cdot e^{1/2\varepsilon}\cdot(\text{bounded oscillation in }T_0).
$$
**Archimedean + pole** are $O(\log)$ and $O(1)$ relative to $\|\widehat g_\varepsilon\|_{L^2}^2$ —
negligible against $e^{1/2\varepsilon}$.

**The Rayleigh quotient.**
$$
\frac{\mathfrak t(g_\varepsilon)}{\|g_\varepsilon\|_{H(E)}^2}\ \asymp\ \frac{-\,\|\widehat g_\varepsilon\|_{L^2}^2\,e^{1/2\varepsilon}}{e^{\pi|T_0|/2}\,\|\widehat g_\varepsilon\|_{L^2}^2}\ =\ -\,\frac{e^{1/2\varepsilon}}{e^{\pi|T_0|/2}}\ \xrightarrow[\varepsilon\to0]{}\ -\infty .
$$
(The sign of the oscillation can be fixed negative by choosing $T_0$; or symmetrized over a quartet.)
**Conclusion: $\inf\operatorname{spec}_{H(E_\gamma)}\mathfrak t=-\infty$ — the Weil form is _not_
semibounded in the pure de Branges space of $\gamma$.** ◆

**Why the strong norm fails to save it.** The exponential weight $e^{\pi|x|/2}$ is keyed to the
**location** $x=T_0$; it is blind to the **width** $\varepsilon$. But the prime form blows up in the
**width** direction (narrow $\widehat g$ $\Rightarrow$ wide $g$ $\Rightarrow$ reaches $N=e^{1/\varepsilon}$
primes $\Rightarrow$ $\sqrt N$ Chebyshev growth). An amplitude-by-location weight cannot control a
width-driven divergence. **This is the precise break of DB.4-as-stated.**

## 2. Two consistency checks (the break is real, not an artifact) ✅
- **Band-limited test functions: bounded (known).** If $\widehat g\in PW_\tau$ (exponential type $\tau$),
  then $g$ is supported in $[-\tau,\tau]$, $g\star\tilde g$ in $[-2\tau,2\tau]$, so $\mathfrak p$ is a
  **finite** sum over $n\le e^{2\tau}$ and $\mathfrak t$ is **bounded** — semibounded trivially. This is
  the classical "tame on band-limited test functions" phenomenon (Weil/Bombieri–Lagarias explicit-formula
  positivity is studied exactly there). The blow-up of §1 uses the **opposite** regime (wide-in-$u$,
  non-band-limited), so there is no contradiction — and it locates the RH content precisely in the
  **non-band-limited limit**, as expected.
- **v9 numerics: no contradiction.** v9 fixed $\sigma$ and grew $J$ (more Hermite modes at fixed scale);
  §1 sends $\sigma\to0$ (narrowing scale). Different directions. v9's observed stability is in the
  fixed-scale direction; the divergence lives in the scale direction it did not probe. ⚠️ **This is a
  concrete, falsifiable prediction: a v10 that scans $\sigma\to0$ should see $\lambda_{\min}\to-\infty$ in
  the $L^2$/de Branges normalization.**

## 3. Verdict and the corrected geometry ⟶
**DB.4 is broken as stated.** A *pure* de Branges (amplitude-by-location) norm gets **half** the job
(DB.3: bounded off-line evaluations ✅) but **fails the other half** (§1: it does not bound the prime
form / does not make $\mathfrak t$ semibounded). The faithful norm must control **both**:
$$
\boxed{\ \text{faithful norm}\ =\ \underbrace{\text{amplitude/location control}}_{\text{de Branges: off-line evals bounded}}\ +\ \underbrace{\text{width/smoothness control}}_{\text{tames the }\sqrt N\text{ prime blow-up}}\ }
$$
A norm controlling **both position and momentum** symmetrically in the frequency variable is a
**Fock/Bargmann (Gaussian phase-space) norm** — and its natural orthonormal basis is precisely the
**Hermite functions**, i.e. *our localizers*. So the break points at:

> ⟶ **The faithful space is plausibly the Fock/Bargmann space, not a de Branges space.** The program's
> original Hermite–Gauss window was, in hindsight, already the right geometry; the Day-2 "de Branges"
> framing captured the location half and missed the momentum half. The chain picture (DB.4) should be
> replaced/refined by a Gaussian phase-space picture.

This also **explains the sign/value principle in action (CL §2):** semiboundedness genuinely failed in
$H(E_\gamma)$ (a *value/finiteness* failure, norm-dependent) — exactly as the principle warns the value
is norm-dependent. RH's sign content is untouched; what we learned is that $H(E_\gamma)$ is simply not a
*well-posed* home for the form.

## 5. RETRACTION (Day 5) — BR.1 is false; the break does not hold ❌

**Trigger.** Attempting (per referee) to upgrade BR.1 from ◆ to ✅ exposed that it is wrong. The referee
had flagged the replacement $\Theta_\varepsilon(\log n)\rightsquigarrow\mathbf 1_{\log n\le1/\varepsilon}$
as "not innocent." It is in fact **fatal**.

**The clean disproof (explicit-formula identity).** For $g\in\mathcal D$,
$$
\mathfrak t(g,g)=\mathfrak a(g)+\mathfrak q(g)-\mathfrak p(g)=\sum_\rho|\widehat g(\gamma_\rho)|^2
\quad\text{(analytic continuation; an \emph{identity}).}
$$
Take $\widehat g_\varepsilon$ a smooth bump of width $\varepsilon\to0$ at a **generic** $T_0$ (not a zero
ordinate). The right side counts only zeros within $\sim\varepsilon$ of $T_0$, hence is **finite and
$\to0$** (no zero is hit). Therefore $\mathfrak p(g_\varepsilon)=\mathfrak a+\mathfrak q-\sum_\rho|\cdots|^2$
is **finite** — there is **no $\sqrt N$ blow-up**. Equivalently, $\mathfrak p(g_\varepsilon)=\frac1\pi\langle|\widehat g_\varepsilon|^2,\Phi\rangle\to\frac1\pi\Phi(T_0)\|\widehat g\|^2$
with $\Phi(T_0)=2\,\mathrm{Re}(-\zeta'/\zeta(\tfrac12+iT_0))$ finite at generic $T_0$. The pointwise-divergent
prime series is **resummed to a finite value by the smooth test function** — exactly the oscillatory
cancellation the referee predicted. The hard cutoff destroyed that cancellation, manufacturing a fake
divergence.

**Scope of the retraction.**
- **BR.1 ❌** ($\mathfrak t$ not semibounded in $H(E_\gamma)$): **false**. Withdrawn.
- **BR.3 ❌** (Fock/Bargmann pivot): it rested entirely on BR.1; **withdrawn / shelved** — no longer
  motivated. (Fock may still be a *fine* space, but there is no evidence it is *required*.)
- **B2.3 (Day 0)** ("naive KLMN fails; $\mathfrak p\gg\mathfrak a$"): used the **same hard-cutoff error**.
  Its conclusion is **not established**; by the identity, $\mathfrak p$ is *comparable* to $\mathfrak a$
  for smooth test functions. Downgraded to ❌-reasoning (the question of soft-vs-arithmetic is reopened,
  now correctly via off-line zeros, not prime partial sums).
- **CL.0 ✅** (de Branges weight is exponential): independent of BR.1; **survives**.
- **DB.1–DB.3 ✅**, **Day-1 off-axis-evaluation mechanism ✅**: untouched.

**Where this leaves B-2 (the honest, corrected picture).** The **only** rigorous source of $L^2$
unboundedness is **Day 1's**: an *off-line* quartet contributes $4\,\mathrm{Re}[\widehat g(t-ib)^2]$
(indefinite; see `EF-identity-audit.md` Q2 for the exact form — **not** $\sum|\widehat g(\gamma_\rho)|^2$,
that shorthand is valid only under RH) via **off-axis point-evaluations**, unbounded on $L^2$. In a de
Branges space $H(E_\gamma)$ these evaluations are **bounded (DB.3)**, so each off-line zero contributes a
**bounded** amount. Hence:
$$
\boxed{\ \text{B-2 in }H(E_\gamma)\ \rightsquigarrow\ \text{is }\sum_{\text{off-line }\rho}(\text{bounded }H(E)\text{-terms})\text{ bounded below?}\ =\ \text{a zero-density question.}\ }
$$
This is **exactly §3.3's original conjecture** ("B-2 sits at the zero-density tier"), now reached cleanly
and with the prime-blow-up phantom removed. **$H(E_\gamma)$ is back in play**; the chain/Fock detours were
chasing an artifact.
