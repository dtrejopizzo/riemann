# B2.4a / D(i) — The de Branges norm: does a natural $H(E)$ exist where strip evaluations are bounded?

**Question (the one Day 1 said is logically prior to semiboundedness).** Is there a Hilbert space of
the admissible test functions in which (i) the off-axis point-evaluations $\widehat g(a\pm ib)$,
$|b|<\tfrac12$, are **bounded** functionals, and (ii) the Weil form $\mathfrak t$ is **closable**? If yes,
B-2 is well-posed and we can then ask for semiboundedness (B2.4b); if the only such space forces exact
positivity, then $\text{semibounded}\iff\text{RH}$ and the separation collapses.

This file pursues that question. **Rigor tags:** ✅ rigorous · ◆ classical (cite) · ⟶ strategic
reduction (motivated, not yet a theorem).

Imports A.0; $\gamma(s)=\pi^{-s/2}\Gamma(s/2)$ is the archimedean factor of $\xi(s)=\tfrac12 s(s-1)\gamma(s)\zeta(s)$.

---

## DB.1 — The archimedean multiplier *is* the log-derivative of the Gamma factor ✅
Direct computation: $\frac{\gamma'}{\gamma}(s)=-\tfrac12\log\pi+\tfrac12\psi(\tfrac s2)$, so at $s=\tfrac12+ir$,
$$
2\,\mathrm{Re}\,\frac{\gamma'}{\gamma}\!\big(\tfrac12+ir\big)
=-\log\pi+\mathrm{Re}\,\psi\!\big(\tfrac14+\tfrac{ir}{2}\big)
=\boxed{\ \Omega(r)\ }.
$$
So the "free" part $\Omega(D)$ of $\mathcal T$ is **exactly the Gamma-factor geometry**. This is not a
coincidence: in the explicit formula the archimedean term *is* the contribution of $\gamma$. **The
ambient geometry of B is the archimedean (de Branges) geometry of $\gamma$.**

---

## DB.2 — The width-$\tfrac12$ triple coincidence ✅
Three constants that entered separately are the **same** number:
1. the **admissible-class strip width** $>\tfrac12$ forced by the pole term $\mathfrak q$ (A1 §3.2);
2. the **off-line displacement** range $|b|=|\beta-\tfrac12|<\tfrac12$ of any nontrivial zero (since
   $\beta\in(0,1)$) — the points where evaluation must be bounded (B2 §5.3);
3. the **first singularity of the Gamma factor**: $\gamma(\tfrac12+iz)=\pi^{-(1/4+iz/2)}\Gamma(\tfrac14+\tfrac{iz}{2})$
   has its first pole where $\tfrac14+\tfrac{iz}{2}=0$, i.e. at $z=\tfrac i2$. So $\gamma(\tfrac12+iz)$ is
   analytic and zero-free **exactly in the open strip $|\Im z|<\tfrac12$**, with the singularity on its
   edge.

The geometry that bounds strip evaluations, the strip where zeros can hide, and the analyticity domain
of the archimedean factor are **one strip**. This is strong evidence that the correct space is the
**archimedean de Branges space of $\gamma$**, scaled to width $\tfrac12$.

---

## DB.3 — In a de Branges space, strip evaluations are bounded — this *dissolves* the $L^2$ obstruction ◆
Recall the $L^2$ obstruction (B2 §5.3): on $L^2(\mathbb R,du)$, $\mathrm{ev}_{a\pm ib}$ is unbounded
because $e^{\mp bu}\notin L^2$. A **de Branges space** $H(E)$ (Hermite–Biehler $E$, norm
$\|F\|^2_{H(E)}=\int_{\mathbb R}|F(x)/E(x)|^2\,dx$) consists of *entire* functions and has a reproducing
kernel $K_w\in H(E)$ at **every** $w\in\mathbb C$:
$$
F(w)=\langle F,K_w\rangle_{H(E)},\qquad
K_w(z)=\frac{\overline{E(w)}E(z)-\overline{E^\#(w)}E^\#(z)}{2\pi i\,(\bar w-z)} .
$$
Hence $|\mathrm{ev}_w|=\|K_w\|=K(w,w)^{1/2}<\infty$ for all $w$ — in particular for $w=a\pm ib$,
$|b|<\tfrac12$. **So the very pathology that broke B-2 in $L^2$ simply does not occur in $H(E)$:
off-axis evaluations are automatically bounded.** This answers half of B2.4a affirmatively: *a space
with bounded strip evaluations exists* — the de Branges space of the archimedean factor.

The weight: ⚠️ **UNVERIFIED — flagged (Day 3).** I previously wrote $|E(x)|^{-2}\asymp|x|^{1/2}e^{+\pi|x|/2}$,
reading off the *modulus* $|\gamma(\tfrac12+ix)|^2\sim C|x|^{-1/2}e^{-\pi|x|/2}$. But the de Branges weight
$|E|^{-2}$ is the **density of states** $\varphi'$ (where $E=|E|e^{-i\varphi}$), and for the archimedean
factor the density is $\varphi'(x)\sim\tfrac1{2\pi}\log\tfrac{|x|}{2\pi}$ — **logarithmic, not exponential**.
The modulus $|\gamma|$ and the phase density $\varphi'$ are *different* objects; conflating them is exactly
the kind of slip that broke $\Phi$ (Day 1). **Which one is the H(E) weight decides everything** (see
`B2.4a-closability.md` §0). Do not build on the exponential claim until verified. (The precise
Hermite–Biehler $E$ for the archimedean factor is classical — de Branges, Lagarias — but its weight
asymptotic must be quoted correctly, not guessed.)

## DB.4 — The catch, and why it *fuses B into D* ✅ obstruction / ⟶ reduction
There is a price, and it is the real content of today.

**The de Branges norm is exponentially stronger than the Weil energy.** The de Branges weight grows like
$e^{+\pi|x|/2}$; the Weil archimedean energy is $\Omega(r)\sim\log|r|$. So
$$
\|g\|^2_{H(E)}\asymp\!\int|\widehat g|^2 e^{\pi|r|/2}\,dr\ \ggg\ \mathfrak a(g)=\!\int|\widehat g|^2\,\Omega(r)\,dr .
$$
These are **not** the same Hilbert space. Two consequences, one good, one subtle:

- **(good) Semiboundedness is *norm-dependent*, and the strong norm makes it *easier*.** Positivity
  $\mathfrak t\ge0$ (= RH) is norm-independent, but semiboundedness $\mathfrak t\ge-C\|g\|^2$ is **not**:
  a larger $\|g\|^2$ on the right is a *weaker* demand. So passing from $L^2$ to $H(E)$ relaxes B-2 — the
  strong norm "absorbs" the off-axis blow-up. ✅ This is exactly why the move is the right one.

- **(subtle) The ladder must be measured in the *same* norm, or it converges to the wrong thing.** The
  faithful-ladder claim is $\lambda_{\min}(Q_J)\to\inf\operatorname{spec}(\mathcal T)$; but $\lambda_{\min}$
  is a *Rayleigh quotient*, $\inf \mathfrak t(g,g)/\|g\|^2$, and its value **depends on which $\|\cdot\|$**.
  Choosing $H(E)$ fixes B-2's well-posedness **but redefines the eigenvalue problem** the numerics must
  solve. So the choice of $E$ is now a *shared* parameter of B (semiboundedness) **and** C (what the
  pollution-free limit even is). ⟶

**Why a single $E$ is not enough — the chain.** We need one space that simultaneously (a) bounds strip
evaluations (wants a *strong*, $H(E)$-type norm) and (b) keeps the form comparable to the Weil energy so
the bottom eigenvalue is the *Weil* bottom (wants a *weak*, $\Omega$-type norm). These pull oppositely;
no single fixed norm does both. The principled reconciliation of a one-parameter family of de Branges
norms interpolating between "strong enough to bound evaluations" and "weak enough to be the Weil form"
is precisely de Branges' **chain** $\{H(E_a)\}_{a}$ (a canonical system / Krein string), with the
**ordering (chain) condition** governing how the bottom of the form behaves along the family. ⟶
