# E72.69 -- Resolvent algebra certificate for half-power Abel divisibility

**Date:** 2026-07-08.
**Role:** turn the half-power Abel concomitant into a finite rational/exponential certificate.

## 0. Object

E72.68 defines:

```text
F_x(s;tau)=<v_{x,s},A_x(tau)k_x>,
```

where:

```text
A_x(tau)=Q_x(0)+(1/2+i tau)int_0^L exp(i tau y)Q_x(y)dy.
```

The spectral divisibility target is:

```text
F_x(s;tau)=P_x(tau)B_x(s;tau)+E_x(s;tau).          (HPAC-DIV)
```

## 1. Resolvent algebra of the entries

Let:

```text
d_n=2*pi*n/L,
E_tau=exp(i tau L).
```

The finite integrals in E72.68 show that every entry of `A_x(tau)` lies in the algebra generated by:

```text
E_tau,
(tau I-D_x)^(-1),
(tau I+D_x)^(-1),
(tau I-D_x)^(-2),
(tau I+D_x)^(-2),
D_x,
J.
```

The squared resolvents occur only from the affine diagonal factor `(1-y/L)`.

Thus:

```text
A_x(tau)
 = A_x^{(0)}(tau)+E_tau A_x^{(1)}(tau),           (RA)
```

where `A_x^{(0)}` and `A_x^{(1)}` are rational matrix functions with poles only at the mesh points
`tau=+-d_n`, of order at most two.

## 2. Clearing mesh denominators

Define:

```text
Delta_x(tau):=prod_n (tau^2-d_n^2)^2.
```

Then:

```text
G_x(s;tau):=Delta_x(tau)F_x(s;tau)
```

has the finite exponential-polynomial form:

```text
G_x(s;tau)=G_x^{(0)}(s;tau)+E_tau G_x^{(1)}(s;tau),                (EP)
```

where `G_x^{(0)}` and `G_x^{(1)}` are ordinary polynomials in `tau`, with coefficients explicitly
computed from:

```text
D_x, v_{x,s}, k_x, L.
```

Because finite Weyl roots lie away from the mesh poles, `Delta_x(tau_j) != 0` whenever
`P_x(tau_j)=0`.

Therefore:

```text
F_x(s;tau_j)=0
```

is equivalent to:

```text
G_x^{(0)}(s;tau_j)+exp(i tau_jL)G_x^{(1)}(s;tau_j)=0.              (CERT-root)
```

## 3. Finite certificate

For a compact height window `K`, let:

```text
Z_x(K):={tau_j in K : P_x(tau_j)=0}.
```

Define the finite residual:

```text
R_x^{HPAC}(s;K)
 := max_{tau_j in Z_x(K)}
    |G_x^{(0)}(s;tau_j)+exp(i tau_jL)G_x^{(1)}(s;tau_j)|
      / |Delta_x(tau_j)|.                                         (CERT)
```

Then:

```text
R_x^{HPAC}(s;K)=max_{tau_j in Z_x(K)} |F_x(s;tau_j)|.
```

The finite, root-level certificate for asymptotic divisibility is:

```text
R_x^{HPAC}(s;K)->0                                                 (CERT0)
```

on the two Cauchy heights, plus the one-derivative analogue and a local normal quotient bound for
`F_x/P_x` away from the mesh.

## 4. Why this is the desired finite identity

This certificate uses only finite CCM data:

```text
D_x,
xi_x through P_x,
k_x,
C_x^(-1)Q_x(sI-D_x)^(-1)1,
and the closed matrix A_x(tau).
```

It does not evaluate zeta zeros. It does not use Weil positivity. It does not estimate a generic
Chebyshev discrepancy. It asks for a finite determinant-ideal cancellation of a specific
Feshbach-Abel concomitant.

## 5. Reduction theorem

### Theorem 72.69

Assume `(CERT0)`, its one-`s`-derivative analogue, and the normal quotient bound for `F_x/P_x` on
compact height windows. Assume also finite spectral convergence:

```text
P_x/P_x(0)->Xi/Xi(0).
```

Then scalar WRL resonance annihilation follows.

### Proof

`(CERT0)` plus the quotient bound is exactly asymptotic divisibility `(HPAC-DIV)` on compact windows:
the quotient has no asymptotic residue at finite zeros and stays normal away from the mesh. E72.68 then
applies, giving `F_x(s;tau_rho)->0` at every `Xi` zero height `tau_rho`. By the half-power Abel
identification, this is the scalar WRL resonance annihilation for the finite cell; with the
model-matching condition of E72.31, it gives the full scalar WRL resonance statement. `QED`
