# E72.63 -- Low-high Cauchy-Hilbert closure theorem

**Date:** 2026-07-08.
**Role:** isolate the two finite estimates that would prove ECHC and hence scalar WRL annihilation.

## 0. Starting point

E72.62 reduced LFDC to:

```text
<v,K_x^E(z)k>
 = sum_n alpha_n sigma_n(z)
   + sum_n beta_n partial_{d_n}sigma_n(z),                         (ECF)
```

where:

```text
alpha_n=sum_{m != n}[conj(v_n)k_m+conj(v_m)k_n]/(d_n-d_m),
beta_n=conj(v_n)k_n,
```

and:

```text
sigma_n(z)
 = int_0^L exp(-(z-1/2)r)sin(d_nr)dE_x^leftarrow(r).
```

The LFDC scalar is:

```text
D_x(s;z)=(2/(L sqrt(x)))<v,K_x^E(z)k>.                            (LFDC-K)
```

## 1. Frequency split

Let `T=T(x)` with:

```text
1 << T << N_x/L.
```

Define:

```text
I_low(T)  = {n: |d_n| <= T},
I_high(T) = {n: |d_n| > T}.
```

Split:

```text
<v,K_x^E k> = B_low(T)+B_high(T),
```

where `B_low` and `B_high` are the corresponding restrictions of `(ECF)`.

## 2. High-frequency sufficient estimate

### HF -- endpoint exponential high-frequency estimate

For some `T(x)->infty`,

```text
sup_{|d_n|>T, z}
  ( |sigma_n(z)| + |partial_{d_n}sigma_n(z)| )
  <= o(L sqrt(x))/A_x,                                             (HF)
```

where:

```text
A_x
 := sup_s sum_{|d_n|>T} ( |alpha_n(v,k)|+|beta_n(v,k)| ).
```

This is deliberately stated with the actual coefficient mass. It avoids the old incoherent ceiling:
one does not need a uniform bound for every possible test vector, only for the Cauchy-Hilbert mass
generated by the Feshbach vector.

## 3. Low-frequency sufficient estimate

### LF -- finite low-current cancellation

For the same `T(x)`:

```text
sup_{s,z}|B_low(T)| = o(L sqrt(x)).                                (LF)
```

This is the place where PNT alone is weakest: for bounded or slowly growing `|d_n|`, the endpoint
transforms can carry the full Chebyshev discrepancy. Therefore `(LF)` must come from the special
Feshbach coefficients, not from estimating `sigma_n` separately.

## 4. Closure theorem

### Theorem 72.63

Assume `(HF)` and `(LF)` on the two Cauchy heights, uniformly for `z` in compact subsets of
`Re z>1/2`, and assume the same two estimates after replacing `v_{x,s}` by `partial_s v_{x,s}`. Then:

```text
LFDC holds with one s-derivative.
```

Consequently, by E72.30, E72.31, and E72.61--E72.62, scalar WRL resonance annihilation holds for the
unique Weyl-Feshbach scalar kernel. Therefore the Schur-free current convergence criterion closes.

### Proof

By `(ECF)`:

```text
<v,K_x^E k> = B_low(T)+B_high(T).
```

The low term is `o(L sqrt(x))` by `(LF)`. For the high term:

```text
|B_high(T)|
 <= A_x sup_{|d_n|>T,z}(|sigma_n(z)|+|partial_{d_n}sigma_n(z)|)
 = o(L sqrt(x))
```

by `(HF)`. Thus:

```text
<v,K_x^E k>=o(L sqrt(x)).
```

Inserting this in `(LFDC-K)` gives:

```text
sup_{s,z}|D_x(s;z)| -> 0.
```

The derivative statement is identical with `v` replaced by `partial_s v`. E72.30 turns LFDC plus this
derivative control into Schur-free current convergence; E72.31 identifies this with scalar WRL
resonance annihilation. `QED`

## 5. What remains to prove

The theorem has deliberately separated the two sources of difficulty:

```text
HF: oscillatory endpoint prime sums above a moving frequency T(x);
LF: Feshbach cancellation of the finitely many low endpoint currents.
```

This is sharper than the previous single half-power target. A proof may use different tools for the two
regions:

```text
high: endpoint Mellin oscillation of n^(it) with t>T(x);
low:  C_xv=Q_x(sI-D)^(-1)1 and H_x^0k=mu_x^0k.
```

The low part is the genuinely new finite CCM identity. The high part is a prime exponential estimate
against a restricted coefficient mass, not a statement about arbitrary test functions.

```text
open:   prove HF and LF from the endpoint Mellin sums and the Feshbach/prolate equations.
```
