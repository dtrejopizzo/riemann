# E67.3 — Choosing the non-group-like CQG for the prime current

**Date:** 2026-07-06.
**Role:** candidate map after the E67.2 group-like ansatz was rejected as Bost-Connes/Pontryagin-dual
in disguise.

## Decision criterion

The prime-side quantum group must satisfy three constraints at once:

1. **Euler multiplicativity.** Prime powers and products must be represented functorially, so that a
   logarithmic derivative produces `Lambda(p^k)=log p`.
2. **Noncommutative corepresentations.** Prime generators cannot be group-like characters. They must be
   matrix coefficients of irreducible corepresentations with quantum dimension `d_q>1`.
3. **Haar contractivity.** The Schur bound must come from Peter-Weyl/Haar/q-dimension weights, not from
   the classical coefficients `p^{-k/2}` alone.

The core tension is:

```text
group-like generators give Euler for free but kill Haar contractivity;
generic noncommutative CQGs give Haar weights but do not know Euler.
```

So the live object has to put Euler in the **fusion/tensor category**, not in group-like elements.

## Candidate families

| Family | Euler multiplicativity | Noncommutative Haar mechanism | Verdict |
|---|---|---|---|
| Group algebra `C[Q_{>0}^x]` / Bost-Connes skeleton | Exact | No; all prime generators are group-like | **NO-GO** |
| Compact abelian torus of prime characters | Exact | No; commutative Peter-Weyl characters only | **NO-GO** |
| Dual of a discrete group `C^*(Gamma)` | Good if `Gamma` is generated by primes | Mostly group-like/cocommutative | **NO-GO unless extra matrix coreps enter** |
| Classical compact groups `C(G)` | Nontrivial reps, but commutative function algebra | Haar exists, but no quantum q-dimension mechanism | Weak; likely not enough |
| `SU_q(2)` / rank-one compact q-group | Strong q-dimension/Peter-Weyl | No natural independent prime labels | Weak alone; useful as local fiber |
| `O_N^+` / `A_o(F)` | Strong noncommutative fusion, SU(2)-type rules | Good q-dimension weights | Live as prime-local fiber |
| `U_N^+` / `A_u(F)` | Strong noncommutative fusion, word category `N * N` | Good Haar/Peter-Weyl; free unitary corep | **Best current compact candidate** |
| Free product over primes of a fixed CQG | Prime colors preserved by construction | Haar free product gives noncommutative orthogonality | **Live, but K2-repro may over-orthogonalize** |
| Drinfeld/bicrossed/ax+b quantum groups | Closest to arithmetic semigroup dynamics | Often locally compact/KMS-adjacent, not compact | Interesting but high BC/MW-5 risk |

## Candidate baseline: prime-colored free unitary CQG

For a finite cutoff `lambda`, define

```text
G_lambda := *_{p <= e^lambda} U_N^+(F_p)
```

as a free product of compact quantum groups. Each prime has a fundamental non-trivial unitary
corepresentation

```text
u^(p) = (u^(p)_{ab})_{a,b=1}^N,
Delta(u^(p)_{ab}) = sum_c u^(p)_{ac} tensor u^(p)_{cb},
d_q(u^(p)) > 1.
```

The prime power `p^k` is not `U_p^k` for a group-like `U_p`. It is the tensor word

```text
(u^(p))^{tensor k}
```

and its logarithmic Euler contribution is the normalized cyclic character

```text
X_{p,k}
  := d_q((u^(p))^{tensor k})^{-1/2}
     Tr_q,cyc((u^(p))^{tensor k}).
```

The categorical Euler current is

```text
J_C(s) = sum_{p,k>=1} (log p) p^{-ks} X_{p,k}.
```

This has the right von Mangoldt coefficient source while keeping `X_{p,k}` inside a noncommutative
Peter-Weyl block.

## Candidate prime operator

Couple the above to the Gamma_q archimedean module by Mellin shifts:

```text
B_lambda(z0,q)
  = sum_{p^k <= e^lambda}
      (log p) p^{-k/2}
      X_{p,k} tensor W_{k log p} tensor arch_q(k log p; z0).
```

The group-like baseline is recovered by replacing every `X_{p,k}` by `1`.

Therefore every finite-prime experiment must report two numbers:

```text
baseline(lambda,N) = norm of the group-like/counit current;
cqg(lambda,N)      = norm of the non-trivial Haar-normalized current.
```

If `cqg(lambda,N) == baseline(lambda,N)` up to numerical noise, the CQG did not add a mechanism.

## Why `U_N^+` is currently better than `O_N^+`

`O_N^+` has strong q-dimension weights, but its fusion rules are rank-one/SU(2)-type. That is clean for
prime powers but less natural for oriented Euler factors and conjugates.

`U_N^+` has a word-like fusion category generated by a fundamental corepresentation and its conjugate.
That gives more room for:

- cyclic logarithms;
- oriented Mellin shifts;
- prime-color free products;
- a DH falsifier based on missing Euler/fusion compatibility.

This is not a theorem that `U_N^+` works. It is the most economical compact noncommutative testbed that
does not collapse immediately to group-like characters.

**E67.4 update.** Strict free-product Haar orthogonality over-orthogonalizes the prime-power blocks and
destroys the phase interference required by the exact P52 prime Pick operator. Therefore this candidate
is no longer the preferred final CQG. It remains the correct baseline/falsifier for `K2-mech`.

## K2-mech test specification

For a finite cutoff:

1. Build the group-like baseline current `B_gl`.
2. Build the CQG current `B_cqg` using Haar-normalized `X_{p,k}`.
3. Compare the induced Pick jet:

```text
P_hat_cqg(q)_{ij}
  = < B_cqg v_j^(q)(z0), B_cqg v_i^(q)(z0) >.
```

4. Separately compare the Schur norms:

```text
|| A_N^{-1/2} P_hat_gl  A_N^{-1/2} ||,
|| A_N^{-1/2} P_hat_cqg A_N^{-1/2} ||.
```

The first comparison is `K2-repro`; the second is `K2-mech`.

**Pass condition.**

`P_hat_cqg -> P_lambda` in the classical limit, and the contractivity estimate uses the explicit
Haar/q-dimension normalization of `X_{p,k}`.

**No-go condition.**

The only way to match `P_lambda` is to collapse `X_{p,k}` to the counit/character `1`, or the
Haar-normalized result gives the same norm as the group-like baseline.

## DH falsifier sharpened

Davenport-Heilbronn may mimic the archimedean side, but it has no honest Euler product. In this
framework that should appear as:

```text
no prime-color tensor factorization
or
no cyclic Euler logarithm with Lambda(p^k)=log p
or
no compatible Haar-normalized X_{p,k} whose Mellin shifts reproduce its prime jet.
```

If DH can be assigned the same `X_{p,k}` and passes `K2-mech`, the CQG family is too weak.

## Current verdict

The group-like ansatz is dead. The strict free-product `U_N^+` ansatz is demoted to baseline after
E67.4. It repairs noncommutativity but loses interference.

The next live object is the modular/Woronowicz-character route:

```text
Euler/Mellin evaluation from Woronowicz characters f_z;
contractivity from Haar/Peter-Weyl/q-dimension using the same F matrices.
```

This is now the exact bottleneck of Phase 67.
