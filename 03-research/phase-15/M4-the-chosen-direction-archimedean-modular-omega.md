# Phase 15 · M4 — The chosen direction: the archimedean–modular construction of $\omega$

**Author: David Alejandro Trejo Pizzo · 2026-06-06.**
After M1–M3 and the eight attempts, four advisory groups proposed routes to supply the missing Kähler/Lefschetz
class $\omega$. This document selects the direction, states the **decisive criterion** that orders all four
proposals, classifies them against it, and lays out the concrete construction to execute. The criterion is not a
matter of taste; it is forced by the one theorem we already proved at the terminus — Attempt 8.

## 0. The decisive criterion (forced by Attempt 8)

Attempt 8 proved a determinacy statement, not a wall:

> **(Determinacy.)** The only $(1,1)$-form compatible with the spectral triple $(\mathcal T, J, Q)$ in the standard
> way is $\omega = Q(J\cdot,\cdot)$, and its positivity **is** RH (the second Riemann relation). Equivalently: any
> raising operator built as a function of the Frobenius, $L=f(\mathcal T)$, commutes with $\mathcal T$ but lies in
> the von Neumann algebra $W^*(\mathcal T)$ it generates; the form $\omega_f=Q(J f(\mathcal T)\cdot,\cdot)$ is then a
> function of the spectral data, and asserting $\omega_f\succ 0$ **restates** RH rather than forcing it.

This hands us a precise, checkable filter on any candidate $\omega$ (equivalently any raising operator $L$):

$$
\boxed{\textbf{Independence filter: } L \text{ is admissible} \iff L \notin W^*(\mathcal T).}
$$

- If $L \in W^*(\mathcal T)$ (i.e. $L=f(\mathcal T)$): the Gram form $[L,\Lambda]$ is a function of $\mathcal T$; its
  positivity on primitives is *equal to* the Weil/Hodge-index criterion we already have. The computation returns the
  criterion; it does not cross it. (This is M3, restated.)
- If $L$ lies in the **relative commutant** — it commutes with $\mathcal T$ (so the Frobenius still acts on the
  cohomology) but is **outside** $W^*(\mathcal T)$ — then $L$ is a genuinely new input. Its positivity is an
  independent fact that can *force* $Q\succeq 0$.

Every route must produce an $L$ in the relative commutant **and outside** $W^*(\mathcal T)$. This is the single bit
that separates a construction of $\omega$ from a re-encoding of RH.

1. It removes **A1 as a proof** and keeps it as a **diagnostic**. A1's "Frente 3 first, it only needs your data and

2. It shows the other three **converge** on the same independent ingredient from three sides: the **archimedean
   place** $W(r)=\operatorname{Re}\psi(\tfrac14+\tfrac{ir}{2})-\log\pi$ (A2's curvature, A3-D's potential, A4-iv's

So the direction writes itself: **construct $\omega$ from the archimedean place, realize its independence from
$\mathcal T$ via the modular flow, and live in the dynamical (foliation) geometry A3-E points to** — discarding the

## 2. The chosen construction (M4): archimedean Kähler potential + modular independence

We build $\omega$ in three concrete pieces. Each piece is a computation or a named, classical mechanism — not a wish.

### Piece 1 — the archimedean form (the candidate Kähler metric) — **M4.1 EXECUTED**

The archimedean place contributes to the Weil quadratic form the term
$$
A_\infty(f,f) \;=\; \frac{1}{2\pi}\int_{-\infty}^{\infty} |\widehat f(r)|^2\, \Psi(r)\,dr,
\qquad
\Psi(r) \;=\; \operatorname{Re}\psi\bigl(\tfrac14+\tfrac{ir}{2}\bigr)-\log\pi,
$$
the smooth density of the $\infty$-factor $\Gamma_{\mathbb R}(s)=\pi^{-s/2}\Gamma(s/2)$ in the explicit formula.
**The original guess (that a constant shift $c_0$ makes the density $\ge0$ everywhere) is FALSE, and the computation
M4.1 shows it cleanly — but it also shows the true, usable positivity.**

\begin{proposition}[M4.1 — structure of the archimedean density, proven + verified to 40 digits]
$\Psi$ satisfies:
\begin{itemize}
\item[\rm(i)] \emph{even}, $\Psi(-r)=\Psi(r)$;
\item[\rm(ii)] \emph{strictly increasing on $r>0$}: $\Psi'(r)=-\tfrac12\operatorname{Im}\psi'(\tfrac14+\tfrac{ir}{2})
  =\sum_{n\ge0}\frac{(\tfrac14+n)\,r}{\bigl((\tfrac14+n)^2+(r/2)^2\bigr)^2}>0$
  (every term positive — a one-line proof, no estimate);
\item[\rm(iii)] \emph{unique sign change} at $r_0=6.28983598883690\ldots$, with global minimum
  $\Psi(0)=\psi(\tfrac14)-\log\pi=-\gamma-3\log2-\tfrac{\pi}{2}-\log\pi=-5.37218\ldots$;
\item[\rm(iv)] $\Psi(r)\to+\infty$, $\Psi(r)=\log\frac{|r|}{2\pi}+o(1)$.
\end{itemize}
\end{proposition}

**Consequence (the corrected, true Piece 1).** $\Psi$ is **indefinite** on $\mathbb R$: negative exactly on the
bounded band $|r|<r_0$, positive on $|r|>r_0$. So $A_\infty$ is **not** a globally positive metric — and no
formula-admissible constant fixes it. **But the negativity sits entirely in the spectral gap:**
$$
r_0 = 6.2899 \;<\; \gamma_1 = 14.1347\ldots \quad\Longrightarrow\quad
(-r_0,r_0)\subset(-\gamma_1,\gamma_1)=\text{the zero-free gap of }\zeta,
$$
and on the **support of the actual zeros** ($|r|\ge\gamma_1$) one has $\Psi\ge\Psi(\gamma_1)=0.8105\ldots>0$.
Moreover $\Psi$ is, to leading order, **the smooth zero-counting density itself**: $\tfrac{1}{2\pi}\Psi(r)=
\tfrac1{2\pi}\log\frac{r}{2\pi}+o(1)=N'(r)$ (verified to $8$ digits at $r=10^3$). The archimedean term is the
**density of states of $\mathcal T$.**

> **What M4.1 establishes.** $A_\infty$ is a **strictly positive Kähler metric on the spectral part** of
> $\mathcal H_W$ — the closed span of test functions with $\widehat f$ supported on $|r|\ge\gamma_1$ (where the
> Frobenius $\mathcal T$ actually has spectrum). Its only indefiniteness is a single finite band $|r|<r_0$ lying in
> the zero-free gap, i.e. on the **trivial/ample part** (M2 territory: poles at $r=\pm i/2$ and the spectral gap).
> So the archimedean place **does** supply the positive metric the program needed — on the bulk — and the residual
> negative band is exactly the finite-rank piece M2 already controls. *(Data: `experiments/m4_archimedean_density.csv`;
> code: `m4_archimedean_density.py`, `m4_density_structure.py`.)*

This **sharpens, not weakens, the direction**: the curvature is positive precisely where the Frobenius lives, and
the obstruction is confined to a finite gap band. The next sub-task (M4.1b) is to show the band $|r|<r_0$ is
absorbed by the M2 ample cone, giving a genuinely positive $A_\infty$ on all of $\mathcal H_W^{\mathrm{prim}}$.

### Piece 2 — the $(1,1)$-form and the modular flow that makes it independent of $\mathcal T$

Let $J$ be the spectral complex structure (Attempt 7, Def. 4.2: the $\pm$ Hilbert-transform splitting aligned with
$\mathcal T$). Define the candidate Kähler form by **pairing $J$ against the archimedean metric, not against $Q$:**
$$
\omega_\infty(x,y) \;:=\; A_\infty(Jx,\,y).
$$
Contrast with Attempt 8's determined form $\omega=Q(J\cdot,\cdot)$: there the metric was $Q$ (the spectral pairing,
inside $W^*(\mathcal T)$), so $\omega$ was forced and circular.

**Where independence genuinely lives (corrected by M4.1b).** A naive reading says $A_\infty$ is the multiplication
operator $\Psi(\mathcal T)$ — a *function of $\mathcal T$*, hence in $W^*(\mathcal T)$, hence circular. M4.1b shows the
precise escape: $A_\infty$ is $\Psi$-multiplication on the **continuum** $L^2(\mathbb R, dr)$, whose multiplication
operator $\mathcal T_{\mathrm{cont}}$ (spectrum $=\mathbb R$) is **not** $\mathcal T$ (spectrum $=\{\gamma_\rho\}$,
discrete). $W^*(\mathcal T)=L^\infty(\{\gamma_\rho\})$ sees the zeros *only*; $A_\infty$ depends on $\widehat f(r)$
**between** the zeros, information $\mathcal H_W$ does not retain. The genuinely $\mathcal T$-independent object is
therefore **the sampling bridge** — the explicit formula equating the continuous archimedean measure $\Psi(r)\,dr$
with the discrete zero measure $\sum_\rho\delta_{\gamma_\rho}$ minus primes. *That bridge, not the operator $\Psi$,
is the input outside $W^*(\mathcal T)$.*

The mechanism that *guarantees* $L_\infty\notin W^*(\mathcal T)$ and supplies the integer grading:

> **(Modular grading, A4-i.)** Model $\mathcal H_W$ as the standard representation of the type-III factor
> $\mathcal M$ of the adele class space (Connes). The cyclic separating vector (the Weil/$\zeta$ state) gives a
> modular operator $\Delta$; the modular group $\sigma_t=\Delta^{it}\cdot\Delta^{-it}$ is **canonical and not
> inner** — the textbook witness of an automorphism flow outside the algebra. Set $H:=\log\Delta$ (the modular
> Hamiltonian) as the **grading**, and verify that the archimedean $L_\infty$ is its $+2$ raising part:
> $[H,L_\infty]=2L_\infty$.

**Milestone M4.2 — EXECUTED (explicit $\mathfrak{sl}_2$ + the LI no-go).** The abstract modular sketch above is
realized by an **explicit, elementary operator**, and the execution also proves *why* it must live upstairs.

\begin{proposition}[M4.2a — no Tate-twist Lefschetz on the discrete zeros (LI / P15, operator form)]
On $\mathcal H_W$, $\mathcal T$ has simple spectrum $\{\gamma_\rho\}$, so its commutant is **abelian** (a MASA): any
$L$ that *commutes* with $\mathcal T$ is a function of $\mathcal T$ and cannot raise. The correct hard-Lefschetz
condition is therefore not commutation but the **Tate twist** $[\mathcal T,L]=cL$ (the analogue of
$\mathrm{Frob}\cdot L=q\,L\cdot\mathrm{Frob}$ on $C\times C$). But $[\mathcal T,L]=cL$ forces $L$ to map the
$\gamma$-eigenspace to the $(\gamma+c)$-eigenspace, i.e. the zero set must be **invariant under translation by $c$**.
By LI (P15) — equivalently, just by the variable spacing of the zeros — no $c\ne0$ does this (verified: the best
shift over $[0.5,60]$ sends only $2$ of $40$ zeros back onto zeros). **Hence no hard-Lefschetz raising operator
exists on $\mathcal H_W$ itself.** This is P15 in its sharpest operator-algebraic form.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proposition}[M4.2b — the full $\mathfrak{sl}_2$ exists explicitly on the continuum]
On $L^2(\mathbb R,dr)$ with $\mathcal T_{\mathrm{cont}}=$ mult-by-$r$ (spectrum $=\mathbb R$, which **is** shift
invariant), put
$$
L=T_c,\qquad H=\tfrac{2}{c}\,\mathcal T_{\mathrm{cont}},\qquad
\Lambda=T_{-c}\,m(\mathcal T_{\mathrm{cont}}),\quad m(r)=-\tfrac{r^2}{c^2}+\tfrac{r}{c},
$$
where $T_c$ is translation by $c$. Then $[\mathcal T_{\mathrm{cont}},L]=cL$ (Tate twist) and
$[H,L]=2L,\ [H,\Lambda]=-2\Lambda,\ [L,\Lambda]=H$ — a genuine $\mathfrak{sl}_2$ (verified to $10^{-12}$,
`m4_2_sl2_clean.py`). Crucially $L=T_c$ is **not** a multiplication operator, so $L\notin W^*(\mathcal
T_{\mathrm{cont}})$: the **independence filter is cleared by an explicit operator.** The grading $H=\frac2c\mathcal
T_{\mathrm{cont}}$ has **continuous** spectrum — the LI-compatible "Frobenius-as-a-flow" Lefschetz (Advisor 3-E),
not an integer grading.
\end{proposition}

**What M4.2 establishes.** The hard-Lefschetz $\mathfrak{sl}_2$ is no longer a wish — it is *built and verified* on
the archimedean continuum, with its raising operator provably outside $W^*(\mathcal T_{\mathrm{cont}})$ and
Tate-twist-compatible with the Frobenius flow. And the no-go (M4.2a) explains, rigorously, why it lives upstairs and
cannot be pushed onto the bare zero set. **The Lefschetz geometry exists; the only remaining question is whether it
survives the sampling bridge down to the LI zeros** — which is exactly M4.3.

### Piece 3 — the hard-Lefschetz compatibility (the reduced capstone)

With Pieces 1–2 in hand we now hold, **explicitly and verified**, on the archimedean continuum: a positive metric
$g_\infty=|\Psi|$ (M4.1), a grading sign $S=\operatorname{sgn}(\mathcal T_{\mathrm{cont}}^2-r_0^2)$ (M4.1b), and a
full hard-Lefschetz $\mathfrak{sl}_2$ $(L=T_c,\Lambda,H=\frac2c\mathcal T_{\mathrm{cont}})$ with $L\notin
W^*(\mathcal T_{\mathrm{cont}})$ (M4.2). The remaining node is now a single transport question:

> **(M4.3 — the reduced capstone.)** The continuum hard-Lefschetz $\mathfrak{sl}_2$ with metric $g_\infty$ satisfies
> the **Hodge–Riemann bilinear relations** $g_\infty(x,\Lambda^{k}x)>0$ on degree-$k$ primitives (a positivity that
> is natural upstairs, since the structure is translation-homogeneous). RH is the statement that this positivity
> **survives the sampling bridge** — i.e. restricting from the continuous measure $\Psi(r)\,dr$ to the discrete zero
> measure $\sum_\rho\delta_{\gamma_\rho}$ (the explicit formula) preserves the Hodge–Riemann signs, forcing
> $Q\succeq0$ on $\Pi^\perp$.

**Milestone M4.3 — EXECUTED (the transport is exact; the positivity is a near-cancellation residual).**
We computed the transport on real zeros with primitive test functions $\widehat f(r)=(r^2+\tfrac14)e^{-r^2/2\sigma^2}$
(first $300$ zeros, von Mangoldt to $2\cdot10^4$):

| $\sigma$ | $Q=\sum_\rho h(\gamma_\rho)$ | $A_\infty$ | $P$ (primes) | $A_\infty-P$ | $Q-(A_\infty-P)$ |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| $2$ | $1.6\times10^{-17}$ | $-6.2225$ | $-6.2225$ | $1.6\times10^{-17}$ | $-1.4\times10^{-20}$ |
| $3$ | $1.83\times10^{-5}$ | $-21.677$ | $-21.677$ | $1.83\times10^{-5}$ | $-2\times10^{-12}$ |
| $4$ | $0.3022$ | $-24.239$ | $-24.542$ | $0.3025$ | $-3\times10^{-4}$ |

\begin{proposition}[M4.3 — what transports, and what does not]
\emph{(a) The transport identity is exact:} on primitives the explicit formula gives $Q=A_\infty-P$ (verified to
$10^{-12}$–$10^{-20}$; residual is zero-/prime-truncation). The sampling bridge \emph{is} the explicit formula.
\emph{(b) The upstairs metric does not transport its sign:} on these primitives $A_\infty<0$ (strongly), so the
continuum positivity $g_\infty\succ0$ does \textbf{not} descend to $Q\ge0$. \emph{(c) $Q\ge0$ is a residual:}
$A_\infty$ and the prime term $P$ are large, negative, and \textbf{nearly equal}; $Q=A_\infty-P$ is their tiny
non-negative remainder. The positivity of the Weil form is the residue of an almost-total archimedean$-$prime
cancellation.
\end{proposition}

**Honest terminus of M4.** The archimedean–modular direction delivered three genuine, verified gains: (1) the
positive metric $g_\infty=|\Psi|$ on the spectral bulk (M4.1); (2) the first **explicit operator clearing the
independence filter** — the continuum $\mathfrak{sl}_2$ with $L=T_c\notin W^*(\mathcal T)$ (M4.2); (3) the exact
location of the obstruction. But M4.3 shows the capstone does **not** cross here: the discrete positivity $Q\ge0$ is
not the transported image of the upstairs Hodge–Riemann positivity — it is the near-cancellation residual
$A_\infty-P$, which is exactly the classical Weil/CAP difficulty (the prime term controls the sign, and upstairs the
metric is the *wrong sign* on the relevant primitives). The continuous Lefschetz grading $H=\frac2c\mathcal
T_{\mathrm{cont}}$ spreads the zeros across a continuum of weights instead of concentrating them in one weight, so
the function-field Hodge-index mechanism does not transfer. **M4 reaches the wall cleanly from the Lefschetz side,
names it precisely (the sampling residual = the prime term), and does not manufacture a crossing.**

**The one non-trivial continuation (M5).** M4.3 tested the *degree-$0$* metric $A_\infty(f,f)$. The full
hard-Lefschetz Hodge–Riemann form is the alternating sum across the $\mathfrak{sl}_2$-degrees,
$\sum_k(-1)^{k}\,g_\infty(P_k f,\Lambda^{k}P_k f)$, over the primitive components $P_k f$. The open question M5
isolates: is the discrete pairing $Q$ the image of *that* full HR form (not of $A_\infty$ alone) — in which case the
near-cancellation would be the degree-grading reorganizing the prime term — or does the full HR form also reduce to
the same residual? This is the only handle the construction leaves that is not already known to be CAP; it is
worth one explicit test before declaring the direction closed. *(Certificate: `m4_3_transport.py`.)*
