# B3.2 / M2 — The de Branges comparison: structure functions, the chain mechanism, and why it is the same template

**Route B, Milestone M2.** Author: David Alejandro Trejo Pizzo · 2026-06-03.
References: de Branges, *Hilbert Spaces of Entire Functions*; Lagarias (2006); Suzuki arXiv:2301.00421
(eqs. 1.3–1.4, Thm 1.1); Conrey–Li, *A note on some positivity conditions related to ζ and L-functions*
(2000).

**Question (B3.2).** Does the de Branges route supply a *zero-independent positivity certificate* that P8 and
Connes do not — a structural condition on explicit objects that forces Weil positivity?

**Answer, in one line.** No. De Branges gives the **same Kreĭn/positivity template** as P8 and Connes, via a
genuinely different *mechanism* (a totally ordered **chain** of subspaces / interlacing of structure
functions), but its positivity certificate (Hermite–Biehler of $E_\xi$ / positivity of the reproducing
kernel) is itself $\iff$ RH, and the specific *unconditional substitutes* proposed historically were
**refuted** (Conrey–Li). The one durable, possibly-new observation is structural, not a route to a proof:
**the de Branges chain ordering corresponds to P8's explicit height/Nyquist sampling ladder** (§4), tying
the two pictures — but again with the positivity living exactly at RH.

---

## 1. The de Branges object (exact, zero-independent)

Following Lagarias and Suzuki (eq. 1.3), set the **Hermite–Biehler candidate**
$$
E_\xi(z):=\xi(\tfrac12-iz)+\xi'(\tfrac12-iz),
$$
where $\xi(s)=\tfrac12 s(s-1)\pi^{-s/2}\Gamma(s/2)\zeta(s)$ and $\,'$ is $d/ds$. Write $E^\sharp(z):=\overline{E(\bar z)}$
and the **structure functions**
$$
A(z):=\tfrac12\big(E(z)+E^\sharp(z)\big),\qquad B(z):=\tfrac{i}{2}\big(E(z)-E^\sharp(z)\big),\qquad E=A-iB,
$$
with $A,B$ **real entire and zero-independent** (explicit in $\xi,\xi'$). The associated **de Branges space**
$\mathcal H(E)$ has the reproducing kernel
$$
K(w,z)=\frac{B(z)\overline{A(w)}-A(z)\overline{B(w)}}{\pi(z-\bar w)}
=\frac{E^\sharp(z)\overline{E^\sharp(w)}-E(z)\overline{E(w)}}{2\pi i\,(z-\bar w)} .
$$

**The de Branges positivity certificate.** $\mathcal H(E)$ is a genuine Hilbert space (the kernel $K$ is
positive-definite) **iff $E$ is Hermite–Biehler**, i.e. $|E(z)|>|E(\bar z)|$ for $\Im z>0$, equivalently all
zeros of $E$ lie in the closed lower half-plane. For $E_\xi$, zeros of $E$ correspond to zeros of $\xi$, and
$$
\boxed{\ E_\xi\ \text{Hermite–Biehler}\ \iff\ \text{RH}\ }\qquad(\text{Lagarias }[8]\text{ in Suzuki}).
$$

So the de Branges certificate — kernel positivity / Hermite–Biehler — is **$\iff$ RH**: the *same Kreĭn
template* (an a-priori-indefinite object is positive exactly on RH), now phrased as positivity of a
reproducing kernel rather than $\|K\|\le1$. The structure functions $A,B$ are the zero-independent data; the
*condition on them* (interlacing of their real zeros, equivalent to HB) is RH itself.

---

## 2. The mechanism is genuinely different (chain ordering, not a trace-square)

What distinguishes de Branges from P8 (sampling) and Connes (scaling trace) is **how positivity is meant to
be forced**:
- **Connes:** a manifest square (Sonin trace) minus a residual; positivity = residual control
  ($\|K_{\mathrm{CC}}\|\le1$).
- **P8:** a manifest square ($\|P_+EF\|^2$) minus a residual; positivity = angular contraction
  ($\|K_{\mathrm{P8}}\|\le1$).
- **de Branges:** a **totally ordered chain** $\{\mathcal H(E_a)\}_{a}$ of de Branges subspaces (ordered by
  inclusion), with positivity propagated *along the chain* by an ordering/monotonicity condition on the
  structure functions. Positivity here is a **global structural** statement about a one-parameter family,
  not a single operator bound.

This is why de Branges is conceptually attractive (it is the most "structural" of the three) and also why it
has been the hardest to make unconditional: the chain condition is a strong global hypothesis.

---

## 3. The historical obstruction (why this front is high-risk)

De Branges proposed explicit **positivity conditions** (on the structure functions / on certain quadratic
forms) intended to imply RH unconditionally. **Conrey–Li (2000)** showed that the specific positivity
hypotheses are **not satisfied** in the relevant cases — they exhibited explicit numerical/functional
violations. So:
$$
\text{Known de Branges positivity certificates are either (a) }\iff\text{ RH (HB / kernel positivity), or (b) refuted (Conrey–Li).}
$$
There is, at present, **no zero-independent de Branges condition that is both unconditionally checkable and
sufficient for RH.** This confirms the advisor's caution: de Branges risks being philosophical rather than
operative. We record it accurately and do **not** invest beyond the structural identification.

---

## 4. The one durable observation: chain ordering ↔ P8 sampling ladder

There is a genuine, recordable structural correspondence (suggestive, not a proof):

- Suzuki (Thm 1.1) proves that, **under RH**, the Weil completion $\mathcal H_W$ is isomorphic to
  $\mathcal H(E_\xi)$ via $F\mapsto[\psi_F]$, $\psi_F=\mathsf F^{-1}(F)$, with
  $\|E_\xi F\|^2_{\mathcal H(E_\xi)}=\|F\|^2=\pi\langle\psi_F,\psi_F\rangle_W$.
- P8's space $H_+$ is the **unconditional Kreĭn version** of $\mathcal H_W$ (P8 §2): under RH the indefinite
  part vanishes and $H_+\cong\mathcal H_W\cong\mathcal H(E_\xi)$.
- The de Branges **chain** $\{\mathcal H(E_a)\}$ is a filtration by an increasing scale parameter $a$; P8's
  realization carries an **explicit height/Nyquist sampling ladder** (the cells of length $1/d$, the
  over-sampling $P=\rho/d\to\infty$). 

> **B3.2.1 (heuristic structural correspondence — an organizational insight, not a theorem).** Under RH, the
> de Branges chain filtration and the P8 sampling ladder are two filtrations of the *same* space
> $\mathcal H(E_\xi)\cong H_+$ — the former by de Branges sub-spaces $\mathcal H(E_a)$, the latter by
> height/bandwidth. Heuristically this places P8's explicit finite sampling structure alongside (a slice of)
> the de Branges chain. **Caveat:** the two filtrations are *not* constructed canonically the same way, and
> the identification rests on $H_+\cong\mathcal H_W\cong\mathcal H(E_\xi)$ which holds *only under RH*. We keep
> this purely as an **organizational insight for the Rosetta stone** — it is not a mathematical result, and we
> do not claim it survives, or helps, off RH.

This is the honest yield of M2: a **Rosetta-stone entry** (de Branges chain ↔ P8 ladder, under RH), not a
new certificate. It strengthens the unifying picture but does not move the sign.

## 5. Verdict and routing

| de Branges ingredient | status for route B |
|---|---|
| Structure functions $A,B$ of $E_\xi$ (zero-independent) | ✅ explicit; the natural zero-free data |
| Certificate (HB / kernel positivity) | ❌ $\iff$ RH — same template, no free lunch |
| Specific de Branges positivity hypotheses | ❌ refuted (Conrey–Li 2000) |
| Chain-ordering mechanism | ◆ genuinely different from Connes/P8; but a strong global hypothesis |
| Chain ↔ P8 sampling ladder (under RH) | ✅ recordable structural correspondence (Obs. B3.2.1) |

**Routing.** Consistent with the advisor: **de Branges is deprioritized** as an operative route (its
certificates are $\iff$ RH or refuted). It contributes one structural identification (Obs. B3.2.1) to the
Rosetta stone and nothing to the sign. **The live route remains B2 (the semi-local Connes residual)** —
see M3 (`B2-semilocal-residual.md`).

**Net for the panel.** *The de Branges picture yields the same Kreĭn positivity template via a chain-ordering
mechanism; its zero-independent certificate (Hermite–Biehler of $E_\xi$ / positivity of the reproducing
kernel) is $\iff$ RH, and the historically proposed unconditional substitutes were refuted (Conrey–Li).
Its one durable contribution is structural: under RH the de Branges chain and P8's sampling ladder filter the
same space, placing P8's explicit finite sampling inside the de Branges chain. No new attack on the sign
comes from this front.*
