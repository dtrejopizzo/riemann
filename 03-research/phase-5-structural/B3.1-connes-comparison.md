**Route B, Milestone M1.** Author: David Alejandro Trejo Pizzo · 2026-06-03.
Reference read: `papers-referencia/2006.13771v1.pdf` (Connes–Consani, *Weil positivity and trace formula,

**Question (advisor's sharpened M1 target).** *Is the Connes–Consani residual the indefinite part of the P8
operator written in another basis? I.e. is P8's wall the same as Connes' wall?*

**Answer, in one line.** Both criteria are instances of the **universal Kreĭn template $\|K\|\le1$**
(Prop.~B3.1.1), and both are $\iff$ RH (trivially). But the two operators $K_{\mathrm{P8}}$ and
$K_{\mathrm{CC}}$ are **genuinely different**: $K_{\mathrm{P8}}$ is *zero-dependent and vanishes under RH*;
$K_{\mathrm{CC}}$ is *zero-independent, fixed, and non-vanishing*, so **they admit no natural zero-independent
unitary identification** (Prop.~B3.1.3). Consequently **P8's no-go (Thm C) does NOT transport to the Connes
residual** — and that strategic fact is the durable output: the Connes residual remains a live, unobstructed
attack surface for route B (Cor.~B3.1.4). The matching *template* is not progress (it is universal Kreĭn
geometry); the *content* is the zero-independence, which only the Connes side has.

> **Careful wording (for the panel).** We do **not** claim P8 and Connes are "the same wall" (both are
> $\iff$ RH, which is vacuous), nor that the operators are inequivalent in every category. We claim the
> precise, defensible statement: *they share the same abstract Kreĭn positivity template, but no natural
> zero-independent unitary identifies the two operators; hence the P8 no-go does not rule out the Connes
> route.*

## 1. The universal template (why both walls have the same shape)

Let $\mathfrak t$ be a Hermitian form written as a difference of two non-negative forms,
$\mathfrak t = q_+ - q_-$, with $q_+$ chosen as the Hilbert metric (so the realization space is the
completion in $q_+$). Let $K:=$ the angular operator carrying the $q_-$-data to the $q_+$-data (so
$q_-(F)=\langle K x_+, Kx_+\rangle$ with $x_+$ the $q_+$-representative of $F$). Then, tautologically,

> **Proposition B3.1.1 (universal Kreĭn criterion).**
> $$
> \mathfrak t\succeq 0 \iff q_-\le q_+ \iff \|K\|\le1 .
> $$
> This holds for *any* decomposition $\mathfrak t=q_+-q_-$ into a metric-defining positive part and a
> residual. The shape "$\|K\|\le1$" is therefore **not specific** to any one construction; it is the generic
> form of "is an indefinite form positive."

**Both P8 and Connes–Consani are instances.** Hence the fact that both reduce RH to "$\|K\|\le1$" is *not* a
coincidence and *not*, by itself, a bridge between them — it is the template every such reformulation must
take. Any claim of a deeper identity must compare the *operators*, not the *shapes*.

## 2. The Connes–Consani decomposition (exact, from the paper)

Connes–Consani work at the **archimedean place**, on $L^2(\mathbb R)_{\mathrm{ev}}\cong L^2(\mathbb R_+^*,d^*\lambda)$,
with the **scaling action** $\vartheta_{\mathrm m}(\lambda)\xi(v)=\xi(\lambda^{-1}v)$. The archimedean Weil
distribution is realized as a trace of the quantized differential (their eq.~(7)):
$$
W_\infty(f)=-\tfrac12\,\mathrm{Tr}\big(\widehat f\,u^*\,du\big).
$$

**The manifest square (Sonin trace).** Let $\mathbf S$ be the orthogonal projection onto **Sonin's space** —
the orthogonal complement of the range of the phase-space cutoff projections $\mathcal P_1\vee\widehat{\mathcal P}_1$
at $\Lambda=1$ (their §4). Sonin's space is **zero-independent** (it is defined purely by the
band/time-limiting cutoff of the scaling action, via prolate spheroidal wave functions). Then
$\mathbf S=\mathbf S^*=\mathbf S^2$, so for any test $g$,
$$
\mathrm{Tr}\big(\vartheta(g)\,\mathbf S\,\vartheta(g)^*\big)
=\mathrm{Tr}\big((\vartheta(g)\mathbf S)(\vartheta(g)\mathbf S)^*\big)\ \ge\ 0
\qquad\text{— a manifest square } T T^*,\ T=\vartheta(g)\mathbf S.
$$

**The residual.** Their Theorem 3 (eq.~(13)) is the exact identity
$$
\boxed{\ \mathrm{Tr}\big(\vartheta(f)\,\mathbf S\big)=W_\infty(f)+\int f(\rho)\,\epsilon(\rho)\,d^*\rho\ },
\qquad \epsilon(\rho)=\sum_n\frac{\lambda(n)}{\sqrt{1-\lambda(n)^2}}\langle\zeta_n\,|\,\vartheta(\rho^{-1})\zeta_n\rangle,
$$
with $\zeta_n$ the prolate-function vectors (eq.~(14)). Polarizing ($f=g*g^*$),
$$
W_\infty(g*g^*)=\underbrace{\mathrm{Tr}(\vartheta(g)\mathbf S\vartheta(g)^*)}_{\text{manifest square }\ge0}
\ -\ \underbrace{E(g*g^*)}_{\text{residual},\ E(f):=\int f\,\epsilon\,d^*\rho}.
$$

**The residual operator and the wall.** After imposing the vanishing conditions $\widehat f(\pm i/2)=0$
(equivalently inserting $Q=-(\rho\partial_\rho)^2+\tfrac14$, their eqs.~(11)–(12) and footnote 1), the
residual functional $E\circ Q$ on $L^2(\sqrt I,d^*\rho)$ decomposes as (their §5, after eq.~(14))
$$
\boxed{\ E\circ Q \ \sim\ -2\,\epsilon'(1^+)\,(\mathrm{Id}-K_{\mathrm{CC}}),\qquad \epsilon'(1^+)\approx 22.9965,\ }
$$
with $K_{\mathrm{CC}}$ a **compact, Hilbert–Schmidt** operator (associated to a Hilbert–Schmidt kernel,
zero-independent, built from $\epsilon$/prolate data). The unconditional positive functional
$L=D+W_\infty$ (eq.~(9)) and the essential negativity of $D\circ Q\sim-2\epsilon'(1^+)(\mathrm{Id}-K)$ give:

> **The Connes–Consani wall.** Weil positivity (archimedean, then semi-local) $\iff \mathrm{Id}-K_{\mathrm{CC}}\succeq0$
> on a finite-codimension subspace $\iff \|K_{\mathrm{CC}}\|\le1$ after conditioning out its single
> eigenvalue $>1$ (their §6, Toeplitz/prolate strategy, Theorem 6.11).

**Crucial scope note.** For the **single archimedean place** with $\mathrm{supp}\,f\subset(1/2,2)$ (no
primes), CC *prove* $W_\infty\ge0$ — there is **no wall** there; the residual is *controlled* (Thm 6.11,
$W_\infty(g*g^*)\ge\mathrm{Tr}(\vartheta(g)\mathbf S\vartheta(g)^*)-c|\widehat g(0)|^2$, $13<c<17$). The wall
that equals RH is the **semi-local** extension (all finite primes included): there the analogous
$\mathrm{Id}-K\succeq0$ is **not** proven. So:
$$
\text{CC archimedean residual: SOLVED (a square minus a controlled remainder).}\qquad
\text{CC semi-local residual: } \iff \text{RH (open).}
$$

1. **Both positive parts are already manifest squares** ($A^*A$ / $TT^*$). So route B's question

## 4. The structural theorem (the durable M1 output)

> **Proposition B3.1.3 (no natural zero-independent unitary identification).**
> There is no RH-independent unitary $U$ with $U K_{\mathrm{P8}} U^{-1}=K_{\mathrm{CC}}$. Precisely:
> 1. **Within the P8 realization constructed in §§6–7** (the Kreĭn space $\ell^2(\rho)$ with $J$ the
>    off-line-pair swap), $K_{\mathrm{P8}}=0$ **iff** RH holds (under RH there are no off-line swap-pairs, so
>    $\mathcal K_-=\{0\}$ and $K_{\mathrm{P8}}=0$; if RH fails, $K_{\mathrm{P8}}\ne0$ and $\|K_{\mathrm{P8}}\|$
>    measures whether off-line zeros are dominated). Thus *this particular* $K_{\mathrm{P8}}$ is a direct
>    functional of the off-line zeros, vanishing exactly on RH. *(This is a statement about the P8
>    realization, not about every operator one might attach to RH.)*
> 2. $K_{\mathrm{CC}}$ is a *fixed, zero-independent, compact* operator (prolate/scaling data), present and
>    non-zero whether or not RH holds; RH $\iff \|K_{\mathrm{CC}}\|\le1$ with $K_{\mathrm{CC}}\ne0$.
> 3. Hence any intertwiner $U$ with $U K_{\mathrm{P8}} U^{-1}=K_{\mathrm{CC}}$ would have to map a
>    operator that *vanishes under RH* to one that does *not* — impossible unless $U$ itself depends on
>    whether/where the off-line zeros lie.
>
> **What this does and does not say.** It rules out the *naive* identification (a fixed, zero-independent
> unitary). It does **not** claim the two operators encode unrelated obstructions in every category, nor
> exclude some subtler (RH-dependent, or non-unitary, or categorical) relation. The defensible content is
> exactly: *no natural zero-independent unitary identifies $K_{\mathrm{P8}}$ with $K_{\mathrm{CC}}$.*

*Proof.* (1) is immediate from the Kreĭn decomposition of P8 §6–7: $J=I_{\mathrm{on}}\oplus$(swap-blocks),
$\mathcal K_-=\bigoplus_{\text{off pairs}}\mathbb C(e_\rho-e_{\sigma\rho})$, which is $\{0\}$ exactly when
there are no off-line pairs, i.e. under RH; then $K_{\mathrm{P8}}=P_-(P_+|_{R(E)})^{-1}=0$. (2) is the CC
construction recalled in §2 ($K_{\mathrm{CC}}$ is the fixed Hilbert–Schmidt operator of their §5–6,
independent of the zero locations). (3) If $UK_{\mathrm{P8}}U^{-1}=K_{\mathrm{CC}}$ with $U$ unitary and
RH-independent, then under RH the left side is $0$ while the right side is $\ne0$ — contradiction; so $U$
must depend on whether/where the off-line zeros are. $\qquad\square$

> **Corollary B3.1.4 (the no-go does NOT cross over — and that is the good news).** P8's Theorem C (the
> zero-side cannot attack the sign because the depths enter only through $\mathfrak t$) is a statement about
> $K_{\mathrm{P8}}$, which is *built from the zeros*. It does **not** apply to $K_{\mathrm{CC}}$, which is
> built without the zeros. Therefore the Connes residual is a genuinely different attack surface, **not
> obstructed by the P8 no-go**, and is the correct target of route B.

**Interpretation (the Rosetta stone).** The diagram below aligns the languages:
$$
\begin{array}{ccc}
\textbf{P8 (zero-side)} & & \textbf{Connes–Consani (archimedean)}\\[2pt]
\mathfrak t=\|P_+EF\|^2-\|P_-EF\|^2 & \xrightarrow{\ \text{both}\ \iff\text{RH}\ } & W_\infty=\mathrm{Tr}(\vartheta(g)\mathbf S\vartheta(g)^*)-E(g*g^*)\\[2pt]
\text{square }A_{\mathrm{P8}}=P_+E\ (\text{zero-dep.}) & & \text{square }A_{\mathrm{CC}}=\vartheta(g)\mathbf S\ (\text{zero-indep.})\\[2pt]
\|K_{\mathrm{P8}}\|\le1,\ K_{\mathrm{P8}}=0\text{ on RH} & \not\cong_{\text{constructive}} & \|K_{\mathrm{CC}}\|\le1,\ K_{\mathrm{CC}}\ne0\text{ fixed}
\end{array}
$$
Same Kreĭn template, two languages; the *content* lives entirely in the zero-independence that only the
right column has. P8 is the clean picture of *what* the positivity criterion is; CC is the only picture from
which one may *push* on it.

## 5. What this hands to B2 (the next milestone), and the one live sub-question

Theorem B3.1.3 + Corollary B3.1.4 redirect route B precisely:
- **Drop** any plan to transport P8's structure (or its no-go) to obstruct/solve CC: the operators are
  constructively inequivalent.
- **Focus B2** on the CC residual itself: *is $\mathrm{Id}-K_{\mathrm{CC}}\succeq0$ in the **semi-local**
  case (all primes)?* The archimedean case is solved (a square minus a controlled remainder); the prime
  places are the wall.
- **The one live $PW_d$/Slepian sub-question (B2.2).** CC's $K_{\mathrm{CC}}$ is built from **prolate
  spheroidal wave functions** (eigenfunctions of the phase-space cutoff at $\Lambda=1$). P8 lives on the
  **band-limited** class $PW_d$, and Slepian's prolate spheroidal functions are exactly the eigenfunctions
  of the band-/time-limiting operator. **Question:** does the explicit band-limit $d$ of $PW_d$ give a
  cleaner, $PW_d$-native handle on CC's prolate operator (a sharper $\mathrm{Id}-K_{\mathrm{CC}}$ estimate),
  or is the coincidence of "prolate" superficial (CC's prolates come from the *scaling* cutoff at $\Lambda=1$,
  not from a band-limit $d$)? Honest prior: superficial, but it is the single non-redundant technical bridge
  and worth a bounded check. **Deliverable of B2.2:** either a band-limited sharpening of the residual
  estimate, or a no-go explaining why the two "prolate" structures do not align.

**Net for the panel (single sentence, robust form).** *M1 shows that P8's angular operator and the
Connes–Consani residual operator share the same abstract Kreĭn positivity template but admit no natural
zero-independent unitary identification; therefore the P8 no-go does not rule out the Connes route, and the
semi-local Connes residual remains the only known structural attack surface.* Supporting facts: the positive
part is already a manifest square on both sides, so the open problem is precisely the non-negativity of the
semi-local Connes residual ($\mathrm{Id}-K_{\mathrm{CC}}\succeq0$ with primes included).
