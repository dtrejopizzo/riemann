$$
\log G_z=-\sum_p\sum_{k\ge1}\frac{\alpha^k+\beta^k}{k}p^{-ks}=-\sum_k\frac{\alpha^k+\beta^k}{k}P(ks),\quad
P(ks)=\sum_m\frac{\mu(m)}{m}\log\zeta(kms),

$$
\boxed{\ G_z(s)=\prod_{N\ge1}\zeta(Ns)^{c_N},\qquad c_N=-\frac1N\sum_{k\mid N}(\alpha^k+\beta^k)\,\mu(N/k)\ }

$7\times10^{-6}$ ($z=\tfrac32$), $8\times10^{-4}$ ($z=2$, slower as the $c_N$ grow). The match is exact;

every $p$), and a constant-Satake Euler product is **ζ-isobaric** (a product of $\zeta(Ns)$-powers). Therefore a
prime-blind weight can never manufacture a **prime-distinguishing** — hence zero-distinguishing — $L$-function.

> **This is the spectral twin of the explicit-formula ω-blindness** found earlier: $\omega(p^a)=1$ on every
> prime power, so $\Lambda\cdot z^\omega=z\Lambda$ and the explicit formula sees $\omega$ as a trivial factor.
> Both facts have one root: **$\omega$ counts primes but does not distinguish them, while the zeros are encoded
> in the prime-by-prime (Euler) structure.** The ω-statistics live on the prime-*count* axis; the zeros live on
> the prime-*identity* axis; they are orthogonal. This is why the ω-direction — exact and spectral though M14.2
> showed it to be — cannot reach the zeros.
