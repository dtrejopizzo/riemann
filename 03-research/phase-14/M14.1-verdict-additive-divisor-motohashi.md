## 1. The object (confirmed)

The off-diagonal of the fourth moment of $\zeta$ is the **additive divisor (shifted convolution) sum**
$$
D(x,h)=\sum_{n\le x}d(n)\,d(n+h),\qquad d(n)=2^{\omega(n)}\ \text{on squarefree }n.
$$
Its main term is $D(x,h)\sim\tfrac{6}{\pi^2}\sigma_{-1}(h)\,x\,(\log x)^2$ (a degree-2 polynomial in $\log x$
with arithmetic coefficient $\sigma_{-1}(h)=\sum_{d\mid h}d^{-1}$). *Verified* ($x=4\times10^6$):

| $h$ | $\sigma_{-1}(h)$ | $D/(x(\log x)^2)$ | $\tfrac{6}{\pi^2}\sigma_{-1}(h)$ |
|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| 1 | 1.000 | 0.709 | 0.608 |
| 2 | 1.500 | 1.006 | 0.912 |
| 3 | 1.333 | 0.886 | 0.811 |
| 6 | 2.000 | 1.255 | 1.216 |
| 12 | 2.333 | 1.406 | 1.419 |

The measured constant tracks $\tfrac{6}{\pi^2}\sigma_{-1}(h)$ (the residual is the lower-order $\log^1,\log^0$
terms of the degree-2 polynomial; the leading $(\log x)^2$ order and the $\sigma_{-1}(h)$ dependence — the
additive-divisor structure — are confirmed). This is the object Motohashi expands spectrally.

## 3. The honest outcome (and where it sits)

Applying the filter to the *consequence*: the fourth-moment bound that Motohashi's formula yields gives a
**zero-density theorem** for $\zeta$ (the classical route from moment bounds to $N(\sigma,T)$ estimates) — i.e.
**few off-line zeros, not none** (mechanism (b)). The quantitative wall is the same as Littlewood's
(M12.11): the moment is $(\log T)^4$-large, a single off-line zero contributes $O(1)$, so the available control
bounds the *density* of off-line zeros, not their *absence*.

> **So Motohashi is the right \emph{kind} of object — exact, touching the spectrum/zeros, passing the filter —
> and it produces the strongest current zero-density input, but not absence.** This is consistent with the whole
> program: the exact zeros-facing tools (Littlewood, Motohashi) give density; absence is the $O(1)$/RH-strength gap.
