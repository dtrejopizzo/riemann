## 1. The computation, from the spectral side

The input to the Motohashi/Kuznetsov machine for the weighted shifted convolution
$D_z(x,h)=\sum_{n\le x}z^{\omega(n)}d(n)\,d(n+h)$ is the multiplicative function $a(n)=z^{\omega(n)}d(n)$; its
Dirichlet series fixes the spectral weights. The **local (Euler) factor** is
$$
\sum_{a\ge0}z^{\omega(p^a)}d(p^a)\,x^a=1+\sum_{a\ge1}z(a+1)x^a=\frac{1+2(z-1)x+(1-z)x^2}{(1-x)^2},\qquad x=p^{-s},
$$
so
$$
\boxed{\ \sum_n\frac{a(n)}{n^s}=\zeta(s)^2\,G_z(s),\qquad G_z(s)=\prod_p\bigl[1+2(z-1)p^{-s}+(1-z)p^{-2s}\bigr]\ }
$$
with $G_z(s)\sim\zeta(s)^{2(z-1)}H_z(s)$ near $s=1$ (the local $p^{-s}$-coefficient of $G_z$ is $2(z-1)$), hence
$\sum a(n)/n^s\sim\zeta(s)^{2z}H_z(s)$, pole order $2z$.

*Verified:* the exponent of $\sum_{n\le N}z^{\omega(n)}d(n)/n$ tracks $2z$ ($1.09,1.85,2.48,3.01$ for
$z=0.5,1,1.5,2$ at $N=3\times10^6$; slow $(\log N)^{2z}$ drift). The extra-factor local roots
$1+2(z-1)x+(1-z)x^2=(1-\alpha x)(1-\beta x)$ are: $\alpha=\beta=0$ at $z=1$ (so $G_1=1$); a complex pair at
$z=\tfrac12$; and real $\alpha>0>\beta$ with $\alpha\beta=1-z$ for $z>1$ (e.g. $z=2$: $\alpha=0.414$, $\beta=-2.414$).

## 2. The binary answer: NON-TRIVIAL

> **The $z^\omega$ weight is a non-trivial spectral modification.** For $z\ne1$ a genuine extra Euler factor
> $G_z(s)\ne1$ appears ($G_z=1\iff z=1$), so the weight is **not spectrally invisible**. Moreover
> $G_z(s)\sim\zeta(s)^{2(z-1)}$ is a **power of $\zeta$**: for non-integer $z$ it has **branch points at the
> $\zeta$-zeros**, and the spectral weight in the Motohashi/Kuznetsov expansion therefore **carries the $\zeta$
> zeros**. The local factors $(1-\alpha p^{-s})(1-\beta p^{-s})$ are the Satake-like parameters the spectral
> side sees, $\alpha\beta=1-z\ne0$.

This is the first object in the entire program that (i) touches the zeros exactly (through Motohashi), (ii)
passes through a real spectral identity, (iii) **incorporates the new ω-structure non-trivially**, and (iv) was
not examined before. Per the advisor's criterion, this is the threshold the line had to clear, and it clears it.

## 3. The honest characterization of the non-triviality (and the next question)

The modification is non-trivial but of a **specific type**, and honesty requires naming it:
- $G_z\sim\zeta^{2(z-1)}$ is a **$\mathrm{GL}(1)$ / Eisenstein-type** factor (a power of $\zeta$), **not** a new
  cuspidal (Maass) structure. The $z^\omega$ weight **reweights** the Motohashi spectrum by a $\zeta$-power; at
  the cuspidal spectral points $s=\tfrac12+i\kappa_j$ it multiplies the Maass contribution by
  $G_z(\tfrac12+i\kappa_j)\sim\zeta(\tfrac12+i\kappa_j)^{2(z-1)}$.
- The $\zeta$-power carries the zeros, so the modification is genuinely zeros-facing; but it is a $\zeta$-power,
  i.e. **potentially self-referential** (the zeros entering via $\zeta$ itself rather than via an independent
  source).
