Along the one-mode-larger path:

(6,5)        6.54502e-13      3.40151e-8

critical/neighbor ratio reaches `1.5e-8` in the largest case.  The data are
consistent with spectral convergence in `N` coupled to arithmetic
selectivity in `L`, but six points do not determine an all-orders law.

## Arithmetic deformation

P76.005 studies

```text
H(t)=H_arch-t H_prime=(1-t)H_arch+t H_full.
```

For `lambda=6`, `N=4`, the critical ground-state overlap is approximately
`0.16` from `t=0` through `t=0.99`.  It falls only in a singular endpoint
layer:

```text
t             gap                 q(gamma_1)
0.99          2.3137e-2           1.5272e-1
1-1e-4        1.6480e-4           7.7678e-3
1-1e-8        1.1961e-8           4.9749e-5
1-1e-12       1.0571e-12          9.6485e-8
1-1e-15       9.6691e-16          1.1855e-9
1             2.9080e-15          1.6157e-10
```

The gap collapses with the deformation distance and the eigenline rotates
through the same boundary layer that creates the lock.

## Autopsy of the deformation route

The formal Feynman-Hellmann and projector derivative identities remain
exact, but their bounds contain inverse spectral gaps.  The measured gap
degenerates at precisely the scale where the desired cancellation appears.
Therefore integrating a perturbative derivative from `t=0` to `t=1` cannot
give a uniform `NORMALIZED-ARITH-LOCK` estimate without an additional
endpoint identity controlling the rotating low spectral cluster.

That additional estimate is the original arithmetic problem in another
form.  P76.006 is consequently closed as a standalone perturbative route.
The deformation remains useful only as a diagnostic or if a new exact
endpoint factorization is first found.

## Surviving direction

The next attack is the endpoint scalar resolvent/bordered determinant itself,
expanded before taking its residue.  It must expose a completed-zeta factor
or a finite signed remainder without estimating through the collapsing gap.
