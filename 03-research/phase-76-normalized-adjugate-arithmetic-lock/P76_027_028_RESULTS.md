# P76.027-P76.028 - Boundary-kernel norm audit

## Fixed support

At `lambda=6`, increasing `N=6,...,12` gives

```text
L1 error:             0.7794 -> 0.7630
weighted L1 error:    0.9682 -> 0.9033
odd L1 defect:        0.1919 -> 0.1251
```

The odd defect decreases, but strong convergence to the Riemann kernel is
slow and is not established by increasing the Fourier cutoff at fixed `L`.

## Increasing-support sample

Using `N/L=4.33` at `lambda=4` and `N/L=4.74` at `lambda=6` gives sampled
`L1` errors `0.7591` and `0.7568`.  Thus there is no visible strong-`L1`
convergence on this short path, although the odd defect decreases from
`0.103` to `0.089` and endpoint mass is negligible.

The `lambda=8,N=22` solve at 50 digits produced an interior norm explosion.
Because the Schur system is far beyond that working precision, this datum is
classified as numerically unresolved, not as a mathematical falsifier.

## Decision

`BF-WL1` remains a valid sufficient theorem but is too strong to retain as
the primary endpoint.  The exact Fourier representation instead suggests
the weaker pair:

```text
uniform exponentially weighted total variation;
Euler-safe convergence of exponential moments.
```

This pair is exactly what Montel/Vitali needs and does not require inverse
Fourier densities to converge strongly.
