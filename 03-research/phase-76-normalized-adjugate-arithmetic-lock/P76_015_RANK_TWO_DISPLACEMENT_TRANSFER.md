# P76.015 - Rank-two displacement form of the boundary transfer

## Displacement identity

On an inner cutoff mesh let

```text
D=diag(d_n),
s=(S_L(d_n))_n,
1=(1,...,1)^T,
A=H_{N-1}-mu_N I.
```

P76.011 gives

```text
H=2 diag(C_L)-(2/L)Loew(S_L).
```

Since

```text
[D,Loew(S_L)]=s 1^T-1 s^T,
```

the shifted inner matrix satisfies the exact rank-two displacement equation

```text
[D,A]=-(2/L)(s 1^T-1 s^T).                    (RT-1)
```

When `A` is invertible,

```text
[D,A^(-1)]
=(2/L)(u v^T-v u^T),                          (RT-2)

u=A^(-1)s,
v=A^(-1)1.
```

Thus the full inverse displacement is generated by only two vectors.

## Boundary columns

For a new boundary node `d_b` with `S_b=S_L(d_b)`, the coupling column
`b=(H_{n,b})_n` obeys

```text
(D-d_b I)b=-(2/L)(s-S_b 1),

b=-(2/L)R_b(s-S_b1),
R_b=(D-d_b I)^(-1).                            (RT-3)
```

Combining `(RT-2)` with

```text
A^(-1)R_b
=R_bA^(-1)+R_b[D,A^(-1)]R_b
```

gives an explicit generator formula for `x_b=A^(-1)b`:

```text
x_b=-(2/L)R_b(u-S_bv)
    -(4/L^2)R_b(u v^T-v u^T)R_b(s-S_b1).       (RT-4)
```

The boundary Cauchy transfer is therefore

```text
T_b(z)=1/(z-d_b)-r_z^T x_b,                   (RT-5)
```

with `x_b` given entirely by two inner generator solves and scalar Cauchy
pairings.

## Significance

`RT-4` removes the ambient inverse from `BOUNDARY-SCHUR-LOCK`.  The theorem
is reduced to signed estimates for:

```text
r_z^T R_b u,
r_z^T R_b v,
v^T R_b(s-S_b1),
u^T R_b(s-S_b1).
```

All four are scalar divided-difference/Cauchy transforms of the coupled
Gamma-prime symbols.  No eigenvector `xi_N` appears.

This does not yet prove their cancellation.  It identifies a finite
rank-two arithmetic package that is strictly smaller than the full
adjugate, Loewner contour remainder, or Phase-74 Cauchy-plane Schur transfer.

## Theorem target

For both boundary nodes and every resolved critical ordinate, prove directly
from `(RT-4)` that

```text
|T_b(gamma)|/||r_gamma||=O_M(L^-M)
```

uniformly in an admissible `N(L)` regime.  Absolute estimates of the four
terms are not admissible if they are individually large; the antisymmetric
combination `u v^T-v u^T` must remain coupled.
