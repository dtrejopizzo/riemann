# P76.007-P76.011 results - resolvent, prolate bridge, and Loewner residual

## Pre-residue resolvent autopsy

For `lambda=6`, `N=5`, define

```text
F_delta(z)
=delta r_z^T((mu+delta)I-H)^(-1)r_z/||r_z||^2.
```

The exact spectral expansion is

```text
F_delta(z)
=sum_j delta/(delta-(mu_j-mu_0))
 |r_z^T xi_j|^2/||r_z||^2.
```

The ground residue has critical/neighbor ratio `3.7e-10`.  Contamination by
the other modes dominates as soon as `|delta|/gap` exceeds the scale of the
ground observable.  Numerically:

```text
-delta/gap     |F_delta(gamma)/F_delta(gamma+0.125)|
1e-8           6.6e-9
1e-5           6.2e-6
1e-3           5.8e-4
1              1.4e-2
1e4            4.1e-1
```

Thus a generic scalar resolvent has no uniform critical divisor before the
residue.  Resolving the residue by a finite spectral displacement requires
an accuracy already comparable to `NORMALIZED-ARITH-LOCK`.  Together with
E70.3 and E72.26, this closes the unexpanded endpoint resolvent as an
independent forcing mechanism.

## Cutoff eigenline stability

At fixed `lambda=6`, the embedded overlaps of consecutive multiprecision
ground eigenlines are

```text
N -> N+1       overlap
3 -> 4         0.996289509
4 -> 5         0.997975958
5 -> 6         0.999032093
6 -> 7         0.999278346
7 -> 8         0.999480566
```

Meanwhile `q(gamma_1)` falls from `1.30e-7` at `N=3` to `8.27e-24` at
`N=8`.  This rejects the hypothesis that the lock is produced by an erratic
choice inside an unresolved low cluster.

## Prolate comparison

The zero-independent Connes model `k_lambda=E(h_lambda)` was constructed
directly from the Riemann summation formula.  Its overlap with the CCM ground
line grows from `0.901` to `0.966` for `N=3,...,8`, and its first four CCM
modes capture `99.3%` at `N=8`.

However, the load-bearing estimates fail:

```text
N      ||(H-Rayleigh(k))k||/gap
3      1.39e9
4      2.09e12
5      9.50e14
6      3.53e16
7      1.53e18
8      6.27e20
```

The weak critical channel also does not transfer at these cutoffs:

```text
N      q(xi)          q(k_lambda)      q(xi-k_lambda)
3      1.30e-7        8.12e-2          8.12e-2
6      3.86e-15       6.15e-3          6.15e-3
8      8.27e-24       3.66e-5          3.66e-5
```

Therefore high Hilbert-space overlap does not prove either Estimate 17.E or
the required Cauchy-weak convergence.  The prolate bridge remains a useful
model, not the source of the observed finite critical lock.

## Exact Loewner interpolation identity

P76.011 proves and independently verifies

```text
H_L=2 diag(C_L(d_n))-(2/L)Loew(S_L),
S_L(t)=W_L[sin(ty)],
C_L(t)=W_L[cos(ty)].
```

For `H_L xi=mu xi`, the entire removable function

```text
R_S(z)=S_L(z)c(z)-sum_n S_L(d_n)xi_n/(z-d_n)
```

satisfies the exact nodal equation

```text
R_S(d_n)=L(C_L(d_n)-mu/2)xi_n.
```

With `I_xi(d_n)=xi_n`, this gives the exact divisibility

```text
E_L(z)
=R_S(z)-L(C_L(z)-mu/2)I_xi(z)
=D_L(z)G_L(z),
```

or equivalently

```text
S_L(z)P_L(z)-P_{S,L}(z)
=L(C_L(z)-mu/2)D_L(z)I_xi(z)+D_L(z)^2G_L(z).
```

The matrix identity has relative defect `2.1e-59`; the nodal interpolation
identity has error `2.5e-60` in the independent 60-digit probe.

## Interpretation

This is genuine new algebraic compression, but not yet the critical
divisibility theorem.  `D_L` is the known pole-mesh polynomial, not a Xi
divisor.  At the first critical ordinate the scalar symbols are nonzero:

```text
S_L(gamma_1)  = -0.450180564495
C_L(gamma_1)  =  3.81325043675
S_L'(gamma_1) =  7.19574698432.
```

Consequently no isolated scalar factor kills `P_L(gamma)`.  The next
theorem-grade target is the signed interpolation remainder package

```text
LOEWNER-CRIT-REM:
P_{S,L}(gamma)
+L(C_L(gamma)-mu/2)D_L(gamma)I_xi(gamma)
+D_L(gamma)^2G_L(gamma)
=O_M(L^-M)D_L(gamma),
```

derived from a contour or exponential-type estimate for `G_L`, not by
substituting the definition of `P_L`.  If that package recombines exactly to
`EG_LOCK`, the Loewner route is an equivalence and must be closed.
