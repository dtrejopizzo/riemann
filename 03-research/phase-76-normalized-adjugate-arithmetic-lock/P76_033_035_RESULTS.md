# P76.033-P76.035 - Safe-axis scaling and logarithmic endpoint

## Direct cofinal audit

Every CCM entry was rebuilt at 70 digits.  On the shallow path with pole
horizon near 18 (`N/L` approximately 4), the bilateral safe-axis maximum
relative error at `sigma=0.6,0.75,1,1.5,2` was

```text
lambda=4,  N=12: 0.0490
lambda=6,  N=15: 0.1046
lambda=8,  N=16: 0.1575
lambda=10, N=18: 0.1852
```

Thus fixed pole coverage is not an admissible identification path.

At `lambda=8,N=22`, rebuilt at 100 digits, the lowest eigenvalue and gap are

```text
mu  =1.1586e-62,
gap =1.9395e-59.
```

The maximum error falls from `0.1575` to `0.1016`.  This both confirms the
need for deep cutoffs and explains why the 50-digit run in P76.028 failed.

## Safe ratios

At fixed `lambda=6`, normalization at `sigma_0=1` gives maximum ratio errors

```text
N=6:  0.2707
N=9:  0.1591
N=12: 0.1068.
```

The safe ratio removes every critical-center normalization and decreases
monotonically with the finite section.

## Logarithmic derivative

For `SR-LOG`, the maximum relative errors decrease

```text
N=6:  1.7435
N=9:  1.0697
N=12: 0.7354.
```

At each `N` the relative defect is nearly constant across
`sigma=0.6,...,2`.  This points to a dominant finite-section transfer defect
rather than a badly behaved Euler tail.

## Decision

The live theorem is `SR-LOG`, followed by integration to `SR-SAFE` and the
canonical-product closure.  Its proof must split

```text
finite Schur log derivative - Xi log derivative
= finite-section transfer error + absolutely convergent prime tail
  + archimedean cutoff tail,
```

with the first term controlled by the exact rank-two displacement identity.
