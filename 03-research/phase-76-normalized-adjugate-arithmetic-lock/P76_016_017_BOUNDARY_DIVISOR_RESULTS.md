# P76.016-P76.017 - Rank-two wedge and boundary divisor

## Wedge audit

The rank-two generator identity writes each boundary component as

```text
T_b(z)
=1/(z-d_b)
 +(2/L)(alpha-S_b beta)
 +(4/L^2)(alpha p-beta q).
```

The last term is the antisymmetric determinant

```text
alpha p-beta q
=det [[alpha,beta],[q,p]].
```

It is load-bearing.  At the first critical ordinate:

```text
N side   |pole|      |linear|     |wedge|      |T_b|
6  -     4.06e-2     5.71e4       5.71e4       1.02e-10
6  +     2.77e-1     3.89e5       3.89e5       1.12e-10
9  -     3.34e-2     3.72e5       3.72e5       1.19e-17
9  +     6.08e-1     6.75e6       6.75e6       5.30e-17
```

The generator formula agrees with the direct inner solve to `1e-53--1e-62`.
Any proof must preserve the wedge; separate absolute bounds lose more than
twenty orders in the resolved cases.

## Phase-free boundary roots

Each rational component

```text
T_b(z)=1/(z-d_b)-sum_n x_b(n)/(z-d_n)
```

has a root near the first critical ordinate.  Multiprecision Newton isolation
gives:

```text
N       tau_-(N)-gamma_1       tau_+(N)-gamma_1
5       3.51210e-5             1.09900e-5
6       7.83157e-7             1.83399e-7
7       2.79420e-9             2.30294e-10
8       7.45595e-13            3.21822e-15

```

The derivatives at `gamma_1` are nonzero and grow from `1e-5--1e-4` at
`N=5` to `8.5` and `133` at `N=9`.  Hence the small transfer values are
genuine simple-root locking, not flatness.

## Canonical one-boundary completion

For one boundary column put

```text
A=H_{N-1}-mu_N I>0,
x=A^(-1)b,
h_b^can=mu_N+b^T A^(-1)b.
```

Strict interlacing supplies `A>0`.  Define

```text
K_b^can=
[[H_{N-1}, b],
 [b^T,       h_b^can]].
```

Then

```text
K_b^can-mu_N I
=[[A,b],[b^T,b^T A^(-1)b]]
=[A^(1/2);b^T A^(-1/2)]
 [A^(1/2),A^(-1/2)b]
>=0.                                             (BC-1)
```

Its kernel is generated by

```text
y_b=(x,-1),
```

and

```text
T_b(z)=-r_z y_b.                                (BC-2)
```

Thus `T_b` is the Cauchy divisor of a canonical simple ground line of a
positive-semidefinite one-boundary completion.  This positivity is a finite
Schur identity following from interlacing; it does not assert positivity of
the original Weil form on an exhausting space.

## New closure target

Define the two boundary numerators by clearing their fixed real pole meshes:

```text
T_-(z)=N_-(z)/Q_-(z),
T_+(z)=N_+(z)/Q_+(z).
```

The new theorem is

```text
COMMON-BOUNDARY-DIVISOR:
N_-(z) and N_+(z) have simple roots tau_-(L),tau_+(L)
in every resolved critical window, with

|tau_+(L)-tau_-(L)|=O_M(L^-M),
|tau_+/- (L)-gamma|=O_M(L^-M).
```

Equivalently, a normalized local resultant/subresultant of `N_-` and `N_+`
must be all-orders small and its common local factor must be identified with
the completed-zeta divisor without inserting a zero list.

This target is phase-free, two-dimensional, falsifier-stable, and strictly
stronger than `NORMALIZED-ARITH-LOCK`.  It is the current live endpoint.
