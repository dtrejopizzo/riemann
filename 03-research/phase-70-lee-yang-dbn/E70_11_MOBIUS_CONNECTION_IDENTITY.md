## Semigroup shift algebra

Work in the multiplicative semigroup algebra generated by

```text
e_n = n^{-1/2} S_log n,        n>=1,
```

so that

```text
e_m e_n = e_mn.
```

Introduce an Abel parameter `epsilon>0` and define

```text
Z_epsilon = sum_{n>=1} n^{-epsilon} e_n,
M_epsilon = sum_{n>=1} mu(n)n^{-epsilon} e_n.
```

Algebraically, with Dirichlet convolution,

```text
M_epsilon Z_epsilon = Z_epsilon M_epsilon = e_1.
```

This is just Mobius inversion. No zero location and no positivity of zeta is used.

## The derivation

Let `delta` be the scale derivation

```text
delta(e_n) = (log n) e_n.
```

Then

```text
delta(Z_epsilon)
 = sum_{n>=1} (log n)n^{-epsilon} e_n.
```

Therefore

```text
M_epsilon delta(Z_epsilon)
 = sum_{N>=1} N^{-epsilon}
     (sum_{de=N} mu(d) log e) e_N.
```

Using the arithmetic identity

```text
Lambda = mu * log,
```

this gives the exact operator identity

```text
V_{Lambda,epsilon}
 := M_epsilon delta(Z_epsilon)
  = sum_{N>=2} Lambda(N)N^{-epsilon} e_N
  = sum_{N>=2} Lambda(N)N^{-1/2-epsilon} S_log N.
```

Thus

```text
T_{Lambda,epsilon} = V_{Lambda,epsilon} + V_{Lambda,epsilon}^*.
```

This is the first non-fitted construction of the prime current in the Phase 70 language.

## Interpretation

The prime current is the logarithmic derivative of the Euler unit:

```text
V_{Lambda,epsilon} = Z_epsilon^{-1} delta Z_epsilon.
```

Equivalently, it is a connection one-form on the Euler/Mobius line.

This matters because E70.10 showed that an ordinary square in `V_Lambda` is contaminated by the
Euler ratio-square `V_Lambda^*V_Lambda`. The current should not be squared as a free positive object.
It should be treated as a connection term, where its quadratic part is either curvature, boundary, or
gauge artifact.

