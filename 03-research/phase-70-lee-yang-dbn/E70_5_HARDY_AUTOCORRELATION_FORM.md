# E70.5 — Hardy/autocorrelation form of AHM

**Date:** 2026-07-07.
**Role:** rewrite Cauchy-Weil positivity as the concrete arithmetic autocorrelation inequality that a
proof must establish.

## Cauchy tests as Laplace transforms

Let `z_j=x_j+i y_j` with `y_j>0` and

```text
phi(u) = sum_j c_j/(u-z_j).
```

Using the boundary convention

```text
1/(u-z) = i integral_0^infty exp(-iut) exp(i z t) dt,
```

we write

```text
phi(u) = i integral_0^infty f(t) exp(-iut) dt,
f(t) = sum_j c_j exp(i z_j t).
```

Because `Im z_j>0`, `f(t)` decays exponentially on `t>=0`.

Thus the Cauchy test cone is the Laplace/Hardy cone generated by finite exponential sums on the
positive half-line.

## Autocorrelation

The Fourier transform of `|phi|^2` is the autocorrelation of `f`. For `a>=0`, define

```text
C_f(a) = integral_0^infty f(t+a) conjugate(f(t)) dt.
```

Then, up to the fixed Fourier-normalization convention,

```text
widehat{|phi|^2}(a) = C_f(a),
widehat{|phi|^2}(-a) = conjugate(C_f(a)).
```

So the Euler/vonMangoldt part of the explicit formula samples exactly

```text
C_f(log n)
```

at prime-power delays.

## The exact inequality shape

Cauchy-Weil positivity becomes:

```text
A[f] >= P[f],
```

where

```text
P[f] =
  2 Re sum_{n>=2} Lambda(n) n^{-1/2} C_f(log n)
```

with the appropriate phase/gauge convention, and `A[f]` is the explicit Gamma/polar archimedean
quadratic form.

Equivalently:

```text
A[f] - 2 Re sum_{n>=2} Lambda(n)n^{-1/2} C_f(log n) >= 0
```

for every finite exponential sum

```text
f(t)=sum_j c_j exp(i z_j t),   Im z_j>0.
```

This is the concrete form of AHM.

## Why absolute estimates fail

The trivial estimate

```text
|C_f(log n)| <= C_f(0)
```

or any termwise Cauchy-Schwarz bound gives the incoherent ceiling

```text
S_abs = sum_n Lambda(n)n^{-1/2} * size bound,
```

which E67.4 measured to be enormous compared with the true marginal norm:

```text
true norm ~ 1,
S_abs    ~ 219, 18683, ...
```

Therefore the proof cannot be an absolute majorization. It must prove cancellation in

```text
Re sum_n Lambda(n)n^{-1/2} C_f(log n)
```

for every Hardy autocorrelation `C_f`.

## The final arithmetic inequality

The real missing lemma can now be written:

### Hardy-Euler Autocorrelation Lemma

For every finite exponential sum `f` on `R_+`,

```text
2 Re sum_{n>=2} Lambda(n)n^{-1/2} C_f(log n)
  <= A[f],
```

where `A[f]` is the Gamma/polar quadratic form coming from the archimedean factor of `xi`.

This lemma is equivalent to Cauchy-Weil positivity, hence to AHM, hence to `Omega_7`.

## What a proof must exploit

The inequality is not a positivity of the weights `Lambda(n)`. It must exploit:

1. the exact placement of the sampling set `log n`;
2. the prime-power weights `Lambda(n)`;
3. the functional equation through the Gamma/polar form `A[f]`;
4. cancellation of the real part of the autocorrelation sum;
5. no zero locations.

In this language, Davenport-Heilbronn should fail because its coefficients can imitate the functional
equation but do not produce the same Euler autocorrelation inequality.
