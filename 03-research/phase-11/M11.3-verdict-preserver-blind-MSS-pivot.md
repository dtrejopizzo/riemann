## The shift, identified — and its arithmetic

Since $\Xi(t)=\int_0^\infty\Phi(u)\cos(tu)\,du$ (the de Bruijn–Newman kernel, $\Phi>0$), the coefficients are
$$b(k)=(-1)^k a(k)=\frac{m_{2k}}{(2k)!},\qquad m_{2k}=\int u^{2k}\Phi(u)\,du,$$
the **even moments of $\Phi$** — *this* is where the $\zeta$ arithmetic sits. The Jensen shift $n\to n+1$
corresponds to $\Phi\to u^2\Phi$, i.e. $\Xi\to-\Xi''$ (**differentiation**), which **preserves the
Laguerre–Pólya class** (Rolle / Gauss–Lucas) — a Borcea–Brändén stability preserver.

## The genuine escape (the pivot, and why it is new)

The MSS technique is **not** a preserving operator — it is an **averaging** construction. It proves
real-rootedness of an **expected characteristic polynomial** of a random ensemble, via common interlacing,
**without a positivity certificate**. So the genuine escape is:

> **M11.4 target — a constructive Hilbert–Pólya via MSS.** Realize $\Xi$ (or its Jensen polynomials) as the
> **expected characteristic polynomial** $\mathbb E\det(xI-A_\omega)$ of a random matrix ensemble $A_\omega$
> whose distribution **carries the $\zeta$ arithmetic** (the moments $m_{2k}$). Then the MSS interlacing-family
> argument would force **real-rootedness** (RH) — *not* by self-adjointness (the classical Hilbert–Pólya, where
> constructing the operator is the wall) but by **interlacing** (a positivity-free route to real spectra).

This is genuinely new in two ways: (i) it is the first time the arithmetic-blindness is met with the right
response — an *averaging* construction rather than another preserving operator; (ii) it replaces the classical
Hilbert–Pólya requirement (self-adjointness, which has resisted for a century) with the **MSS interlacing**
requirement (a different, modern, positivity-free tool). The arithmetic enters through the **ensemble's
distribution**, the one place N5/N6 do not forbid.
