## 1. The task, stated exactly

Every proof of RH must, at some point, establish the **absence** of off-line zeros: $N_{\rm off}(T)=0$ for all
$T$. M12.9 left two open links; the deeper one is **N7**: a *deterministic* implication
$$\text{[control of an extreme / large-value quantity]}\ \Longrightarrow\ N_{\rm off}=0.$$
M12.10 asks whether such an implication can be made rigorous **without** invoking a positivity (which would be
the capstone).

## 2. The enumeration: every known "absence of zeros" mechanism

I enumerated the mechanisms by which mathematics proves a function has no zeros in a region, and classified each:

| mechanism | example | type | reaches "none, all $T$, deterministic"? |
|---|---|---|---|
| **(a) Positivity** | Weil explicit formula; Hodge index (Weil conj.); the $3+4\cos\theta+\cos2\theta\ge0$ zero-free region; Selberg/Hilbert–Pólya self-adjointness | a "$\ge0$" | **yes — but it is the capstone** (wrong sign / not available) |
| **(b) Counting / density** | zero-density theorems $N(\sigma,T)\ll T^{c(1-\sigma)}$ | upper bound on a count | reaches "**few**", never "none" |
| **(c) Finite computation** | verified RH to height $10^{13}$ | exhaustive check | reaches height $T$, never **all** $T$ |
| **(d) Statistics** | pair correlation; multiplicative chaos; the FHK extreme | ensemble/probabilistic | **describes**, does not **force** (this is N7) |

> **The meta-obstruction.** *There is no known mechanism that is simultaneously (i) deterministic, (ii) yields
> absence (not "few"), (iii) uniform in $T$, and (iv) not a positivity. The four available mechanisms each fail
> exactly one of (iv), (ii), (iii), (i+iv).* The **wrong-sign capstone** is the failure of (iv) in mechanism
> (a); **N7** is the failure of "force" in mechanism (d). **They are two faces of one gap.**

## 3. Why closing N7 loops back (each concrete attempt)

I tried each concrete route to a deterministic, non-positivity "force reality," and each returns to (a)–(d):

- **Argument principle / winding.** $N_{\rm off}=\frac1{2\pi}\oint d\arg\zeta$ over the off-line region — an
  *integer*. To conclude $0$, bound the winding $<1$. Bounding $\arg\zeta$ on the contour is the **zero-free
  region** method, whose engine is the $3+4\cos\theta+\cos2\theta\ge0$ **positivity** (a). Loops to the capstone.
- **Integrality + the de Bruijn–Newman flow.** $N_{\rm off}(t)\in2\mathbb Z_{\ge0}$, $=0$ for $t\ge\Lambda$;
  RH $=$ no collision in $(0,\Lambda_{\rm up}]$. Ruling out collisions over **all** heights is a uniform integer
  bound — needs an upper bound (a/b) or a finite check (c). The specific input (the primes) enters via the
  explicit formula $=$ positivity (a) or the moment problem (N7/d).
- **Off-line zero $\Rightarrow$ anomalous large values.** An off-line zero perturbs $\zeta$ and could exceed an
  unconditional upper bound on the extreme — *if* such a sharp bound existed (the supercritical moment problem,
  open) *and* the implication were quantitative (the heuristic N7 step). Loops to (d)+the open moment problem.
- **Rigidity.** The function-field RH forces $|\alpha|=q^{1/2}$ from **algebraicity** (Frobenius eigenvalues are
  algebraic integers) + the Hodge bound. $\zeta$'s zeros are **transcendental** — no analogous discreteness is
  known. The rigidity angle needs a *new discreteness structure* for the zeros.

Every route returns to a positivity (a), a count that stops at "few" (b), a finite check (c), or statistics that
describe but do not force (d). **N7 cannot be closed with current mechanisms.**

## 4. The honest verdict — and the precise naming of what a crossing requires

> **A proof of RH requires a mechanism for the *absence* of zeros that is deterministic, uniform, and not a
> positivity.** No such mechanism exists in 2026 mathematics. The wrong-sign capstone (mechanism (a) is a
> positivity) and the probabilistic–deterministic barrier N7 (mechanism (d) only describes) are the two faces of
> this single gap. The program has now characterized the obstruction completely: it is not that we lack a
> *route*, but that we lack a *new kind of theorem* — a non-positivity deterministic absence principle.

The one place such a principle could conceivably come from is **(d-prime) a new discreteness / rigidity for the
zeros** — an analogue of the algebraicity that closes the function-field case — which would let an *integer*
zero-count be pinned to $0$ by exactness rather than by a positivity. This is the function-field $\mathbb F_1$ /
arithmetic-geometry dream (Phase 10's cohomological turn aimed here and reduced to a Hodge positivity, P13). A
**genuinely new** discreteness — not yet a Hodge positivity in disguise — is the missing idea. We do not have
it, and neither does the field.
