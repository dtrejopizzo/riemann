# E73.243 - Rational left-coborder audit with fixed geometric betas

Date: 2026-07-14.

## 1. Purpose

E73.242 shows that polynomial Cauchy primitives

```text
span{D^k q_j^*}
```

do not reduce the scalar obstruction in the `CURV-COB` target.  This note
tests the next fixed symbolic enlargement: rational Cauchy primitives with
geometric denominators.

## 2. Module

For a Cauchy plane `Q_w={q_w,q_-w}`, let

```text
beta in B_geom(w,L)={ |w|, 2pi/L, 1 }.
```

The tested module is

```text
P_rat(r,s)
= span{ D^k q_j^*,
        D^k q_j^*/(D^2+beta^2)^m :
        j=1,2, 0<=k<=r, beta in B_geom, 1<=m<=s }.
```

The betas are fixed before seeing the residual and do not depend on `xi`,
`h=Qxi`, or the final scalar center.

## 3. Audit

As in E73.242, fit

```text
rho_a approx Y^*E,      Y in P_rat(r,s),
E=H_L-mu_L I.
```

Measure:

```text
row residual norm,
scalar residual pairing against xi_L.
```

The row norm improves compared with the purely polynomial module, but the
scalar pairing remains at the original center scale in the tested windows.

## 4. Meaning

Fixed geometric rational denominators are still not the missing coborder.
They improve approximation geometry but do not hit the scalar obstruction.

This narrows the remaining route:

```text
CURV-COB requires denominators generated by the actual partial-fraction
structure of the CCM/Loewner cell, not just natural geometric scales.
```

## 5. Status

```text
tested:  rational Cauchy module with fixed geometric betas;
failed:  scalar obstruction remains;
next:    derive primitive denominators symbolically from the finite
         Loewner divided-difference action itself.
```
