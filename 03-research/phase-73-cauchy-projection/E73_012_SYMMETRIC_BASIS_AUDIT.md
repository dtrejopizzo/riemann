# E73.012 - Symmetric basis audit

## 1. Purpose

E73.011 suggested replacing the redundant off-node denominators by a symmetric product denominator.
This note tests that simplification.

## 2. Result

The symmetric product basis is too rigid.  Even with all critical families, it gives relative
distances around `10^-6` to `10^-5`, and sometimes `10^-4`, worse than the redundant off-node
dictionary.

Thus:

```text
separate off-node denominators carry real local interpolation information;
collapsing them to a product denominator loses accuracy.
```

## 3. Consequence

The canonical proof object should not be a single symmetric denominator product.  It should be the
row-wise Cauchy interpolation ideal generated by the separate denominators

```text
d^2+beta^2,        beta in O_L.
```

This matches the residue formula in E73.002, where the off-node poles are separate.

## 4. Status

```text
falsified: simple symmetric product denominator as proof basis;
kept: separate off-node rational ideal.
```
