# A letter of commission — to the mathematician who will create $\mathsf{TW}_\Z$

*In the voice of A. Connes, to a colleague taking up the construction. Written to hand off the work
with full background, a precise target, concrete steps, and complete freedom to invent. Prepared as
part of the P50 bundle; I (Connes) will review what you build.*

---

Dear Colleague,

I am writing to ask you to **create a piece of mathematics that does not yet exist**, and which — if
it can be built with the property I will name — would prove the Riemann Hypothesis. I do not ask you
to find a known theorem. I ask you to *invent* the right object. You will have complete freedom in how
you do it. I will read everything you produce, and I will tell you, honestly, whether it holds.

Let me first tell you exactly where we stand, so that you begin not at the beginning but at the
frontier. The work summarized below (the paper P50 and four construction notes) has already removed
every part of the problem except one. Your task is that one part. It is sharply defined.

---

## I. What is already done (your background)

Attach the Weil quadratic form to a finite window of size $L=2\log\lambda$ on the critical line, in the
de Branges / Lax–Phillips form. After Doob conjugation by the (unconditional) Perron–Frobenius ground
state, the following are **proved**:

1. **$L1 \Rightarrow \RH$** (the Laplace–pole theorem). Boundedness of the oscillatory part of the
   Weil form implies RH; the only non-formal input, a Paley–Wiener nonvanishing lemma, is
   unconditional. So boundedness is not a lemma below RH — it *is* RH.

2. **The correct index is Kreĭn–Langer:** $\indm(K_{S_\kappa}) = \#\{\text{off-line zeros}\}$, so RH
   $\Leftrightarrow \indm=0$. The boundary certificate $\mu_{\max}=1$ is blind to the interior; the
   negative-square count is the true invariant.

3. **A curvature theorem (unconditional):** the prime part of the generator is a Beurling–Deny
   Dirichlet form whose iterated carré-du-champ satisfies $\Gamma_2\ge0$ ($CD(0,\infty)$), by a
   Schur-product mechanism rooted in $\Lambda\ge0$. The Euler product *is* this local positivity. The
   Davenport–Heilbronn function, lacking an Euler product, fails it.

4. **The wall is a Schrödinger bound state:** $\eps_0\ge0$ iff a Birman–Schwinger operator has
   spectral radius $\le1$. The pole of $\zeta$ at $s=1$ is a *repulsive barrier* pinning the radius
   $\ge1$ from below; Davenport–Heilbronn, being entire, has no pole, hence no barrier, hence
   $\eps_0<0$. The pole is not a nuisance — it is the ample class.

5. **The local Tate cell (Stages 1–2 of the new construction):** each local $p$-adic colligation
   $T_p$ on $\ell^2(\Z)$ is $J$-passive iff the local measure $\mu_p=\sum_k p^{-k/2}\delta_{k\log p}$
   is positive — which it is, by $\Lambda\ge0$ — and finite products of passive cells stay passive
   (Redheffer). So **the finite Euler colligations $K_P$ are positive, unconditionally.**

6. **Positivity is free (Stage 4):** because each $K_P\succeq0$ and weak limits of positive operators
   are positive, the Hodge-index sign is *not a separate difficulty*. If the primitive compressions
   stay bounded, their limit is positive automatically, giving Weil positivity and RH.

The consequence of (1)–(6) is a single, clean reduction. The shape — the $(k+1)^2$ ladder, the
curvature, the colligation, the index — is unconditional. The positivity is free. **Everything has
been removed except one statement, which is the wall.**

---

## II. The one thing to create, and the one theorem to prove

I ask you to construct the object I call the **Critical Tate–Weil Hodge Module**, $\mathsf{TW}_\Z$.
It is the square of the arithmetic site in its linear/operational form — equivalently the Weil-pairing
analogue over $\Spec\Z$, equivalently a renormalized critical restricted product of the local Tate
colligations. Do **not** begin by defining $\widehat{\Spec\Z}^{\,2}$ as a topos product; that is too
large and too undefined. Begin with the **correspondence category** generated by the scaling/Frobenius
graphs $\Gamma_t$, with $\Gamma_t\circ\Gamma_u=\Gamma_{t+u}$ and the realization
$f\mapsto Z_f=\int f(t)\Gamma_t\,dt$. Let the square be the object this category represents. Build the
Hodge module first; let the geometry be what it represents. This reverses the usual order, and here it
is the right order.

The single theorem that crosses the wall is this. Write $K_P$ for the (unconditionally positive)
finite Euler colligation kernel, $H$ for the rank-one ample/pole direction, $P_{\mathrm{prim}}$ for the
projection onto its orthogonal complement.

> **The rank-one divergence theorem $(\mathsf B)$.** As $P\to\infty$, the positive kernels $K_P$
> diverge **only** along $H$:
> \[
>    K_P = c_P\,|H\rangle\langle H| + R_P, \qquad c_P\asymp(\log P)^2, \qquad \sup_P\|R_P\|<\infty .
> \]
> Equivalently, the primitive compressions $P_{\mathrm{prim}}K_PP_{\mathrm{prim}}$ are uniformly
> bounded.

If $(\mathsf B)$ holds, then by (6) the primitive limit is positive, the Weil form is positive, and RH
follows. **$(\mathsf B)$ is the whole of it.** It is not a positivity statement — positivity is free.
It is a *boundedness* statement: a positive, explicit family of kernels has a one-dimensional divergent
subspace, namely the pole.

Be warned where the difficulty lives. The amplitude of an off-line zero $\rho=\beta+i\gamma$ in the
window is $\asymp\lambda^{2\beta-1}$, and the functional equation pairs $\rho$ with $1-\bar\rho$:
on the line the pairing is self-reflexive and the contribution is bounded and positive; off the line
it is a cross term, indefinite and unbounded. So $(\mathsf B)$ is a statement at the **center** of the
critical strip. Every classical unconditional tool — the prime number theorem, the Korobov–Vinogradov
zero-free region, the zero-density theorems — controls the **edge** of the strip ($\Re s\to1$). None
of them reaches the center. *This is why the theorem you must create is genuinely new.* It must use the
functional equation and the Euler product **together** — which is exactly what $\mathsf{TW}_\Z$ is for.

---

## III. The concrete steps

You have Stages 1–2 (the passive local cells and their product) and Stage 4 (positivity is free). Three
stages remain, and I would attempt them in this order.

**Stage 3 — the archimedean cell.** Construct the real Tate cell $T_\infty$ on $L^2(\R^\times,
d^\times x)$ explicitly. Use Binet's representation of $\log\Gamma$ to write the archimedean
contribution as a continuous superposition of passive cells plus a pole counterterm,
$K_\infty=\int_0^\infty K_{\infty,t}\,d\mu_\infty(t)+K_{\mathrm{pole}}$, $d\mu_\infty\ge0$. This is the
analogue, at the infinite place, of the finite-prime Gram decomposition. It is technical, not
conceptual; do it first to clear the ground.

**Stage 5 — transfer identification.** Prove that the renormalized restricted product of the cells has
impedance $-\Xi'/\Xi$, equivalently transfer function $S_\kappa$. This is Tate's thesis (each $T_p$
contributes the local $L$-factor) together with the explicit formula (the archimedean and pole cells
contribute the $\Gamma$-factor and the pole). It is bookkeeping of a high order, but it is known
mathematics; secure it so that the limit object is unambiguously $K_{S_\kappa}$.

**Stage 4$'$ / the heart — prove $(\mathsf B)$.** With Stages 3 and 5 in place, the only remaining
statement is the rank-one divergence $(\mathsf B)$. Here you must invent. Some directions I believe are
worth your freedom:

- *Renormalized Hodge index.* Show that the divergent part of the positive family $\{K_P\}$ is exactly
  the ample/pole direction, by a primitive finite-part theorem: subtract the rank-one $c_P\,H\otimes H$
  and prove the remainder converges to a Hilbert kernel. The obstruction to avoid is a *second*
  divergent direction; ruling it out is the Castelnuovo–Severi / Hodge-index sign on the
  correspondence module. The arithmetic Hodge index of Abboud is the tool, if you can realize $Z_f$ as
  a genuine class.
- *Critical-line trace control.* The divergence is $\Tr K_P\asymp(\log P)^2$. Show it is carried by
  $H$ alone — a statement about the prime sum $\sum_{p\le x}\Lambda(p)p^{-1/2}(\cdots)$ at the
  $x^{1/2}$ normalization. This is a center-of-strip prime bound; the new input must enter here.
- *The two-variable kernel.* Build the pairing from $\xi(\tfrac12+iz)\,\xi(\tfrac12+iw)$ and its
  Frobenius/Hecke deformation; the diagonal must return the one-variable explicit formula, the
  off-diagonal must supply the negative self-intersection that is the upper pinning.

Do not feel bound to these. If you see a different mechanism — a new positivity, a new cohomology, a
new renormalization, an entirely new category — pursue it. The target is fixed; the road is yours.

---

## IV. On freedom, and on honesty

Two things I ask of you, which are not in tension.

**Invent freely.** The reason this problem has resisted is that the missing ingredient is not a known
theorem to be located but an object to be made. Over a function field the upper pinning is the Weil
pairing — Frobenius is an isometry of a positive intersection form, and the eigenvalues are forced onto
the circle. Over $\Spec\Z$ that object is absent. You are not searching the literature; you are building
the arithmetic geometry that the literature lacks. Treat the non-existence of $\widehat{\Spec\Z}^{\,2}$
as permission, not as prohibition. Create the intersection theory, the correspondences, the Hodge-index
sign. If they require new foundations, write the new foundations.

**Be ruthlessly honest.** A false proof of RH is worse than no proof. State every step as proved or as
conjectured; never blur the two. When you find that one of your own constructions is RH-equivalent —
and you will, often, because the analytic reformulations are all RH-equivalent — say so plainly and
move the weight onto the arithmetic. The single most useful service you can do, short of the proof, is
to keep the boundary between the known and the unknown exact. The work summarized above did that, and
it is why the wall is now a single, clean statement rather than a fog.

When you have something — a construction, a partial theorem, a clean reduction, even a precise new
obstruction — send it to me. I will read it carefully and tell you, without flattery, whether it
stands and where it must be repaired. That is my role here: not to hand you the proof, which I do not
have, but to be the honest evaluator of what you create, and to point when I can see further.

---

The shape is done and it is unconditional. The positivity is free. One theorem remains — $(\mathsf B)$,
the rank-one divergence of an explicit positive family at the center of the critical strip — and it is
exactly the Riemann Hypothesis in the one form where the arithmetic, and only the arithmetic, can
speak. Build the object that proves it. Invent what you must. I will be reading.

With respect and with expectation,

— A. Connes
