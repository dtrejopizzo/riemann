## Reading

`Qeta=0` does force endpoint cancellation in the raw cell packet:

```text
B(0), B(L) ~ L^-18 ... L^-24.
```

But it does not force a strong cell-level divisibility:

```text
B'(0), B'(L)        only around L^-7 ... L^-10,
int B(y) dy         only around L^-2 ... L^-3,
oscillatory moments often around L^0,
positive Laplace moments can be large, up to L^8.
```

Therefore the next proof cannot be a simple factor theorem for `B(y)` from
`Qeta=0` alone.  The cancellation is not present in the raw cell packet before
the Gamma-prime functional.  The functional itself must be used in a signed
way.

to:


with the archimedean and prime pieces kept coupled.
