## What we built and found

We reconstructed the candidate intersection form: the Weil–Carleson form $\mathfrak t=A_\Phi-P_F$ (the
**primitive part**, PSD = RH) plus one adjoined **ample** direction (the archimedean polarization), giving a
finite-dimensional model of the Hodge-index pairing. We then grew the band $d$ — i.e. grew the cohomology
dimension ("genus") toward $\zeta$ — and tracked the **Hodge gap** $\lambda_{\min}(\mathfrak t)$ and the
signature of the full form $I$.

| $d$ | $x=2d/\log T_0$ | Hodge gap $\lambda_{\min}(\mathfrak t)$ | signature of $I$ | verdict |
|---:|---:|---:|---:|---|
| 1.0 | 0.43 | $+0.377$ | $(1,21,0)$ | **Lorentzian, definite** (curve-like) |
| 1.5 | 0.65 | $+0.008$ | $(1,21,0)$ | Lorentzian, gap shrinking |
| 2.0 | 0.87 | $+3\times10^{-9}$ | $(1,19,2)$ | **degenerate** (2 null directions) |
| 3.0 | 1.30 | $\sim0$ | $(1,15,6)$ | degenerate |
| 4.0 | 1.74 | $\sim0$ | $(1,12,9)$ | degenerate |

**The finding.** For finite genus (small band) the Weil–Carleson form is **definite** with a positive Hodge
gap — the curve-case structure (Lorentzian, non-degenerate $(1,N,0)$) holds exactly. As the genus grows toward
$\zeta$, the **Hodge gap $\to0$ and the intersection form DEGENERATES**: null directions appear, the signature
becomes $(1,N{-}k,k)$ with $k$ growing. This is the **N3 saturation, in cohomological language.**

## What it means (and the unification)

The curve-case proof of RH (Weil conjectures) runs through the Hodge index theorem on $C\times C$, which
requires the intersection form to be **definite** on the primitive part — and it is, because the genus is
**finite** (the Néron–Severi group has finite rank, so there is a spectral gap). M10.1 shows that $\zeta$'s
candidate "arithmetic surface" has **infinite genus with a vanishing Hodge gap**: the primitive intersection
form is only **marginally** semidefinite and **degenerates** in the limit.

> **The unification.** Four phenomena the program met separately are **one**:
> $$\text{N3 saturation } (C\equiv1)\ =\ \Lambda=0\ \text{marginality}\ =\ \text{vanishing Hodge gap}\ =\ \text{degeneration of the intersection form.}$$
> The wrong-sign capstone, in cohomological language, reads: *the Hodge-index positivity exists but is
> **marginal** (zero-gap) for $\zeta$, whereas it is **definite** (positive-gap) for curves; the finite-rank
> Hodge argument does not transfer.*

## The new mathematics this names (the honest target)

The cohomological turn does **not** trivially cross — the degeneration is the marginality wall in new clothes.
**But** it identifies, precisely and for the first time, *what must be invented*:

> **The required new mathematics: a regularized, INFINITE-DIMENSIONAL Hodge index theorem** that controls the
> **marginal (zero-gap, degenerate)** primitive intersection form of $\zeta$'s arithmetic surface — i.e. a
> $\zeta$-regularized Castelnuovo–Severi positivity surviving the infinite-genus limit.

This is exactly the open heart of the Deninger / Connes–Consani programs, now given a **concrete, measurable
diagnostic**: the Hodge gap $\lambda_{\min}(\mathfrak t)\to0$ that our detector computes. Unlike the abstract
programs, we have the **primitive block explicitly and numerically**, and we can watch it degenerate — which
turns "invent the cohomology" into the sharper "regularize the degeneration of *this measured form*."
