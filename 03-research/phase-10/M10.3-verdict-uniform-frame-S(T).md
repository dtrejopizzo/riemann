## The test and the finding

RH (regularized Hodge index) $\iff$ the regularized gap $\lambda_{\min}(G)$ stays bounded below **uniformly in
$T$** $\iff$ the zeros are a uniform Riesz sequence. Since the zero density $\rho(T)=\frac{\log(T/2\pi)}{2\pi}\to\infty$,
the band must grow $d\sim\frac12\log(T/2\pi)$ (critical sampling) to keep resolving. At a **fixed sampling
ratio** ($K/M\approx0.8$), across two orders of magnitude in height:

| $T_0$ | band $d$ | $K/M$ | regularized gap $\lambda_{\min}(G)$ |
|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| 50 | 1.30 | 15/21 | 0.284 |
| 100 | 1.73 | 16/21 | 0.289 |
| 300 | 2.42 | 16/21 | 0.246 |
| 1000 | 3.17 | 17/21 | 0.292 |
| 3000 | 3.86 | 17/21 | 0.204 |

> **The regularized gap is remarkably $T$-STABLE** ($\approx0.20$–$0.29$, no systematic degradation across
> $T{=}50\to3000$). This is the **first numerical evidence of a uniform lower frame bound** — i.e. that the
> regularized Hodge index positivity could genuinely hold. Downward fluctuations track the tightest pair in the
> window (Lehmer); the uniform infimum is set by the worst cluster $=$ the cornered target (U).

## The new handle — and why it is different (S(T) regularity, a two-sided tool)

The uniform lower frame bound for reproducing kernels in a de Branges space $H(E)$ is governed, by the
**Pavlov / Hrushchev–Nikolskii–Pavlov / Seip** theory, by a **Muckenhoupt $A_2$ (Helson–Szegő)** condition on
$|E|^2$. For $E$ built from $\xi$, $|E(t)|^2\asymp|\zeta(\tfrac12+it)|^2$-type data, whose argument is the
**$S(T)=\frac1\pi\arg\zeta(\tfrac12+iT)$** function. So:

> **RH (regularized Hodge / uniform Riesz basis)** $\iff$ a **uniform Muckenhoupt $A_2$ condition** on the de
> Branges $E\sim\xi$ $\iff$ a **regularity condition on $S(T)$** (the fluctuation of the zero counting).

This is the program's most favorable reformulation, for two reasons:
1. **It is two-sided.** A Muckenhoupt $A_2$ / Riesz-basis condition is a *definiteness* ($c\le\lambda\le C$),
   not a one-sided lower bound. It is therefore plausibly **outside the wrong-sign capstone** (which obstructs
   one-sided positivity tools).
2. **It has unconditional partial control.** $S(T)$ is one of the best-understood objects in the theory:
   **Selberg** proved $S(T)=O(\log T)$ and computed all its moments unconditionally
   ($\frac1T\int_0^T|S(t)|^{2k}dt\sim c_k(\log\log T)^k$), and the distribution of $S(T)$ is known (Gaussian,
   Selberg's central limit theorem) — *unconditionally*. These are genuine two-sided tools on exactly the
   quantity the frame bound needs.

## Honest status

- The uniform lower frame bound is **$T$-stable in the measured range** ($\sim0.2$, $T\le3000$) — encouraging,
  the first such evidence; but the range does not reach the tightest Lehmer pairs, so it is **suggestive, not a
  proof** of the uniform bound.
- **The reformulation is real:** RH $\iff$ uniform $A_2$ on $E\sim\xi$ $\iff$ $S(T)$ regularity (Pavlov/Seip/de
  Branges). Under RH the zeros are a de Branges Riesz basis (known); unconditionally the full uniform $A_2$ is
  **open** — so this is still (U) in the limit.
- **But the handle is genuinely new and two-sided:** $S(T)$ regularity, with Selberg's *unconditional*
  moments/CLT, is a different and more favorable tool than the wrong-signed pair-correlation upper bound. It is
  the first target the program reaches where substantial *unconditional, two-sided* control already exists.

> **The cohomological route, through M10.1–M10.3, has converted RH from a one-sided pair-correlation upper
> bound (wrong-signed, intractable) into a uniform Riesz/Muckenhoupt definiteness governed by $S(T)$
> regularity — a two-sided condition with real unconditional partial control (Selberg). This is the single
> most promising reformulation the program has produced.**
