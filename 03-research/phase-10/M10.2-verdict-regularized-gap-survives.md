## The construction and the finding

M10.1 showed the **bare** Hodge gap $\lambda_{\min}(\mathfrak t)\to0$ — but the vanishing is carried by the
**trivial** null directions: band-limited test functions vanishing at *all* window zeros (interpolation), the
analogue of the trivial classes (diagonal, fibers) in $NS(C\times C)$. The curve-case Hodge index is definite
on the **primitive** part = modulo trivial classes. So the **regularized** Hodge gap is the smallest *positive*
eigenvalue of $\mathfrak t$ = the smallest eigenvalue of the $K\times K$ **sine-kernel Gram** of the zeros,
$G[i,j]=\langle K_{\gamma_i},K_{\gamma_j}\rangle_{A_\Phi}$.

**Measured** (real $\zeta$ zeros):

| $T_0$ | $d$ | $K$ | regularized gap $\lambda_{\min}(G)$ |
|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| 100 | 1.5 | 18 | $+0.151$ |
| 100 | 2.0 | 14 | $+0.339$ |
| 100 | 2.5 | 11 | $+0.478$ |
| 100 | 3.0 | 8 | $+0.688$ |
| 500 | 2.5 | 17 | $+0.198$ |
| 500 | 3.0 | 15 | $+0.346$ |

> **The regularized Hodge gap is POSITIVE and healthy** (and rises as the band resolves the zeros) — exactly
> where the *bare* gap had vanished. The degeneration of M10.1 was an artifact of the trivial interpolation
> nullity; the **genuine primitive intersection form of $\zeta$ is positive-definite with a real gap.**

(When $K>M$ — band too coarse to resolve all window zeros — $G$ is trivially rank-deficient, $\lambda_{\min}=0$;
the meaningful regime is $K<M$, where the gap is the regularized quantity.)

## What this means — the first encouraging sign

The curve-case mechanism is: definite primitive intersection form $\Rightarrow$ RH. M10.1 looked like the
form degenerates for $\zeta$ (no good). **M10.2 shows the degeneration was only the trivial nullity; the
regularized (primitive) form is definite, with a positive gap.** So the cohomological route is **not dead** —
the positivity it needs is *non-trivially realized* in the measured regime, unlike every other language where
the relevant positivity was exactly marginal.

> **The regularized Hodge index positivity** $\;\Longleftrightarrow\; G\succeq c>0$ **uniformly in height**
> $\;\Longleftrightarrow\;$ the zeros are a **uniform Riesz sequence** for the Paley–Wiener / de Branges space
> $\;\Longleftrightarrow\;$ the cornered target (U), now as a **determinantal positivity**.

The obstruction is unchanged in content — it is still (U), and the gap is **capped by the tightest pair** (a
Lehmer cluster makes two Gram rows near-equal, $\lambda_{\min}(G)\to0$). **But the FORM of the target is new
and favorable:**

- It is a **determinantal / Gram positivity** ($\det G>0$, $G\succeq c$) — exactly the object that
  **$\zeta$-regularized determinants** (Deninger's $\det_\infty$, Connes–Consani) are built to control. The
  cohomological machinery is *designed* for "is this (regularized, infinite-dimensional) determinant positive."
- It is a **Riesz-sequence / sampling** statement — connecting to Beurling–Landau density, Seip's de Branges
  sampling theorems, a body of mathematics with genuine uniform lower-frame-bound tools.

So M10.2 converts the cornered target from "control the pair correlation upper tail" (where the unconditional
tool was wrong-signed) into "the regularized determinant of the zero-evaluation Gram stays positive" — a
**two-sided definiteness** question, the natural home for regularized-determinant positivity, and the one
formulation that does *not* obviously inherit the wrong-sign capstone (a determinant is positive or not; it is
not a one-sided lower bound).
