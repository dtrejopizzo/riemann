## Overview <output>
<conclusion>
In this Step 0.5 calibration, the Li coefficients were the most sensitive observables to the ζ_δ deformation, one Jacobi coefficient (Re(a_4)) showed limited sensitivity only at the strongest tested perturbation (m=20, δ=0.1), and the tested |b_n| and H1 persistence observables were effectively blind for δ ≤ 0.1.
</conclusion>
<methods>
I followed a three-part workflow. First, I validated the zeta-zero engine against Rule R2 by reproducing the first three nontrivial zeta zeros with mpmath.zetazero at 50 dps: γ1=14.1347251417347, γ2=21.0220396387716, γ3=25.0108575801457. Generating 5000 zeros directly at 50 dps with mpmath was computationally prohibitive for the full grid, so I used an external precomputed table (Odlyzko zeros1, downloaded to cache/odlyzko_zeros1.txt) and verified that its first three entries matched the Rule R2 targets to within 1e-6. I then cached the first 5000 ordinates in cache/zeta_zeros_5000.pkl. Second, I implemented an explicit deformation model for ζ_δ over δ ∈ {0, 10^-3, 10^-2, 10^-1} and m ∈ {1, 5, 20}. Because a fully symmetric ±δ deformation caused exact or near-exact cancellation in several observables, I used an explicit count-preserving upper-half-plane replacement model: for the first m positive-imaginary zeros, ρ_n = 1/2 + iγ_n was replaced by ρ_n(δ) = 1/2 + δ + iγ_n; undeformed zeros remained at 1/2 + iγ_n. This preserves the number of upper-half zeros but does not preserve the functional-equation symmetry, so it should be interpreted as a detection-power probe rather than a canonical unique ζ_δ construction. Third, I computed one representative observable from each front. For Front I, I computed the first five Jacobi coefficients a_0...a_4 and b_0...b_4 from a Lanczos/Stieltjes reconstruction applied to the complex upper-half zero list, using a complex diagonal multiplication operator and reporting Re(a_n) and |b_n|. For Front II, I computed Li coefficients λ_1...λ_5 from the zero sum λ_k = Σ_ρ [1 - (1 - 1/ρ)^k], explicitly summing over the full nontrivial zero list including conjugates. For Front III, I computed a single H1 total-persistence functional using ripser on a Takens embedding of unfolded zero-gap sequences with an added real-part coordinate to retain δ sensitivity; total persistence was defined as Σ(death-birth) over H1 intervals. I evaluated every observable on the full (m,δ) grid, saved the numerical results to outputs/step0p5_results.pkl and outputs/detection_power_table.csv, and created the final summary figure outputs/step0p5_detection_power.png. The detection-power criterion was the minimum tested δ for which the relative change exceeded 5%: |obs(δ,m)-obs(0)|/|obs(0)| > 0.05.
</methods>
<results>
Rule R2 zeta validation passed for the first three zeros. Using the Odlyzko table, the first three ordinates were 14.134725142, 21.022039639, and 25.010857580, each within 1e-6 of the required targets. Front II (Li coefficients) showed the strongest and cleanest monotone response. Baseline values at δ=0 were λ_1=0.0228689, λ_2=0.0914384, λ_3=0.2055974, λ_4=0.3651611, λ_5=0.5698718. At m=20 and δ=0.1 these became λ_1=0.0260559, λ_2=0.0977882, λ_3=0.2150639, λ_4=0.3776767, λ_5=0.5853478, corresponding to relative changes of 13.94%, 6.94%, 4.60%, 3.43%, and 2.72%, respectively. Detection thresholds: λ_1 crossed 5% at δ=0.1 for m=5 and m=20; λ_2 crossed 5% at δ=0.1 only for m=20; λ_3, λ_4, and λ_5 remained below 5% for all tested m and δ. Front I split into a weakly sensitive and a blind subset. Re(a_n) increased monotonically with both δ and m. Baseline Re(a_0)=...=Re(a_4)=0.5. At m=20 and δ=0.1, these became Re(a_0)=0.5004, Re(a_1)=0.5034591, Re(a_2)=0.5105713, Re(a_3)=0.5217876, Re(a_4)=0.5361096, i.e. relative changes of 0.08%, 0.69%, 2.11%, 4.36%, and 7.22%. Only Re(a_4) exceeded the 5% threshold, and only at m=20, δ=0.1. The |b_n| coefficients were effectively unchanged: for example |b_0| was 1508.62712532 at baseline and 1508.62712531 at m=20, δ=0.1 (relative change 8.49×10^-12); all |b_n| changes were between about 10^-12 and 10^-9, so every tested |b_n| was blind at δ ≤ 0.1. Front III was also effectively blind under the 5% rule. Baseline H1 total persistence was 8.39854. At δ=0.1 it was 8.37372 for m=1 and m=5 (0.30% relative change) and 8.33158 for m=20 (0.80% relative change). No H1 condition crossed the 5% threshold. The resulting detection-power table was:
- Re(a_4): minimum detectable δ = 0.1 for m=20; blind for m=1 and m=5.
- λ_1: minimum detectable δ = 0.1 for m=5 and m=20; blind for m=1.
- λ_2: minimum detectable δ = 0.1 for m=20 only.
- Re(a_0), Re(a_1), Re(a_2), Re(a_3), all |b_0|...|b_4|, λ_3, λ_4, λ_5, and H1 total persistence: blind at δ ≤ 0.1 for all tested m. All tested sensitive observables changed monotonically with δ over the grid; no non-monotone crossings were observed in the reported quantities.

<challenges>
The main challenge was that the supplied primary dataset was a specification document rather than a numerical zero table, so Step 0.5 required constructing the numerical inputs before analysis. Direct generation of 5000 zeta zeros at 50 dps using mpmath was too slow for the full grid within the available runtime, especially since the cost of zetazero increases with zero index. To avoid fabricating or approximating unsupported data, I switched to an external precomputed source explicitly permitted by Rule R8, downloaded Odlyzko’s zeros1 table, and validated it against the first-three-zero R2 gate. This introduced a precision limitation: the bulk zero list had about 9 decimal digits, not full 50-dps precision. A second challenge was definitional: the protocol states that ζ_δ is formed by displacing zeros off the line, but it does not fully fix the deformation map. A fully symmetric ±δ construction caused cancellation or near-cancellation in several observables, especially some Jacobi summaries, making them artificially blind by construction. I therefore adopted one explicit, count-preserving deformation model and documented it. This means the exact numerical detection thresholds are model-dependent. A third challenge concerned Front III: simple VR persistence on unfolded zeros produced trivial H1=0, consistent with the protocol’s prior expectation that H1 may be negative after unfolding. To obtain a nondegenerate H1 functional, I used a Takens embedding of unfolded zero gaps with an added real-part coordinate. This yielded a stable nonzero statistic, but it remained insensitive under the 5% detection rule. Finally, the full Rule R2 gate also asks for validation of the L_DH off-line zeros, but the present task only required Step 0.5 on ζ_δ and no L_DH numerical data were provided. I therefore could not execute the L_DH half of Rule R2 in this run.
</challenges>
<discussion>
The calibration supports the research hypothesis in a qualified way: some observables are measurably responsive to injected off-line displacement, while others are effectively blind over the tested range. Among the tested summaries, the Li coefficients—especially λ_1 and, more weakly, λ_2—had the best detection power. This is consistent with the idea that Li coefficients are direct zero-location functionals and can react coherently to systematic off-line motion. In contrast, the tested TDA H1 summary and the first five |b_n| Jacobi coefficients carried almost no detection power here, so a future null result from those summaries should be treated as uninformative rather than as evidence of structural similarity or RH-relevance. The Jacobi results are mixed: higher-order Re(a_n) terms showed some sensitivity, culminating in Re(a_4) crossing the 5% threshold only in the strongest tested case, whereas |b_n| was effectively frozen. That pattern suggests that not all low-order reconstruction summaries are equally useful for perturbation detection, and that the choice of scalar summary extracted from the reconstructed operator matters at least as much as the reconstruction itself. Because the deformation model was not uniquely specified by the protocol, these results should be interpreted as a calibration under one explicit perturbation design, not as a universal statement about every possible ζ_δ family. Still, the central practical conclusion is robust: before interpreting a null from Front I or Front III, one should first verify that the exact observable used has nontrivial Step 0.5 power. Under the present implementation, λ_1 is the best candidate among the tested observables for detecting small-to-moderate injected off-line perturbations, while H1 total persistence and |b_n| are poor choices.
</discussion>
