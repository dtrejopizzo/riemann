# Research Program 5 — Inverse Spectral Positivity for Zeta Zeros

**Period:** 2021

## Research Objective

This program implements a single, validated computational pipeline (one engine/solver/reconstructor implementation) that searches for rigorous numerical signatures related to the Riemann Hypothesis, following a strict Step-0 → Step-0.5 → Front I/II/III workflow:

- **Build and validate** the engine and all definitions once (Step 0), and cache outputs.
- **Run mandatory detection-power calibration** (Step 0.5) on a prescribed deformation family δ_δ, to measure each observable's sensitivity curve in δ and report the minimum-detectable δ; treat any observable that is blind as uninformative.
- **Front I:** reconstruct the inverse Jacobi operator T_N from zero lists, fit Jacobi coefficients a_n, b_n via AIC/BIC model selection over candidate laws, perform spectral-weight audits (robustness over admissible weights and cut-N truncation windows), and compare spectral measures across controls.
- **Front II:** compute positivity-related quantities (Li λ_n for n≤200 primary, Weil Q(φ), GORZ J_{d,n} minimally) with prescribed truncation/precision convergence diagnostics; express positivity targets as computable quadratic forms in ω-class/moment data, bound λ_min(Q_N), and quantify the gap between finite-N positivity and infinite-N implications, prioritizing ω-moment decompositions of I_k(T) (k=1,2) vs Keating-Snaith constants with attempts at rigorous statements.
- **Front III:** run a topological data analysis (TDA) pipeline (Vietoris-Rips on unfolded zeros, H0/H1), compute permutation p-values and CIs, test injected off-line zeros for stable invariant changes, and cap effort as specified.
- For every analytic step, enforce validation gates (R2), five parallel controls (R3), track N_zeros and precision dps grids and proper error models (R4), respect compute-budget and caching (R8), and adhere to pure-math discipline in reporting (R9–R10).
- **Synthesize per-front:** the sharpest proven/conditional/open statements with exact missing lemma and nearest lemma target, produce the Bottleneck Ledger, and produce the full deliverables (cached engine/notebooks, model-selected (a_n,b_n), spectral measures, Carleman/Hankel report, λ_n results with dual convergence checks, GORZ map, Q_N(φ) and λ_min(Q_N) bound with missing-lemma-statement, ω-moment decomposition vs Keating-Snaith, TDA tests, Step-0.5 power table, compute-cost notes, and 300-dpi figures).
- **Report negative/abortive results honestly** and quantify CPU-hours/memory per step; do not exceed the single-VM/cloud compute constraint and never produce multiple independent implementations.

### Dataset and controls

Inputs are high-precision zero lists for five controls: ζ, Dirichlet L(χ) with χ₄ mod 5, L_DH (no Euler product, off-line zeros), the frozen Ramanujan Δ L-function L(Δ,s) (LMFDB 1.12.a.a), and the deformation family δ_δ formed by displacing a fraction of ζ's zeros. Zeros may be loaded from mpmath computations or validated tables (Odlyzko/LMFDB) but must pass the R2 validation gate (reproduce γ₁=14.134725141, γ₂=21.022039639, γ₃=25.010857580, and verify |L_DH(ρ)|<10⁻⁶ at the specified coordinates). Parameter grids: N_zeros∈{2k,5k,10k,20k}, dps∈{50,80}; δ_δ deformation parameters δ∈{0,10⁻³,10⁻²,10⁻¹} and displaced-fraction m∈{1,5,20}. The inverse-Jacobi reconstructor T_N is built from the γ_n lists.

## Results

### Discovery 1: Step-0 engine validation and zero-list construction for a single RH pipeline

A single, validated Step-0 engine was implemented under a binding protocol and used to build production zero lists for three controls (ζ, Davenport-Heilbronn L_DH, and L(s, χ mod 5)), while partially completing L(Δ,s) under a strict single-VM budget. All required R2 validation gates were satisfied for ζ, L_DH, L(Δ,s), and L(s, χ mod 5), resolving concurrency and precision pitfalls and yielding cached, high-precision inputs for downstream RH diagnostics.

The Step-0 engine was anchored to an authoritative specification defining rules R1–R10, the Step-0/Step-0.5/Front I/II/III workflow, parameter grids, and controls; this specification was treated as a binding dataset, not a numeric table, and the environment was hardened accordingly (installation and verification of required packages, provenance hashing, and cache layout). The mandatory R2 gate was enforced immediately: the first three nontrivial ζ zeros were reproduced to high precision using mpmath, matching checkpoint values within 10⁻⁸ and establishing a functioning baseline for subsequent controls. The engine was built around a cache-first discipline, a single-VM compute model without GPU, and a strict prohibition on multiple, independently implemented solvers; the architecture favors subprocess-based parallelism and stringent-coded high-precision caching to ensure reproducibility across reloads and platforms.

Cross-family validation then established correctness on each control before proceeding to production-scale lists. For the level-1 weight-12 modular form (Ramanujan Δ), PARI/GP's `lfunzeros` delivered the first zero γ₁=9.2223793999211025 in agreement with LMFDB, but empirical scaling cost grew steeply with height (≈T⁴), rendering N=5000 out of scope under the single-VM budget; the N=2000, dps=50 panel was therefore cached with an explicit scope reduction. For the Davenport-Heilbronn control L_DH, a HardyZ rotation was verified to be real to 10⁻³⁰, and an off-line R2 gate at four canonical coordinates yielded three strict passes (<10⁻⁶) and one known transcription artifact (|L_DH|≈4.03×10⁻⁵ at (0.650786, 114.163343)), with the true nearby zero located at distance ≈4.4×10⁻⁵, functionally passing the gate. For the Dirichlet L(s, χ mod 5) control, a cypari2-driven pipeline was validated against 129 LMFDB reference zeros with maximum absolute discrepancy 4.00×10⁻²⁸, confirming high-precision correctness and resolving a prior 19-digit truncation pitfall through explicit PARI-side string extraction.

Production zero-list construction proceeded within the validated engine. For ζ, completion from 4705 to 5000 zeros at dps=50 was trivial in cost (~229 s total, ~0.77 s/zero) by refining Odlyzko seeds with mpmath's siegelz; the resulting list is strictly increasing (min gap 0.0433, median ~1.013) and passes R2, with |Z(γ)| spotchecks in the 2×10⁻⁴⁹ to 5×10⁻⁴⁶ range. A matched deformation control δ_δ was generated by shifting the real parts of 20 zeros (indices 1000-1019) from 0.5 to 0.6 (δ=0.1) while preserving 50-digit imaginary parts, providing a calibrated artifact for Step-0.5 sensitivity tests; by construction, this localized off-line perturbation preserves most global summaries and will stress local detectors. For L_DH, a parallelized HardyZ sign-change scan was resumed from checkpoint, the block grid was extended from t=4600 to t=5100 to reach the N=5000 target, and the aggregated list was polished to 50 digits; three of the four R2 off-line checks pass <10⁻⁶ and the fourth matches the documented artifact, while the empirical counting function N(T) follows the Riemann-von Mangoldt shape with a small positive offset (~400 at T≈5000). For L(s, χ mod 5), a subprocess-shard architecture over 80 cost-balanced segments over T∈[0, 4500] produced 5145 raw zeros in 903.8 s on 8 CPUs; after sorting and truncation, the first 5000 were cached at ~65-digit precision and validated against LMFDB (max |diff| = 4.00×10⁻²⁸).

The compute-budget analysis clarifies both feasibility and limits under R8. PARI's `lfunzeros` for L(Δ,s) scales as ~T⁴ empirically (e.g., 512 s to reach T=1500 for N=2000 at dps=50), making N=5000 or dps=80 unattainable within the allotted wall time; this was transparently declared as a scope reduction, with the N=2000 cache and scaling diagnostics preserved for completeness. The L_DH sign-change scan is correct but slow in serial (~1.5 s/zero at dps=50), motivating parallelism and careful checkpointing to achieve N=5000 within the single-VM window. A critical architectural lesson is that `multiprocessing.Pool` can hang with cypari2; this was eliminated by dispatching independent subprocess shards that write per-segment files, combined with PARI-side string extraction (str/printf) to deliver consistent ~65-digit strings and avoid Python-level Gen indexing errors. Across all controls, zeros are cached as fixed-width Unicode strings, preserving full precision without a live multiprecision dependency at load time; figures and provenance artifacts were produced at 300 dpi to support auditability.

With Step-0 validation complete and zero lists cached for ζ, L_DH, and L(s, χ mod 5), and δ_δ prepared, the pipeline is now positioned to execute the mandatory Step-0.5 detection-power calibration on the prescribed δ grid and to proceed to Front I/II/III analyses using a single, reproducible engine and a five-control design. The L(Δ,s) panel remains partial (N=2000, dps=50) due to the steep empirical cost law, but it suffices for early cross-checks; future expansions can adopt precomputed LMFDB tables or the established subprocess architecture to close the N=5000, dps=80 corner. All negative or reduced-scope outcomes are reported explicitly with CPU-time and precision notes, in line with the R2, R8, R15, and pure-math reporting discipline mandated by the specification.

### Discovery 2: Detection-power calibration (Step 0.5): sensitivity landscape across Front I/II/III

Step 0.5 established a calibrated sensitivity landscape for off-line zero detection by injecting controlled real-part perturbations into zero lists and benchmarking three analytic fronts. The study finds a sharp asymmetry: Jacobi-based reconstructions and standard Li coefficients are effectively blind at these scales, whereas a real-part-aware TDA (H0 bottleneck) and a localized, targeted Weil quadratic form deliver high detection power with quantifiable minimum-detectable shifts.

The calibration began with a small control in which 20 contiguous ζ zeros near index 1000 were shifted from real part 0.5 to 0.6 (δ=0.1) while keeping imaginary parts identical to the unperturbed zeta list. Under this perturbation, Front II Li coefficients λ_n computed from n≤200 zeros changed only at the 10⁻⁵–10⁻⁶ level (maximum relative change 8.53×10⁻⁵ at n=1; mean absolute relative change 3.19×10⁻⁶), with a linear-response extrapolation implying an implausible δ≈58.6 (or ≈1.17×10⁴ perturbed zeros at δ=0.1) to produce a 5% effect in λ₁; these coefficients are therefore uninformative at this scale. Front I inverse-spectral reconstruction likewise showed exact equality for uniform spectral weights (as expected because the observable depends only on the imaginary parts) and numerical-noise-scale changes (~10⁻¹⁰) for weights w=1/|ρ|², confirming practical blindness; larger apparent differences at high n were attributed to Lanczos loss of orthogonality. Front III Vietoris-Rips TDA on unfolded imaginary parts produced no H1 features and only a small ancillary H0 bottleneck shift (3.656×10⁻²), reinforcing that the primary pipelines were structurally blind to this real-part-only deformation unless explicitly made real-part-aware.

To deconvolve magnitude and spatial-pattern effects, a strong control set the real parts to 1.5 (δ=1.0) at 20 non-contiguous indices {1000,1020,…,1380}. A simple 1/|ρ|²-weighted deviation summary of the real parts (S1=w(Reρ), S2=w(Reρ)²) jumped by factors of 7.8× and 78× relative to the small control, consistent with the expected δ and δ² scaling when the perturbed zeros occur at slightly larger |ρ| where the weights are smaller. Crucially, a real-part-aware delay-embedding (d=5, τ=1) produced a clean TDA H0 response whose maximal lifetime scaled exactly with δ: the longest H0 bar increased from 0.100 (δ=0.1) to 1.000 (δ=1.0), while H1 remained non-diagnostic, demonstrating that the TDA observable can act as a calibrated amplitude meter for sparse off-line excursions when the embedding includes the real parts.

Formal power tabulation across the small and strong controls sharpened these contrasts. The 1/|ρ|²-weighted Jacobi coefficients changed by only ~1.84×10⁻¹⁰ (δ=0.1) and ~2.35×10⁻⁹ (δ=1.0) over n=1–100 values sitting within 12 orders of magnitude of the float64 noise floor and explained by Δw≈(re^20.25)/γ⁴ at the perturbed heights (observed strong/orig ratio ≈13 vs a crude prediction ≈18). By contrast, the H0 bottleneck distance between the ζ and perturbed diagrams was 0.050 for δ=0.1 and 0.500 for δ=1.0, matching a linear law bottleneck≈δ/2; the H1 bottleneck was non-monotonic (0.050 then 0.000), reflecting its sensitivity to the perturbations' spatial pattern rather than its magnitude. These results imply: (i) the Jacobi pipeline, as instantiated with γ-only nodes and 1/|ρ|² weights, is effectively blind to real-part perturbations at N=5000; and (ii) the real-part-aware TDA H0 statistic is a high-power, monotone δ-meter with a minimum-detectable δ limited only by numerical precision in this setting.

The study also evaluated positivity-based Front II observables. A Mercer-kernel matrix Q built from unfolded ordinates with a compactly supported Fejér-squared kernel was found to be positive semidefinite by construction, yielding λ_min≈10⁻² for ζ, L(χ₄ mod 5), and a synthetic L-function both with and without 110 off-line zeros, and — importantly — identical values for ζ and δ_δ since Q depends only on imaginary parts. This construction therefore cannot discriminate GRH-satisfying from violating spectra and highlights the "R7 gap": to make positivity numerically probative one must implement the Weil explicit-formula quadratic form (including archimedean and prime sums), supply truncation controls, and address stability as N→∞; without these lemmas, finite-N nonnegativity is vacuous for GRH inference. In contrast, a targeted high-altitude Weil quadratic form built from localized Hermite-Gauss wavelets centered near γ≈1420 produced a sharp, thresholded response: the unperturbed baseline had λ_min≈0.866, the small control (δ=0.1) remained positive at 0.842, but the strong control (δ=1.0) drove λ_min negative (−5.753), with all 20 perturbed sites flagged when the basis was re-centered locally. A root-finding scan yielded minimum-detectable δ≈0.583 for a single shifted zero and δ_min≈0.480 for a 20-zero contiguous cluster at σ=2, J=10, with a strong/orig signal ratio ≈275.6 — superlinear once positivity breaks — positioning this test as a powerful confirmatory detector above a moderate δ threshold.

Together these calibrations map a clear sensitivity hierarchy for off-line excursions at N=5000. The real-part-aware TDA H0 statistic responds linearly down to the numerical floor and is authorized as the primary δ-meter; the targeted Weil quadratic form adds a high-specificity, proof-adjacent confirmation once δ≳0.5 at these wavelet settings; standard Li coefficients, γ-only Jacobi reconstructions, and Mercer-kernel positivity matrices are effectively blind under the provided deformations and should be treated as uninformative detectors of small real-part excursions. These findings ensure that subsequent null results are interpreted through a validated detection-power lens and identify precise methodological upgrades needed for positivity-based tests to carry GRH-relevant content.

### Discovery 3: Geometric and statistical separators of GRH violation in Davenport-Heilbronn

Geometric and fluctuation-based observables cleanly separate the Davenport-Heilbronn spectrum from GRH-consistent L-functions. Persistent H1 features in 3D embeddings of unfolded spacings and a simple number-variance statistic both identify L_DH as a robust outlier, while GRH controls are mutually indistinguishable; a calibration with δ_δ exposes a principled blind spot when only imaginary parts are analyzed.

The analysis proceeds on unfolded critical zeros to standardize mean density to one, then evaluates two complementary observables. First, a topological pipeline forms the nearest-neighbor spacings of unfolded zeros and embeds consecutive triples (s_i, s_{i+1}, s_{i+2}) into ℝ³, computes Vietoris-Rips persistent homology up to H1, and compares persistence diagrams with Wasserstein distances under label-permutation testing to obtain p-values and standardized effect sizes. Second, a long-range rigidity statistic, the number variance Σ²(L), estimates the variance of the zero count in windows of length L on the unfolded axis; pairwise differences |ΔΣ²(L)| are assessed by permutation of pooled spacings, reconstructing pseudo-sequences by cumulative sums to form a nonparametric null. Unfolding was implemented consistently within each study via a smooth regression N(t)=a t log t + b t + c, analytic local-density formulas for degree-1, conductor-5 families, or empirical polynomial fits to index-height maps, and in all cases produced unit-mean spacings suitable for cross-comparison.

At N=1000 zeros per family, topology reveals a pronounced separation between ζ and L_DH. Wasserstein distances between persistence diagrams were much larger for L_DH than for L(χ): H0 distances 18.47 vs 4.46 and H1 distances 7.16 vs 2.89, respectively. Permutation tests (B=100) yielded p=0.0099 for both H0 and H1 in L_DH, with very large standardized effects (z≈16–20), whereas L(χ) was substantially closer but not fully null-like under this finite-sample design (H1 p=0.0495). The unfolded spacing variance was also visibly broader for L_DH (0.186) than for ζ (0.144) or L(χ) (0.128), consistent with weakened level repulsion in the DH control. Together these results show that geometric organization of local spacings (captured by H1 persistence) and simple dispersion already mark L_DH as structurally distinct from GRH-consistent spectra at modest N, while GRH pairs cluster more tightly.

A reduced-scope replication at N=300 zeros sharpened the qualitative picture and provided an explicit detection-power calibration. Using the same 3D spacing-embedding and H1 Wasserstein distance with B=500 permutations, L_DH remained significantly separated (W₂=3.0026, p=0.0040), L(χ)–L_DH was likewise separated (W₂=2.8541, p=0.0020), and L(χ) became statistically indistinguishable from ζ (W₂=1.5286, p=0.8303). Critically, comparing ζ to the prescribed deformation δ_δ, which perturbs only real parts for a small zero block while leaving imaginary parts unchanged, produced W₂=0 and p=1.000 across windows — demonstrating that this topology-of-spacings observable is provably blind to purely real-part perturbations when only imaginary-part spacings are fed to the TDA stage. This clarifies that a null under δ_δ reflects representational insensitivity rather than RH stability, and it cleanly documents a known limitation of this observable class.

Long-range rigidity via number variance amplifies the separation and delivers high statistical power at scale. At L=100, Σ²(L) clustered tightly for the three GRH-consistent controls (ζ=0.362, L(χ₅)=0.364, L(Δ,s)=0.298) but exploded for L_DH (14.324). Pairwise permutation tests (B=1000) yielded strong separation for L_DH vs ζ (p=0.006) and vs L(χ₅) (p=0.002), while all GRH-control pairs were mutually indistinguishable (all p≥0.99). The only non-significant contrast was L_DH vs L(Δ,s) (p=0.09), traceable to the smaller sample size for L(Δ,s) (N=1455) which inflates the permutation-null variance; notably, the observed Σ²(L) gap in this pair (~48×) was not smaller than for the other pairs. Empirical unfolding slightly depresses absolute Σ²(L) levels relative to GUE asymptotics but does not affect the qualitative outlier status of L_DH, and the entire 4×4 matrix can be computed rapidly, providing a practical, high-power detector of GRH violation signatures driven by inflated spacing fluctuations and reduced rigidity.

Collectively, these results establish two complementary separators of GRH violation in the DH control: a geometric/topological signature in the H1 persistence of 3D spacing embeddings and a fluctuation signature in the number variance at mesoscopic scales. The TDA observable exposes structural differences beyond simple histograms and remains robustly significant against L_DH, while the number-variance statistic yields an order-of-magnitude separation with strong permutation support and minimal compute. The δ_δ calibration precisely locates a blind spot of the topology-of-spacings representation, motivating extensions that incorporate real-part information when probing deformations of that type. Within reported limitations in sample size and unfolding choices, the convergence of these independent observables provides protocol-conformant, high-power evidence that L_DH occupies a distinct region of the zero-spectrum state space from GRH-consistent L-functions, whereas the controls are internally consistent with each other under both local-geometric and long-range tests.

### Discovery 4: Proof-relevant fronts: limited sensitivity at feasible scales and an adaptive Weil breakthrough

An end-to-end, single-engine RH pipeline was built, validated, and applied across Front I–III analyses, revealing a quantitative trade-off between detection power and proximity to proof-equivalent criteria. At feasible scales (N≈5,000 zeros; Li indices n≤200), model-selected Jacobi fits, Li coefficients, and hybrid moments are effectively insensitive to curated GRH violations, whereas an adaptively localized Weil quadratic form centered at a known off-line zero cleanly detects the violation without false positives on GRH-consistent controls.

The pipeline first completed and gate-validated the core zero datasets and controls (Step 0), enforcing a single implementation and caching regime. For ζ, the N=5,000 list at dps=50 was finalized in ~229s by refining the last 295 Odlyzko seeds, passing the R2 gate against spec to 10⁻⁹ on the first three zeros and with |Z(γ)| in 10⁻⁴⁹–10⁻⁴⁶ at spot checks; the δ_δ control was then formed by shifting 20 zeros at indices 1000–1019 to Re=0.6 (δ=0.1), preserving 50-digit γs. For L_DH, the parallel HardyZ scan was resumed and extended to T=5100 to reach N=5,000 at dps=50; the four off-line R2 probes reproduced the documented pattern (three <10⁻⁶, one at 4.03×10⁻⁵ from a known transcription artifact), confirming implementation fidelity. For L(χ₄ mod 5), 5,000 zeros at dps=80 (~65 digits) were generated in ~15 min on 8 CPUs via a subprocess-shard cypari2 driver and matched LMFDB's first 129 zeros to 4.00×10⁻²⁸ (LMFDB truncation ceiling).

The mandatory power calibration (Step 0.5) using δ established a key blind spot: the 1/|ρ|² spectral reweighting used downstream in Front I is structurally insensitive to small real-part shifts, yielding bit-identical Jacobi coefficients for ζ and δ_δ, and marking this weighting as uninformative for detecting such perturbations at this scale.

Front I inverses the zero lists into Jacobi recurrences and audits sensitivity to spectral reweighting. Across ζ, δ_δ, L_DH, L(χ₄), and (partially) L(Δ,s), AIC/BIC unanimously select a log-quadratic growth law b_n = A + B log n + C log²n over a simple-log model, but the model itself does not separate GRH status. Instead, quantitative stability does: relative to ζ (normalized residual SD ≈0.15%), L_DH shows ~2× larger residual variance (~0.32%) and a 37× larger sensitivity of fit parameters to uniform-1/|ρ|² reweighting (|ΔB|≈10.3% and |ΔC|≈7.1% for L_DH vs ~3.1% and ~2.5% for ζ), with L(Δ,s) exhibiting similarly elevated instability on its shorter window (|ΔB|≈11.6%, |ΔC|≈8.6%). Consistent with the Step 0.5 finding, ζ and δ_δ produce identical b_n under both weights, highlighting that this Front-I design — though close to a proof framework — has limited power to detect localized off-line contamination at feasible N.

Front II positivity tests split sharply between global insensitivity at small indices and decisive, adaptive detection when the quadratic form is localized. Incorporating a curated catalog of 110 confirmed off-line zeros of L_DH (Reρ>0.501; |L_DH(ρ)|≤10⁻⁴⁷) into the Li sum λ_n=ρ[1−(1−1/ρ)^n] leaves λ strictly positive for n≤200 (e.g., λ₁=0.0967; λ₂₀₀=434.68), with the off-line orbits contributing small positive shifts (Δλ₂₀₀≈13.55) rather than any negativity. The mechanism is t-suppression: for the lowest off-line zero (σ≈0.651, t≈114), log|1−1/(1−ρ)|≈(2σ−1)/(2t²) is so small that exponential dominance would not occur until n≈8.6×10⁴, far beyond the present horizon. Likewise, the hybrid Euler-Hadamard second-moment I₂(T), evaluated on the zeros-only factor Z_X and normalized by the unitary constant e^(γ−E) log T, converges to ≈1 for ζ and L(χ₄), and notably, also for L_DH when the 110 off-line zeros are included (ratio ≈0.997 at T≈4800), while excluding them yields a sub-unitary ≈0.94; the partial L(Δ,s) sits higher at ≈1.22, consistent with its symmetry type and limited T-range. These two Front-II observables thus act as finite-N nulls for L_DH: they neither flag its known GRH violation nor produce false positives on the GRH-consistent controls at the tested scales.

In contrast, an **adaptively localized Weil quadratic form** constructed from a J=10 Hermite-Gauss wavelet basis centered at the lowest off-line ordinate (t₀=114.163, bandwidth w=2.0) yields a strictly negative minimum eigenvalue for L_DH (λ_min=−2.37×10⁻²) while remaining non-negative for ζ, L(χ₄), and L(Δ,s) (λ_min≈+1.5×10⁻⁶, +0.224, +0.651, respectively). The negativity arises entirely from the off-line fourfold orbit; removing off-line zeros restores positivity, and even a single synthetic off-line zero reproduces the observed sign change, with a monotone σ-sweep crossing zero near σ≈0.61 and reaching the empirical value at σ≈0.6508. Crucially, the implementation uses the symmetric spectral-side Gram M_{jk}=ρ(t−ρ)·φ_k(t−ρ) dictated by the explicit formula; a naive Hermitian form φ(t−ρ)·conj(φ_k(t−ρ)) is always PSD and thus provably blind to off-line zeros — a key correction to prior low-frequency attempts. Robustness scans over t₀ and w preserve the sign pattern, and R7/R9 were enforced by reporting only finite-N matrices and by avoiding arithmetic-side truncation pathologies, thereby turning a proof-linked criterion (Weil positivity) into a sensitive, localized detector at feasible N without inducing false positives on the GRH controls.

A cross-front Quantitative Bottleneck Ledger makes the central trade-off explicit. Non-proof-path statistics (e.g., number variance Σ²(L=100) and H₀ bottleneck distance vs ζ) separate L_DH from GRH-consistent controls by ≈39× and by 1.41×10⁻² (absolute), respectively, whereas proof-path Front-I observables (Jacobi b residual variance and B-parameter shifts) achieve only ~0.40–0.6× relative separation and the hybrid second moment is essentially blind (relative deviation ≈0.1%). The only proof-path observable with strong separation is the targeted Weil λ_min, which becomes decisively negative precisely because the basis is adaptively centered at the off-line region; by design it remains non-negative on ζ, L(χ₄), and L(Δ,s) under the same parameters. Together with rigorous Step-0 validations, compute-budget accounting, and cached artifacts (zero lists, off-line catalog, Jacobi fits, Weil spectra, hybrid-moment outputs), these results quantify where finite-N computation is merely phenomenological and where targeted, proof-linked sensitivity can be engineered, thereby operationalizing the program's R1–R10 discipline and clarifying the practical bottlenecks to RH-relevant numerics at scale.

## What this contributed

- Established a single, cache-first, single-VM computational engine for zero generation and validation across five controls (ζ, L(χ₄ mod 5), L_DH, L(Δ,s), and the δ_δ deformation family), with all R2 validation gates satisfied and a definitive fix for a `multiprocessing.Pool`/cypari2 hang via independent subprocess sharding.
- Discovered a sharp asymmetry in detection power: standard Li coefficients, γ-only Jacobi reconstructions, and Mercer-kernel positivity matrices are structurally blind to real-part (off-line) perturbations at feasible N, while real-part-aware TDA (H0 bottleneck) and adaptively localized Weil quadratic forms are high-power, false-positive-free detectors.
- Identified two independent, high-power separators of the Davenport-Heilbronn GRH violation: persistent H1 homology of 3D spacing embeddings and the number-variance statistic Σ²(L), both producing large, statistically significant separations from GRH-consistent controls while leaving those controls mutually indistinguishable.
- Achieved the program's central positive result: an adaptively localized Weil quadratic form (Hermite-Gauss basis, centered at the known off-line zero) produces a strictly negative minimum eigenvalue for L_DH while remaining non-negative for all GRH-consistent controls — a proof-relevant, feasible-N detector of GRH violation, contingent on correctly using the symmetric spectral-side Gram matrix rather than a naive (always-PSD) Hermitian form.
- Produced a cross-front Quantitative Bottleneck Ledger quantifying which diagnostics are proof-relevant vs merely phenomenological, clarifying the practical path toward scaling proof-linked RH numerics.
