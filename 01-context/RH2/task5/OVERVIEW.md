## Overview **HYPOTHESIS REJECTED:** The upper envelope of composite coherence R_comp(t;N) does NOT distinguish between ζ(s) and the anomalous functions L_DH(s) and L(s,λ) in the manner hypothesized. **KEY FINDINGS:** 1. **All functions show systematic decay with N:** - ζ(s): sup_t R_comp ~ 11755.6 / log(N)^5.40 (R² = 0.9990) - L_DH(s): sup_t R_comp ~ 12346.0 / log(N)^5.28 (R² = 0.9908) - L(s,λ): sup_t R_comp ~ 1652.0 / log(N)^4.40 (R² = 0.9945) 2. **None of the functions approach 1:** At N = 10^6, the supremum values are: - ζ(s): 0.007 (99.9th percentile) - L_DH(s): 0.009 (99.9th percentile) - L(s,λ): 0.013 (99.9th percentile) 3. **Decay exponents are statistically similar:** - ζ vs L_DH: α_ζ - α_DH = 0.12 ± 0.41 (p = 0.80, not significant) - ζ vs L(s,λ): α_ζ - α_λ = 1.00 ± 0.27 (p = 0.03, weakly significant) - L_DH vs L(s,λ): α_DH - α_λ = 0.88 ± 0.45 (p = 0.15, not significant) 4. **The hypothesized model sup_t R_comp ≈ 1 - c/log(N) is incorrect:** The data are better fit by a power-law decay model sup_t R_comp ~ b/log(N)^α with α ≈ 4-5 for all functions. **INTERPRETATION:** The composite coherence metric R_comp exhibits universal destructive interference across all tested functions, consistent with Discovery 2 which found that "all four tested Dirichlet-series classes exhibit a common regime of destructive interference across squarefree ω-classes." This universal behavior makes R_comp alone insufficient for discriminating between resonance-suppressing (ζ) and anomalous (L_DH, L(s,λ)) functions. The hypothesis that anomalous functions would maintain sup_t R_comp ≈ 1 is not supported by the data. Instead, all functions show rapid decay of composite coherence with increasing N, suggesting that composite terms do not maintain coherent phase alignment across the t-range tested, regardless of whether the function has off-line zeros. **CONCLUSION:** While ζ(s) does show slightly stronger decay (α = 5.40 vs 5.28 for L_DH and 4.40 for L(s,λ)), this difference is not statistically robust except for the comparison with L(s,λ). The composite coherence supremum does not provide the clear quantitative discrimination predicted by the hypothesis. This finding suggests that either: (a) R_comp is not the appropriate observable for detecting resonance suppression in this context, or (b) The resonance suppression mechanism operates differently than hypothesized. The empirical estimate for a decay constant c in the originally proposed model sup_t R_comp ≈ 1 - c/log(N) cannot be provided because the data do not follow this functional form. Instead, the best-fit model is sup_t R_comp ~ b/log(N)^α with b ≈ 11756 and α ≈ 5.40 for ζ(s). --- **DISCRETIONARY DECISIONS:** • Used 1000 t-points instead of 5000 for computational efficiency while maintaining statistical robustness (reduced from 5000 to stay within runtime limits)

• Applied non-parametric model fitting (curve_fit with least squares) for parameter estimation
• Used t-range [1000, 20000] as specified in research objective
