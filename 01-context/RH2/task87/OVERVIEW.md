## Overview <output>
<conclusion>
The S_k covariance structure reveals three distinct dynamical regimes (progressive cancellation, stable cancellation, and cancellation decay) but does not universally classify L-functions by RH status, as the RH-violating Davenport-Heilbronn function exhibits behavior similar to RH-satisfying functions.
</conclusion> <methods>
Analysis was performed using numerical computation of Dirichlet partial sums S_k(t;N) = Σ_{ω(n)=k, n≤N} a_n(F)/n^(1/2+it) for k=1..6 across all eight L-function classes at N=10^5 and N=10^6 over t∈[10000, 20000] with 2000 equally-spaced points. Pre-computation of the omega(n) (number of distinct prime factors) and Omega(n) (total prime factors) arrays up to n=10^6 enabled efficient classification of integers by their prime factorization structure. Arithmetic coefficients a_n were implemented as: unity for ζ(s), Legendre symbols (n/5) for L(χ₄,s), complex character exp(2πi·ind_2(n)/4) mod 5 for L(χ_complex,s), random ±1 at primes extended multiplicatively for random multiplicative, cos(2π log n) periodic approximation for Davenport-Heilbronn (using the documented workaround due to canonical implementation validation failures), λ(n)=(-1)^Ω(n) for Liouville, μ(n) for Möbius, and completely random ±1 for fully random functions. For each function and N value, the signed covariance matrix Cov(Re(S_j), Re(S_k)) was computed using numpy's cov function on the time series data. Classification metrics were defined as: CS_N (Cancellation Strength) = sum of all negative off-diagonal covariance matrix elements at N=10^6, and CG_N (Cancellation Growth) = 100×(sum_offdiag_N2 - sum_offdiag_N1)/|sum_offdiag_N1| representing percentage change from N=10^5 to N=10^6. Statistical separation between RH-satisfying (F1, F2, F3) and other functions was assessed using Mann-Whitney U tests (non-parametric, appropriate for small sample sizes) and Cohen's d effect sizes with pooled standard deviation estimates. Cluster analysis employed Euclidean distances in the (CS_N, CG_N/100) feature space, with CG_N scaled to comparable magnitude.
</methods> <results>
Classification metrics for all 8 functions (sorted by CS_N): F1 (Riemann ζ): CS_N=-2.146, CG_N=-48.2%; F3 (L(χ) complex mod 5): CS_N=-1.392, CG_N=-73.5%; F6 (Liouville): CS_N=-1.201, CG_N=-1.5%; F5 (Davenport-Heilbronn): CS_N=-1.016, CG_N=-58.8%; F2 (L(χ₄) real mod 5): CS_N=-0.912, CG_N=-141.1%; F4 (Random multiplicative): CS_N=-0.698, CG_N=-28.9%; F7 (Möbius): CS_N=-0.481, CG_N=+251.8%; F8 (Fully random): CS_N=-0.284, CG_N=-119.7%. RH-satisfying cluster (F1, F2, F3): mean CS_N=-1.483±0.622, mean CG_N=-87.6±48.0%, compact with mean pairwise distance=1.06, maximum intra-group distance=1.54. Non-RH functions: mean CS_N=-0.736±0.376, mean CG_N=+8.6±142.9%. Mann-Whitney U tests yielded p=0.143 for CS_N separation and p=0.250 for CG_N separation (both non-significant at α=0.05 due to small sample sizes: n_RH=3, n_other=5). Effect sizes were large: Cohen's d=1.74 for CS_N and d=1.02 for CG_N, indicating CS_N provides stronger discriminative power. Three distinct dynamical regimes emerged: (1) Progressive cancellation (CG_N < -25%): F1, F2, F3, F5, F8 with mean CG_N=-78.4%; (2) Stable cancellation (|CG_N| < 25%): F6 alone with CG_N=-1.5%; (3) Cancellation decay (CG_N > 25%): F7 alone with CG_N=+251.8%. The sum of negative off-diagonal terms increased in magnitude from N=10^5 to N=10^6 by: F1: -0.737, F2: -0.536, F3: -0.592 (RH-satisfying); F5: -0.393 (RH-violating); F6: -0.087 (stable); F7: -0.153 (unique positive CG_N despite negative CS_N growth). Critical finding: L_DH (F5, RH-violating) exhibits CG_N=-58.8%, falling within the RH-satisfying range [-141.1%, -48.2%], with mean distance to RH centroid of 0.79 compared to mean RH intra-cluster distance of 1.06. F6 (Liouville) shows uniquely stable covariance structure with near-zero growth, consistent with documented "stable weak-cancellation pattern". F7 (Möbius) displays anomalous positive growth in total off-diagonal sum despite strengthening negative terms.
</results> <challenges>
The primary challenge was the documented failure of canonical L_DH validation (|L_DH(ρ)| >> 10^-6 at known zeros), requiring use of the "historical" real-valued periodic approximation L_DH(n) ≈ cos(2π log n) as a workaround, which may affect quantitative accuracy for F5 results. Computational constraints limited analysis to N≤10^6 with 2000 t-points, restricting statistical power for trend analysis; the Mann-Whitney U tests failed to achieve significance (p>0.14) despite large effect sizes due to small group sizes (n=3 RH-satisfying vs n=5 others). The random multiplicative function (F4) required complex implementation of multiplicative extension from random prime coefficients, with potential for subtle errors in the recursive factorization logic. Runtime for N=10^6 computation approached ~600 seconds total across all functions, near the documented constraint limits. The interpretation of CG_N as "cancellation growth" is complicated by the fact that it measures percentage change in the sum of ALL off-diagonal terms (which can be positive or negative), while CS_N measures only negative terms; this created asymmetry where F7 showed increasing negative-term magnitude but positive CG_N due to even larger increase in positive terms. The small-sample regime (only 8 functions total, 3 with confirmed RH status) fundamentally limits the statistical conclusiveness of classification performance.
</challenges> <discussion>
The analysis reveals that N-dependent destructive interference dynamics, quantified through S_k covariance structure evolution, capture mechanistically distinct behaviors but do not align perfectly with Riemann Hypothesis status. The hypothesis that RH-satisfying functions show strong cancellation growth while RH-violating functions show stable patterns is partially supported: all RH-satisfying functions (ζ, L(χ₄), L(χ_complex)) do exhibit progressive strengthening of negative covariance terms (CG_N ranging from -48% to -141%), and Liouville (known for anomalous resonance) does show remarkable stability (CG_N=-1.5%). However, the critical failure is that L_DH, the only confirmed RH-violating function in the dataset, behaves similarly to RH-satisfying functions with CG_N=-58.8%, placing it firmly within their dynamical regime rather than forming a distinct cluster. This finding has important theoretical implications: the S_k decomposition reveals interference structure at the level of prime factorization classes, but the specific pattern of this interference may be determined by factors beyond RH satisfaction alone. The strong cancellation growth in both ζ (RH-satisfying) and L_DH (RH-violating) suggests that this dynamical signature may relate to analytic properties shared by both (e.g., both are degree-1 L-functions with Euler products, both satisfy functional equations) rather than zero distribution per se. The uniquely stable behavior of Liouville aligns with previous findings of its "stable weak-cancellation pattern" documented in the dataset description, suggesting the Liouville function's unusual multiplicative structure λ(n)=(-1)^Ω(n) creates persistent phase relationships that resist N-dependent evolution. Möbius function's anomalous positive CG_N (+251.8%) represents a third dynamical class entirely, where cancellation weakens with increasing N despite absolute magnitude of negative terms still growing. This may reflect the increasing sparsity of μ(n)≠0 as N grows (density ~6/π² → 0), fundamentally altering correlation structure. The method's discriminative power, while substantial (Cohen's d > 1.0 for both metrics), is inferior to a universal RH classifier but superior to the documented R_comp/CAS approach for mechanistic insight. CS_N provides better separation than CG_N (d=1.74 vs d=1.02), suggesting that absolute cancellation strength at large N is more informative than growth rates, though neither achieves the hoped-for clean dichotomy between RH-satisfying and RH-violating functions.
