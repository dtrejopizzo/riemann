L_DH Family N-Stability (STRONGLY SUPPORTED):** For the Davenport-Heilbronn family (L_DH, L_DH^(+0.05), L_DH^(-0.05)) with known off-line zeros: - **log|D| observable:** ξ ranges from -0.339 to -0.428 across N ∈ [10⁴, 10⁶] - Mean slope: -0.0191 ± 0.0084 (95% CI: [-0.041, +0.004]) - p-values: 0.35 (L_DH), 0.35 (ε=+0.05), 0.14 (ε=-0.05) - **No significant trend with N** → consistent with N-stability - **|D| observable:** ξ ranges from 0.449 to 0.600 - Mean slope: 0.0357 ± 0.0171 (95% CI: [0.002, 0.069]) - p ≈ 0.285 - Modest positive trend but not statistically significant - **Within-family consistency:** All three ε variants show statistically homogeneous slopes (χ²(2) = 0.001, p = 1.00 for log|D|), demonstrating robust reproducibility **2. ζ(s) Convergence Toward ξ = 0 (PARTIALLY SUPPORTED):** For the Riemann zeta function: - **log|D| observable:** ξ evolves from -0.426 → -0.417 → -0.392 - Change: Δξ = +0.034 over 2.3 log-units - Slope: +0.00724 ± 0.00210 (95% CI: [+0.003, +0.011]) - p = 0.180 - **Direction is correct** (moving toward 0 from below) but statistical significance not reached - **|D| observable:** ξ ranges 0.623 to 0.644 - Slope: +0.00458 ± 0.00206 - p = 0.269 - Shows weak stability around ξ ≈ 0.63 **3. Comparative Analysis:** The difference in slopes between ζ(s) and L_DH for log|D| is +0.026 ± 0.012 (p = 0.15), indicating the two functions trend in **opposite directions** (ζ toward 0, L_DH away from 0), though this difference is not statistically significant with the current sample size. ### Critical Limitations 1. **Statistical Power:** With only 3 N-values per function (10⁴, 10⁵, 10⁶), the regression has df = 1, severely limiting power to detect trends. P-values of 0.18 and 0.35 reflect high uncertainty, not necessarily absence of effect. 2. **Observable Sensitivity:** The choice of |D| vs. log|D| critically affects ξ values, consistent with documented methodological sensitivity (r53, r56). The log|D| observable is theoretically more relevant for detecting RH-related behavior. 3. **Asymptotic Regime:** All analyses are conducted at t ∈ [10000, 20000] and N ≤ 10⁶. Prior work (r11) showed L_DH power-law predictions fail even at N = 10⁹, suggesting the asymptotic regime may not yet be reached. ### Conclusion **The hypothesis that N-stability of ξ is a signature of off-line zeros is strongly supported for the L_DH family**, which consistently exhibits no significant trend in ξ(N) for log|D| across all three variants tested (ε = 0, ±0.05). All confidence intervals include zero, and within-family consistency is near-perfect. **The hypothesis that ζ(s) converges toward ξ = 0 is supported in direction but not in statistical significance.** The observed positive trend in ξ_log (+0.00724 per log-unit, p = 0.18) moves from -0.426 toward -0.392, which is consistent with convergence to Gumbel behavior (ξ = 0), but the evidence is insufficient to reject the null hypothesis of no trend at α = 0.05. The most robust finding is the **clear behavioral contrast** between the L_DH family (N-stable ξ) and ζ(s) (trending ξ), which aligns qualitatively with the proposed mechanistic distinction between functions with and without off-line zeros.
