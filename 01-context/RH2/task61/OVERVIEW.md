## Overview ## ANALYSIS RESULTS: L-KURTOSIS VS GEV SHAPE PARAMETER CORRELATION ### Main Findings The strong negative correlation between bulk tail shape and extreme value statistics is a **robust phenomenon** that persists when using L-kurtosis instead of standard excess kurtosis. However, the correlation is slightly weaker with L-kurtosis. **Quantitative Results:**
- **L-kurtosis (τ₄) vs ξ**: Spearman ρ = -0.786, p = 0.0208 (statistically significant at α = 0.05)
- **Standard kurtosis vs ξ**: Spearman ρ = -0.810, p = 0.0149 (statistically significant at α = 0.05)
- **Difference**: Δρ = +0.024 (L-kurtosis yields slightly weaker correlation) **F8 (Fully Random) Outlier Status Confirmed:**
- L-kurtosis τ₄ = 0.155 (highest among all 8 functions, rank 1/8)
- Standard kurtosis = 2.419 (highest among all 8 functions, rank 1/8)
- GEV ξ = -1.370 (most negative, rank 8/8, indicating bounded distribution) As predicted by the hypothesis, F8 exhibits both **high L-kurtosis** and **strongly negative ξ**, maintaining its outlier status under the more robust L-moment metric. **Robustness Analysis:**
- With all 8 functions: Spearman ρ = -0.786, p = 0.021 (significant)
- Without F8 (7 functions): Spearman ρ = -0.679, p = 0.094 (not significant at α = 0.05) F8 is a key driver of the correlation, but the negative trend persists even without it (ρ = -0.679), suggesting the phenomenon has both a strong outlier component and a more general underlying pattern. ### Interpretation The persistence of the negative correlation with L-kurtosis confirms this is a **genuine phenomenon** rather than an artifact of standard kurtosis's sensitivity to outliers. The slightly weaker correlation with L-kurtosis (-0.786 vs -0.810) is noteworthy: L-moments are designed to be more robust to extreme values, yet the relationship weakens. This suggests the correlation may be inherently driven by extreme tail behavior (which standard kurtosis captures more sensitively) rather than by robust distributional features. Both kurtosis metrics reveal the same fundamental inverse relationship: functions with heavier-tailed bulk distributions (higher kurtosis) exhibit lighter-tailed extreme value distributions (less negative ξ, closer to Gumbel), while functions with lighter-tailed bulk distributions exhibit bounded extreme value distributions (more negative ξ, Weibull-like). **Additional Finding:** Both skewness metrics show positive correlation with ξ (L-skewness: ρ = 0.810, p = 0.015; standard skewness: ρ = 0.857, p = 0.007), providing complementary information about the bulk-extreme relationship. ### Conclusion **The hypothesis is confirmed with important nuances:** 1. ✓ The negative correlation **persists** with L-kurtosis (ρ = -0.786, p = 0.021)
2. ✓ F8 (fully random) **remains an outlier** with high L-kurtosis (0.155) and strongly negative ξ (-1.370)
3. ⚠ The correlation is **slightly weaker** with L-kurtosis than with standard kurtosis (Δρ = +0.024)
4. ⚠ The correlation **becomes non-significant** (p = 0.094) when F8 is excluded, though the negative trend persists (ρ = -0.679) **Recommendation:** Both L-kurtosis and standard kurtosis are valuable metrics for characterizing the bulk-extreme relationship in arithmetic L-functions.
