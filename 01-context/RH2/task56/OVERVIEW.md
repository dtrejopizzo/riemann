## Overview # Analysis of Phase Transition in α-Parametric Multiplicative Functions ## Summary I attempted to construct a parametric family of multiplicative functions where a_p = +1 with probability α and -1 with probability 1-α, to map the transition from ζ(s) (α=1) to L(s,λ) (α=0) using three metrics: GEV shape parameter ξ, maximum composite coherence R_comp, and Shannon entropy H at N=10^6. **Due to computational constraints, I was unable to complete the full analysis at N=10^6.** However, I successfully implemented all required components and obtained pilot results at N=10^4 that provide insights into the expected behavior. ## Key Findings from Pilot Analysis (N=10^4) From the pilot study with α ∈ {0.0, 0.5, 1.0}: **GEV Shape Parameter ξ:**
- α = 0.0 (Liouville): ξ = 0.294 ± 0.000
- α = 0.5 (random): ξ = 0.485 ± 0.044
- α = 1.0 (ζ): ξ = 0.077 ± 0.000 **Maximum Composite Coherence R_comp:**
- α = 0.0: R_comp = 0.0293 ± 0.0000
- α = 0.5: R_comp = 0.0370 ± 0.0080
- α = 1.0: R_comp = 0.0174 ± 0.0000 **Shannon Entropy H:**
- α = 0.0: H = 0.9999 ± 0.0000
- α = 1.0: H = 0.0000 ± 0.0000
- α = 0.5: H = 1.0000 ± 0.0000 ## Evidence Against the Hypothesis **The pilot data does NOT support the existence of a critical α_c where ξ crosses from negative to non-negative:** 1. **All measured ξ values are positive**, ranging from 0.077 to 0.485 across the tested α values. There is no zero crossing or sign change observed. 2. **ξ shows non-monotonic behavior**: The highest ξ occurs at α=0.5 (random multiplicative), not at the extremes. ζ (α=1) has the smallest positive ξ. 3. **The trend contradicts the hypothesis**: If a phase transition existed with ξ crossing zero, we would expect ξ to be negative on one side of α_c and positive on the other. Instead, ξ remains positive throughout. ## Interpretation in Context of Prior Research The pilot results align with the discovery report findings: 1. **Discovery 1** reported that multiplicative functions prefer log-correlated growth models (which typically have negative or small positive ξ values in GEV fits), but the random multiplicative model showed the **strongest heavy tails and largest resonance scores**. 2. **Discovery 4** showed that multiplicative anomalies (like L(s,λ), which corresponds to α=0) exhibit **intrinsic constructive interference** and elevated composite coherence, not suppression. 3. The fact that α=0.5 shows the highest ξ (≈0.485) suggests that **intermediate mixing of signs may produce more extreme resonances** than either pure cooperation (α=1) or structured opposition (α=0). ## Methodological Limitations 1. **Computational constraints**: Unable to complete analysis at N=10^6 as specified. Results limited to N=10^4. 2. **Sample size**: Only three α values tested in pilot (0.0, 0.5, 1.0), insufficient to map detailed transition. 3. **Single realization effect**: At N=10^4, the specific random realization may influence results, though the Liouville (α=0) and ζ (α=1) cases are deterministic. 4. **GEV fitting uncertainty**: With n_blocks=50-100 and limited data, confidence intervals on ξ are large (±0.28-0.34). ## Conclusion **The research hypothesis is NOT SUPPORTED by the available data.** The prediction of a critical probability α_c ∈ (0, 0.5] where the GEV parameter ξ crosses from negative to non-negative is not observed. All tested α values show positive ξ, with the maximum occurring at α=0.5 rather than at the extremes. The data suggest that the landscape between ζ-like (α=1) and λ-like (α=0) behavior is more complex than a simple phase transition model. The **random multiplicative case (α≈0.5) may represent a distinct regime** with enhanced resonance properties compared to both structured extremes. --- ## DISCRETIONARY ANALYTICAL DECISIONS - **Computational scale**: Used N=10^4 instead of specified N=10^6 due to computational time constraints (20-minute timeout per cell).
