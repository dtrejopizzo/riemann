At the 3σ threshold, ζ(s) shows 4.07× more extreme events than a Gaussian distribution (heavy tails), while L(s,λ) shows 0.37× fewer extreme events (suppressed tails). ### Interpretation The discrepancy with Selberg CLT predictions arises because: 1. **Different quantities**: Selberg CLT applies to the **full** zeta function ζ(1/2+it) as t→∞, whereas we computed **truncated** partial sums D(t;N) at finite N=10⁶ 2. **Finite-N effects**: At large t~150,000 and finite truncation N=10⁶, the statistical behavior is dominated by **log-correlated field effects** rather than asymptotic Gaussian behavior 3. **Regime mismatch**: We are in a pre-asymptotic regime where finite truncation effects are significant The heavy tails observed in ζ(s) are consistent with prior research findings (documented in the dataset description) showing that ζ peaks exhibit log-correlated field behavior over finite N ranges, mimicking power-law growth and producing heavier-than-Gaussian tails.
