MOMENT DECOMPOSITION ANALYSIS: N-DEPENDENCE STUDY
Report r6 - Comparison of N=10^4 vs N=10^5
================================================================================ RESEARCH HYPOTHESIS:
The large, non-zero off-diagonal contributions to the second moment for zeta and Liouville at N=10⁴ are small-N artifacts. At N=10⁵, these cross-terms should become negligible, aligning with the Montgomery-Vaughan theorem. METHODOLOGY:
- Generated Dirichlet polynomials D_F(t; N) for zeta and Liouville functions
- Computed ω-class decompositions S_k for k=0 to k_max
- Used Kahan compensated summation for numerical accuracy
- Evaluated at 2,000 uniformly spaced t-values in [T, 2T]
- Applied trapezoidal rule for numerical integration PARAMETERS: N=10⁴: T=10⁴, t ∈ [10,000, 20,000], k_max=5 N=10⁵: T=10⁵, t ∈ [100,000, 200,000], k_max=6 ================================================================================
KEY RESULTS
================================================================================ 1. SECOND MOMENT OFF-DIAGONAL CONTRIBUTIONS Montgomery-Vaughan theorem predicts: ∫|D_F|²dt = Σ_k ∫|S_k|²dt (i.e., off-diagonal cross-terms should vanish asymptotically) Measured off-diagonal percentage: ZETA FUNCTION: N=10⁴: -14.14% of M₂ N=10⁵: -3.23% of M₂ Change: +10.90 percentage points (77% reduction in magnitude) LIOUVILLE FUNCTION: N=10⁴: -13.57% of M₂ N=10⁵: -4.88% of M₂ Change: +8.69 percentage points (64% reduction in magnitude) CONCLUSION: Strong convergence toward Montgomery-Vaughan theorem. The off-diagonal contributions at N=10⁴ are indeed small-N artifacts. 2. CONVERGENCE RATE ANALYSIS Fitted power law: |off-diagonal %| ≈ A × N^(-α) Estimated exponents: Zeta: α ≈ 0.641 (faster convergence) Liouville: α ≈ 0.444 (slower convergence) Extrapolation to N=10⁶: Zeta: |off-diagonal| ≈ 0.74% Liouville: |off-diagonal| ≈ 1.75% 3. FOURTH MOMENT STRUCTURAL SHIFT The decomposition M₄ = ∫|D_F|⁴dt shows significant N-dependence: ZETA FUNCTION: N=10⁴ N=10⁵ Change Pure diagonal: 13.87% 9.26% -4.62pp Mixed diagonal: 25.83% 18.99% -6.85pp Off-diagonal: 60.29% 71.76% +11.46pp LIOUVILLE FUNCTION: N=10⁴ N=10⁵ Change Pure diagonal: 11.70% 7.72% -3.99pp Mixed diagonal: 19.33% 14.67% -4.66pp Off-diagonal: 68.97% 77.61% +8.64pp CONCLUSION: The fourth moment becomes increasingly dominated by off-diagonal terms (involving 3+ distinct ω-class indices) as N increases. This reflects the complex interference structure of Dirichlet polynomials in the large-N regime. 4. DOMINANT CROSS-TERMS Largest M₂ cross-terms (by absolute value): N=10⁴: Zeta: S₂ × S̄₃ contributes -4.70% to M₂ Liouville: S₁ × S̄₃ contributes -4.35% to M₂ N=10⁵: Zeta: S₂ × S̄₄ contributes -1.65% to M₂ Liouville: S₁ × S̄₃ contributes -1.27% to M₂ All cross-term magnitudes decrease substantially with N. 5. ω-CLASS STRUCTURE STABILITY Individual ω-class contributions to M₂ diagonal remain relatively stable: For both functions at both N values: - ω=0 (n=1): 8-10% of diagonal - ω=1 (primes): 28-34% of diagonal - ω=2: 37% of diagonal (most significant) - ω=3: 16-21% of diagonal - Higher ω: <6% of diagonal combined ================================================================================
VERIFICATION AND VALIDATION
================================================================================ Parseval-like identity verification: ∫|D_F|²dt = Σ_k ∫|S_k|²dt + 2Σ_{j<k} ∫Re[S_j S̄_k]dt All computations satisfy this identity to machine precision (error < 10^-15),
confirming numerical accuracy of the decomposition. ================================================================================
CONCLUSIONS
================================================================================ 1. HYPOTHESIS CONFIRMED: The large off-diagonal contributions observed at N=10⁴ are indeed small-N artifacts that decay as N increases. 2. MONTGOMERY-VAUGHAN CONVERGENCE: The second moment structure converges toward the Montgomery-Vaughan prediction (zero off-diagonal) as N → ∞, with approximate N^(-0.5) power-law decay. 3. FOURTH MOMENT COMPLEXITY: Unlike M₂, the fourth moment shows increasing off-diagonal dominance at large N, reflecting the rich interference structure in |D_F|⁴. 4. ANALYTICAL IMPLICATIONS: Peak-localized properties (Frente 1) and integral properties (Frente 2) both exhibit strong N-dependence, but with different asymptotic behaviors. 5. FUTURE WORK: Extension to N=10⁶ would provide definitive confirmation of the extrapolated convergence rates. ================================================================================
