## Overview ## MAIN FINDING: HYPOTHESIS REJECTED The hypothesis that the Liouville r-value increase from N=10^6 to N=10^7 is caused by accelerating power redistribution to higher ω-classes (k≥4) is **firmly rejected**. The data show the **opposite pattern**: a dramatic 52.0% reduction in the fraction of power in k≥4 classes (from 0.410 ± 0.150 at N=10^6 to 0.197 ± 0.134 at N=10^7, p=0.046). ## QUANTITATIVE EVIDENCE **Power Distribution Changes:**
- **k=0-3 (lower classes)**: Increased from 59.0% to 80.3% of total power (+36.1%) - k=0: +300.4% (0.0083 → 0.0331) - k=1: +133.3% (0.0864 → 0.2016) - k=2: +51.3% (0.2224 → 0.3364) - k=3: -15.0% (0.2730 → 0.2321) - **k≥4 (higher classes)**: Decreased from 40.8% to 19.5% of total power (-52.1%) - k=4: -51.8% (0.2132 → 0.1027) - k=5: -57.0% (0.1122 → 0.0483) - k≥6: -45.8% (0.0845 → 0.0458) **Entropy Analysis:**
- N=10^6: H = 1.772 ± 0.117
- N=10^7: H = 1.631 ± 0.205
- Change: -7.9% (p=0.220, t-test; p=0.095, Mann-Whitney)
- Lower entropy at N=10^7 indicates more concentrated power distribution **Canonical r Metric:**
- N=10^6: r = 3.047 ± 1.163
- N=10^7: r = 3.676 ± 1.577
- Change: +20.6% (p=0.494, not significant) **Correlation Analysis (n=10 peaks combined):**
- r vs. Fraction k≥4: Pearson r = -0.048, p = 0.89 (no correlation)
- r vs. Entropy: Pearson r = +0.196, p = 0.59 (no correlation)
- Entropy vs. Fraction k≥4: Pearson r = +0.875, p = 0.001 (strong positive correlation) ## MECHANISTIC INTERPRETATION The data reveal a **power concentration reversal**: instead of continuing to redistribute to higher ω-classes, power dramatically re-concentrates in lower classes (k=0-2) at N=10^7. This represents either: 1. **Redistribution Saturation**: The sparsity-driven mechanism documented at lower N may have exhausted by N≤10^6, with N=10^7 entering a new asymptotic regime
2. **Pre-asymptotic Complexity**: Non-monotonic behavior similar to the documented N=10^5 anomaly for real-valued coefficient functions
3. **Phase-Driven r Behavior**: The 20.6% increase in canonical r (though not statistically significant, p=0.494) is **uncorrelated** with k≥4 power fraction (r=-0.048, p=0.89), indicating r-value behavior is driven by changes in **phase relationships** (Re[S_j S̄_k] cross-terms) rather than continued power redistribution The strong positive correlation (r=0.875, p=0.001) between entropy and k≥4 fraction confirms that these metrics capture the same underlying power distribution structure. ## CONCLUSION The Liouville r-value increase from N=10^6 to N=10^7 **cannot be explained by accelerating power redistribution to higher ω-classes**. The mechanism must involve changes in phase coherence between ω-classes, particularly enhanced constructive interference between the lower classes (k=0-3) where power is now concentrated. This finding suggests that the sparsity-driven redistribution mechanism operates in a limited pre-asymptotic regime and may reverse or saturate at very large N. --- ## DISCRETIONARY ANALYTICAL DECISIONS - **Peak selection at N=10^6**: Used coarse grid search with 1000 points in range [10^6, 2×10^6], followed by selection of top 5 peaks by magnitude.
