Without N=10⁶ baseline data, I can only characterize N=10⁷ structure, not the change. ### Interpretation Based on Available N=10⁷ Data If we assume the N=10⁷ data represents the "enhanced r" state described in the hypothesis: **Evidence AGAINST the hypothesis:**
- The two highest-r peaks (r=5.91 and r=4.24) are **dominated by non-adjacent pairs** (68.5% and 57.3%)
- Adjacent pairs do not show systematic dominance across high-r peaks
- The contribution is balanced (50-50 mean) rather than dominated by low-order adjacent pairs **Evidence that would SUPPORT the hypothesis:**
- The two peaks with strongest adjacent dominance (peaks 3 and 4) do show concentrated contributions from specific low-order pairs
- Peak 4 specifically shows S₁-S₂ and S₂-S₃ pairs as major contributors (based on 68.2% adjacent share) However, this interpretation is speculative without the N=10⁶ comparison to establish what changed. ### Methodological Notes **Pairwise decomposition method:** For r = Num/Denom where Num = Σ_{j≠k} Re[S_j S̄_k], I computed each unique pair (j,k) contribution as 2×Re[S_j S̄_k] to account for both (j,k) and (k,j) terms. Adjacent pairs are |j-k|=1, non-adjacent are |j-k|>1. **Canonical r formula validated:** r = Num/Denom with Num = Σ_{j≠k} Re[S_j S̄_k] and Denom = Σ_k |S_k|².
