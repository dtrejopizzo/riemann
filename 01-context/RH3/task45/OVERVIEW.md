## Overview ## Analysis of f_canon_rand Inter-Class Energy Ratio Evolution from N=10^6 to N=10^7 ### Summary of Findings I successfully performed sparse grid searches (1,000 points) for the `f_canon_rand` function at both N=10^6 and N=10^7, identifying the top 50 highest-magnitude peaks at each truncation level. However, due to computational time constraints, I was unable to complete the omega-class decomposition and r-metric calculation for these peaks. ### Quantitative Results from Grid Searches **N = 10^6 Analysis:**
- Grid: 1,000 points over t ∈ [10^6, 2×10^6], spacing ≈ 1,001
- Computation time: 32.6 seconds
- Maximum magnitude: |D| = 42.42 at t = 1,543,543.5
- Top 50 peaks identified with magnitudes ranging from 42.42 down to 7.40 **N = 10^7 Analysis:**
- Grid: 1,000 points over t ∈ [10^7, 2×10^7], spacing ≈ 10,010 - Computation time: 840.6 seconds (~14 minutes)
- Maximum magnitude: |D| = 35.95 at t = 15,295,295.3
- Top 50 peaks identified with magnitudes ranging from 35.95 down to 8.49 **Key Observation on Magnitude Evolution:**
The maximum peak magnitude **decreased** from 42.42 at N=10^6 to 35.95 at N=10^7 (15% reduction). This suggests a magnitude damping trend as N increases, though without statistical analysis of the full peak distributions, I cannot definitively characterize this as monotonic evolution. ### Incomplete Analysis Components **Critical Limitation:** I was unable to compute the canonical r-metric and adjacent/non-adjacent interference structure for the identified peaks due to computational timeout. The omega-class decomposition at N=10^6 for 50 peaks exceeded the 51-second cell timeout limit. This calculation requires:
1. For each peak and each n ∈ [1, N], computing S_k = Σ_{Ω(n)=k} a_n/n^(1/2+it)
2. Computing r = Σ_{j≠k} Re[S_j S̄_k] / Σ_k |S_k|²
3. Decomposing the numerator into adjacent (|j-k|=1) vs non-adjacent (|j-k|>1) contributions At N=10^6, this requires ~10^6 operations per peak × 50 peaks, and the Python implementation (even with vectorization) proved too slow within the available runtime. ### Methodological Limitations 1. **No peak refinement:** Due to time constraints, I used peaks directly from the coarse 1,000-point grid without local refinement via Kahan summation or optimization. This introduces location uncertainty of ±500-5,000 in t-space. 2. **Unable to complete primary objective:** The research objective specifically required computing mean, median, and standard deviation of r-values and percentage contributions from adjacent vs. non-adjacent pairs. None of these statistics could be calculated. 3. **No comparison possible:** Without r-metrics at both N values, I cannot determine if evolution is monotonic or assess whether f_canon_rand differs from arithmetic L-functions. ### Computational Bottleneck Analysis The fundamental issue is that omega-class decomposition at N=10^6+ has O(N × n_peaks) complexity. For 50 peaks at N=10^6, this is 5×10^7 operations involving transcendental functions (log, cos, sin, sqrt), which exceeds feasible computation time in pure Python/NumPy even with Numba acceleration. **Required for future work:** Pre-computing and caching omega-class partial sums S_k(t) on the grid, or implementing the decomposition in compiled code (C/Cython), or using GPU acceleration. --- ## DISCRETIONARY DECISIONS - **Grid density:** Used 1,000 points as specified in research objective (required decision)
