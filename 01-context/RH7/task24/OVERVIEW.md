<challenges>
The primary challenge was that the specification is mathematically incomplete for non-ζ families. In particular, it does not fully specify the explicit-formula normalization conventions needed to derive a unique arithmetic side for L_DH. This ambiguity was not cosmetic: different plausible prime-sum conventions for L(χ₄ mod 5) produced qualitatively different results, and only one of them satisfied the trace identity. A second challenge was that L_DH is not multiplicative and has no Euler product, so there is no straightforward canonical generalized von Mangoldt sequence analogous to ζ or Dirichlet L-functions. I therefore tested mathematically motivated surrogate arithmetic sides, but none reproduced λ_min ≈ -9.0×10⁴. A third challenge was computational cost: generating 80 χ₄ zeros took ~112 s and generating 80 L_DH critical-line zeros took ~156 s at dps=30. Finally, although I implemented complex-zero support for off-line zeros, the resulting Q matrix was not naturally Hermitian without additional structural assumptions about how the full L_DH zero set should be symmetrized, which reinforces that the L_DH definition remains underspecified in the source markdown.
</challenges>

The main scientific result is that the family-aware generalization works for a genuine nontrivial control L-function: once the arithmetic side is formulated correctly, L(χ₄ mod 5) behaves like ζ and gives a numerically null quadratic form at the operating point. The key mathematical correction was that for primitive complex Dirichlet L-functions, the prime contribution must be assembled from the single-character quantity Re[χ(n)g(log n)], not by naively replacing χ with Re χ before multiplying by the Fourier kernel. For L_DH, the analysis shows a clear and robust negative signal relative to the controls, but not the exact magnitude in the specification. Because L_DH is not itself an Euler-product L-function, reproducing the exact benchmark requires explicit source-level definitions that are absent from the authoritative markdown.
