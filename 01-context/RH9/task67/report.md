## Discovery 1: Structural Obstructions to Integer-Graded Hard-Lefschetz Geometry in Noncommutative Spectral Models

### Summary

Noncommutative geometric realizations of the Weil explicit formula recover the arithmetic distributions of the zeta function, but they rely on regularization cutoffs that reflect noncompact scaling and do not yield the compact, integer-graded structures required for a Hard-Lefschetz proof. Quantum statistical mechanical models have Hamiltonians with logarithmic spectra, and attempts to extract an integer grading via cyclic or K-theoretic λ-operations fail in the surveyed settings. A general operator-theoretic analysis shows that a bounded Lefschetz operator satisfying [T, L]=cL is obstructed by the expected ℚ-linear independence of zero ordinates, and unbounded alternatives require infinitely many repeated gaps c that are not seen in high-precision zero statistics. Positivity statements obtained in this framework are equivalent to the Riemann Hypothesis, and an intersection theory on the square of the scaling/adele-class site sufficient to define a diagonal class and Hodge index form is not yet available.

### Background

The geometric class K_geom/SURF must support an integer-graded sl<sub>2</sub>-action with a compact-type Hodge-Riemann form, and a Lefschetz operator L that is independent of the spectral operator T extracted from the Weil explicit formula. Route C (Connes-Consani scaling/adele-class space and related quantum statistical mechanical models) gives a spectral realization of zeros and a trace-formula avatar of the explicit formula, but its scaling dynamics are inherently noncompact. This clashes with the need for a lowest-weight grading, compactness-like positivity, and a bona fide intersection theory on a "square" admitting a diagonal class [Δ]. The following synthesis delineates the precise mechanisms by which these requirements fail or remain unconstructed in the current noncommutative frameworks.

### Results & Discussion

Connes's program rigorously interprets the Weil explicit formula as a trace formula on the adèle class space, realizing the nontrivial zeros as an "absorption spectrum." However, this realization uses regularization cutoffs - phase-space projections, multiplicative windowing [λ<sup>-1</sup>, λ], and finite time/frequency truncations - reflecting the noncompact scaling dynamics. These cutoffs are adjustable and, as surveyed, no published work quantitatively reproduces global long-range signatures of the zeros such as the observed saturation of the number variance Δ<sub>3</sub>(L). In parallel, a full intersection theory on the "square" needed to define a diagonal class [Δ] and an associated Hodge-index pairing is not constructed, leaving the positivity and Lefschetz machinery without a geometric home inside this framework.

Multiple, independent mechanisms block the emergence of an integer-graded Hard-Lefschetz structure. First, in the scaling-site geometry, each periodic orbit C_p=ℝ<sub>+*</sub>/p^ℤ has a Riemann-Roch formula χ(D)=deg(D) with deg(D) an arbitrary real number, making an integer-valued Euler characteristic - and hence a finite-dimensional, integer-graded cohomology - impossible on C_p. Second, in quantum statistical mechanical realizations (e.g., Bost-Connes and its GL<sub>2</sub> generalization), the time evolution is generated by a Hamiltonian with logarithmic discrete spectrum (eigenvalues like log n or log det(m)), which is incompatible with the integer spectrum required of the grading operator in a Hard-Lefschetz sl<sub>2</sub>-triple. Third, passing to cyclic or topological K-theory does not reveal a hidden integer grading: known cyclic λ-operations yielding eigenvalues m<sup>j</sup> apply to commutative settings and not to the noncommutative C*-algebras at hand, and no λ-ring/Adams package is established for these algebras' K-theory.

A separate, operator-theoretic obstruction concerns compatibility with the spectral operator T. For a self-adjoint T with discrete spectrum, any bounded L satisfying [T, L]=cL must vanish unless the spectrum contains arithmetic progressions with step c; under the anticipated ℚ-linear independence of the zeros' ordinates, such progressions are absent. While unbounded L can satisfy [T, L]=cL on a dense domain even without arithmetic progressions, existence requires infinitely many spectral pairs with fixed gap c. High-precision statistics of the first 10<sup>6</sup> zeros show pair correlations matching GUE without spikes at any fixed c, ratio statistics revealing no excess of approximate 3-term arithmetic progressions, and a Δ<sub>3</sub>(L) curve that saturates at long range but not via repeated gap structures - all weighing strongly against the spectral repetitions needed for such an unbounded L tied directly to the zeros.

Finally, positivity sources within the noncommutative adèle-class framework are equivalent to RH and thus cannot furnish an independent Hodge-Riemann form. Although the noncommutative trace formula sharply reproduces the arithmetic structure of the explicit formula (archimedean Ψ and von Mangoldt weights), it stops short of supplying a compact, integer-graded cohomology, an independent Lefschetz class, and a non-circular positivity theory on primitives. As things stand, Route C realizes the explicit formula but does not provide G2 (integer grading/Hard-Lefschetz) or G4 (independent Hodge-Riemann positivity), and it lacks the intersection theory necessary to define [Δ] with the required properties on a geometric "square."

## Discovery 2: Arakelov-λ-Ring Blueprint for K_geom: Independent Lefschetz Class and Non-Circular Positivity with Open sl

### Summary

Arakelov geometry supplies a canonical candidate for the Lefschetz operator L as cup-product with an arithmetic first Chern class, and a bilinear form Q given by arithmetic intersection followed by degree, together yielding a Hodge-Riemann sign via proven arithmetic Hodge index theorems. A metric-variation argument establishes structural independence of L from the spectral operator T: changing the hermitian metric alters L while T, determined by the zero set, remains fixed, implying L ∉ W*(T). λ-ring/Adams operations contribute an intrinsic integer grading, and in Arakelov motivic cohomology L shifts the Adams weight compatibly; however, the sl<sub>2</sub> grading operator aligns with cohomological degree, not Adams weight, and the absolute surface itself is not yet constructed. A geometric Λ (lowering operator) is missing in general (except for abelian varieties), but compatibility patterns such as [L, Hecke]=0 and known tensor-factor decouplings indicate a viable path to coexistence with spectral operators without violating linear-independence constraints.

### Background

Route A seeks K_geom/SURF inside Arakelov-type or 𝔽<sub>1</sub>/λ-ring architectures, aiming for a cohomology with integer grading, a geometric Lefschetz class [Δ], and a positive Hodge-Riemann form independent of RH. Arithmetic Chow and motivic theories deliver canonical intersection pairings and arithmetic Chern classes, while λ-ring structures furnish Adams operations ψ<sup>n</sup> that generate an integer weight decomposition. The open challenge is to assemble a true sl<sub>2</sub>-triple (L,Λ,H) acting on the appropriate arithmetic cohomology of an "absolute surface," with Λ realized geometrically and H giving an integer, lowest-weight grading. This group synthesizes the pieces that already satisfy core gates (independence, positivity) and identifies what remains open.

### Results & Discussion

Arakelov theory identifies the natural bilinear form Q as the arithmetic intersection pairing followed by degree, and the Lefschetz operator as L(x)=x⋅ĉ<sub>1</sub>(H̄) for an arithmetically ample hermitian line bundle. The complex structure J can be implemented by the archimedean Weil operator C acting on (p,q)-forms, so that defining ω(x,y)=−deg(C(L(x))·y) produces positivity on primitive classes. This is a non-circular source of sign-definiteness: for arithmetic divisors, the relevant (1,1)-form component makes C act as the identity and the arithmetic Hodge Index theorem gives −deg((L(x))·x)≥0 on primitives. Thus, Arakelov geometry supplies G4-type positivity without presupposing RH.

Independence of L from T is certified by metric variation. The arithmetic first Chern class depends on the archimedean metric via a Green's function term −log∥s∥<sup>2</sup>, so varying the metric m deforms L to L_m while the spectral operator T, defined by the set of nontrivial zeros {γ_ρ}, is invariant. The difference ĉ<sub>1</sub>(m<sub>1</sub>)−ĉ<sub>1</sub>(m<sub>0</sub>) is a Bott-Chern secondary class and for P<sup>1</sup>_ℤ with O(1), the rescaling m<sub>1</sub>=e<sup>−c</sup>m<sub>0</sub> yields a nontrivial class whose intersection with a rational point like [0] is nonzero. Because W*(T) is fixed by {γ_ρ} and L_m varies with m, this furnishes strong evidence that L ∉ W*(T), passing the independence gate.

λ-ring structures provide an intrinsic integer grading via Adams operations ψ<sup>n</sup>, which act diagonally with eigenvalues n<sup>j</sup> on weight-j components after rationalization. In Arakelov motivic cohomology, L=∪ĉ<sub>1</sub> shifts degree by +2 and Adams weight by +1, supplying a compatible bigrading and a concrete raising operator. However, the grading operator H in an sl<sub>2</sub>-triple is determined by cohomological degree (e.g., 2p−d), not by Adams weight, so identifying H with the Adams-weight operator is obstructed. Moreover, the sought "absolute surface" Spec ℤx<sub>𝔽<sub>1</sub></sub>Spec ℤ with a diagonal class [Δ] remains unconstructed, and adjacent 𝔽<sub>1</sub>-models that give spectral spaces (e.g., H<sup>1</sup>(P<sup>1</sup>_{𝔽<sub>1</sub>},Ω)) do not provide the required sl<sub>2</sub>-triple; related metaplectic/prolate sl<sub>2</sub> realizations have continuous spectra for the scaling generator, not the discrete lowest-weight grading needed.

The lowering operator Λ is not generally realized as a geometric adjoint of L in arithmetic settings; instead, Λ and H are typically imposed axiomatically to secure integrality. A notable exception occurs for abelian varieties, where a Fourier-Mukai mechanism (with Poincaré bundle kernel) yields a bona fide geometric sl<sub>2</sub> with adjointness, but this relies on structures absent for Spec ℤ. Partial geometric progress exists in derived Hecke/correspondence algebras, where explicit degree-raising operators are constructed by pull-cup-push, though a full (e,f,h) triple is not yet assembled. Crucially, decoupled compatibility is available: on Shimura-type geometries, L commutes with Hecke operators because the automorphic hermitian metric is preserved under Hecke correspondences, and, more generally, tensor-factor models show how an sl<sub>2</sub> on one factor can coexist with an independent spectral action on another. Together, these features chart a rigorous blueprint that passes independence and non-circular positivity, isolates the missing Λ/[Δ]/surface pieces, and suggests a concrete path to G1-G4 once the absolute surface is built.

## Discovery 3: Absolute Prismatic Cohomology Lacks a Global Spectral Generator and Hodge-Riemann Package

### Summary

Prismatic and topological-cyclic frameworks provide a plausible global base stack and cohomological object for Spec ℤ, but they do not currently furnish a canonical global operator whose spectrum could be the Riemann zeros. Existing machinery coherently packages local Frobenius data into a monoid action but stops short of producing a single endomorphism with global spectral meaning. Attempts to construct a global Sen-type generator fail, reflecting a fundamental division of labor: prismatic Frobenius determines L-factors while Sen operators encode Hodge-Tate weights at p-adic places. Essential analytic structures for a Hard-Lefschetz/Hodge-Riemann argument - hermitian pairings, a *-operator, and regularized determinants realizing ζ(s) - are also absent, despite local duality theorems indicating partial feasibility.

### Background

Route B aspires to realize Deninger-type absolute cohomology within Bhatt-Scholze's prismatic theory and related structures, hoping to construct a global cohomology H*(Spec ℤ, ·) carrying a flow or Frobenius-like endomorphism whose spectral data links to the zeros. The prismatic formalism packages p-adic period maps and filtrations (notably Nygaard), and interacts with TC/THH to synthesize arithmetic phenomena across primes. For a Lefschetz/Hodge-Riemann strategy, one would also need a Poincaré duality package and analytic structures to encode the functional equation and positivity. The current state of the art, however, lacks the spectral and analytic components required to connect to the Riemann zeros.

### Results & Discussion

The integral prismatization stack Σ_Z has been proposed as a natural global base, with derived objects H_{ΔZ}(Spec ℤ) serving as candidates for absolute cohomology. Nevertheless, there is no canonical global R-action or Frobenius-like operator defined on Σ_Z whose spectrum could match the nontrivial zeros. Available universal mechanisms (animated λ-rings, TC) coherently assemble prime-indexed Frobenius maps {φ_p} into an ℕ<sub>></sub>0-monoid action, but such packaging does not supply a single global endomorphism with a computed or conjectural spectrum equal to the zeros. Hence the "zeros as eigenvalues" vision remains external to current prismatic/TC realizations.

A complementary attempt to globalize Sen theory also fails. Local Sen operators Θ_p exist as infinitesimal generators of cyclotomic Γ_p-actions, and there is a formal analogy to the archimedean scaling generator Θ_∞=s d/ds, but these act on distinct objects and no mechanism unifies them into a Θ_global over Spec ℤ. Moreover, proposals like q-de Rham are explicitly prime-by-prime and do not yield a unified arena for a single global generator to act.

The Nygaard filtration is the strongest candidate for a weight filtration in this environment, but no explicit identification with a Deninger-style weight theory is established. Further, the analytic duality and determinant structures required by Deninger's program - a hermitian pairing, a *-operator compatible with a putative flow, and a regularized determinant formula for ζ(s) - are absent from the surveyed prismatic formalism. The structural separation is sharp: Frobenius φ on crystalline realizations computes local L-factors, whereas Sen operators Θ_p determine Hodge-Tate weights; their roles are not interchangeable.

These gaps persist despite positive local results, such as proven local finiteness and duality theorems in prismatic-type settings, which suggest that parts of the needed package can exist. In sum, while prismatic/TC theory provides a compelling scaffold for a compact, integer-graded cohomology, it does not currently offer a global spectral operator, the analytic/Hodge-Riemann toolkit, or a mechanism to relate spectral data to the global zeros. This leaves G1 (spectral T), G3 (Tate-twist commutation), and G4 (positivity with a *-operator) unaddressed in Route B.

## Discovery 4: Long-Range Spectral Statistics Impose a Prime-Power Design Constraint on K_geom

### Summary

Empirical spectral-rigidity data for the first 10^6 Riemann zeros reveal a pronounced long-range plateau Δ3^∞ ≈ 0.1908, departing from Gaussian Unitary Ensemble (GUE) growth and setting a quantitative target for any proposed “absolute” geometry K_geom behind the Riemann Hypothesis. Semiclassical analysis via the explicit formula predicts a plateau scaling like (1/2π^2)[ln ln(E/2π)+C] with C ≈ 1.4009, in ≈2% agreement with the data and driven by a prime-power series Σ_{p,r} 1/(r^2 p^r), thereby imposing a concrete prime-power design constraint on K_geom.

### Background

Random-matrix universality describes local statistics of Riemann zeros, but long-range correlations probe arithmetic structure encoded by primes through Weil’s explicit formula. The spectral-rigidity observable Δ3(L) (Dyson–Mehta) integrates two-point correlations over a window of length L and is therefore sensitive to non-universal, long-time effects. A geometric model K_geom/SURF for the zeta function that aims to recover the Riemann zeros as Frobenius-type eigenvalues should reproduce both the short-range GUE behavior and the empirically observed long-range rigidity, including its quantitative dependence on prime-power contributions and truncation height. This makes Δ3(L) a stringent, data-driven constraint on any candidate cohomological/Lefschetz package underlying the Riemann Hypothesis.

### Results & Discussion

High-precision analysis of Δ3(L) for the first 10^6 Riemann zeros shows that while Δ3(L) initially tracks the GUE curve, it departs at very small L and rapidly saturates to a plateau Δ3^∞ = 0.190757 ± 6.4 × 10^−5, with the onset reaching within 5% of the plateau by L ≈ 76 and within 1% by L ≈ 342; statistical divergence from finite-N GUE is already significant (z > 5) at L = 4 and grows thereafter [[r69](task46)]. Over the same range, the GUE asymptote continues to rise logarithmically—reaching 0.921 by L = 1000—underscoring that the arithmetic spectrum exhibits robust long-range rigidity absent from random-matrix predictions [[r69](task46)]. These measurements establish a precise empirical target: any proposed K_geom must reproduce not only the local GUE-like slope but also a non-growing Δ3 plateau at ≈0.1908 with a well-characterized onset scale [[r69](task46)].

Semiclassical reasoning from the explicit formula predicts just such a plateau and fixes its scale: Berry’s prime-orbit analysis yields a saturation of the number variance Σ^2 at Σ^2_∞(E) = π^−2[ln ln(E/2π) + 1.4009], where E is the height of the spectral window, implying Δ3^∞ ≈ Σ^2_∞/2 [[r74](task14)]. For the dataset’s truncation height E ≈ 6 × 10^5, this gives Δ3^∞ ≈ 0.194557, i.e. ≈2% above the empirical 0.190757(64); a refined Pandey-integral evaluation at the measured L further reduces the mismatch to ≈1–1.5% across L = 500–1000, consistent with the expected accuracy of the semiclassical approximation [[r74](task14)]. Crucially, the literal prime-sum ansatz (1/2π^2) Σ_{p,k} (log p)^2/p^k diverges, and the correct prediction arises only after imposing the natural Heisenberg-time cutoff from the explicit formula, leaving the finite constant 1.4009 and the characteristic ln ln(E/2π) dependence; this structure is governed by the prime-power series Σ_{p,r} 1/(r^2 p^r), which sets the “prime-power design constraint” that any valid K_geom must encode [[r74](task14)].

On the geometric/trace-formula side, the Connes–Consani scaling-site/adele-class-space framework makes the primitive-orbit mechanism explicit: each prime p defines a periodic orbit C_p of length log p, Frobenius/scaling monodromy acts by multiplication by p, and the trace formula assigns the von Mangoldt weight log p to all prime powers p^m via iterates—exactly the structure needed to feed the explicit formula’s prime-power side [[r55](task9)]. However, in the retrieved sources a completed cohomology H*(S) with a Grothendieck–Lefschetz trace identity Tr(F*|H*(S)) equalling the von Mangoldt-weighted sum is not yet established; the program is formulated via distributional traces and correspondences rather than a proved Lefschetz trace on a full cohomology theory [[r55](task9)]. Moreover, while the noncommutative-geometry approach deploys explicit phase-space and place cutoffs (e.g., Sonin-type projections, multiplicative windows, semilocal truncations) that could qualitatively suppress long-range correlations, no work retrieved here derives the observed Δ3 plateau value or its ln ln(E) scaling from these regularizations, leaving that quantitative link open [[r65](task62)].

Independently, arithmetic Riemann–Roch formulas offer detailed finite-place contributions but, as currently formulated, they do not manifest the prime-power expansion intrinsic to the Δ3 plateau. Across state-of-the-art settings—including non-compact/orbifold arithmetic surfaces with cusps and conductor terms—the non-archimedean pieces are expressed as sums ∑_p a_p log p, where a_p are local geometric lengths, Chern-class degrees, or conductors; these terms do not naturally unfold into an infinite prime-power series ∑_{p,r} 1/(r^2 p^r) [[r76](task39)]. Secondary analytic torsion/R-genus components do involve zeta/Lerch/polylog special values, but in the surveyed evidence they are presented as special-value formulas rather than Euler-product expansions whose logarithms would transparently yield the needed ∑_{p,r} structure; thus, in their current form, ARR formulas lack the explicit prime-power layering required by the rigidity constraint [[r76](task39)].

Together, these results sharpen a concrete, testable requirement for K_geom: the geometric/trace apparatus must (i) realize the primitive-periodic-orbit mechanism with log p weights and prime-power iterates, (ii) produce a Δ3(L) plateau that tracks Δ3^∞(E) ≈ (1/2π^2)[ln ln(E/2π) + 1.4009] across truncation heights, and (iii) account for the percent-level agreement observed at E ≈ 6 × 10^5 without circularly encoding the zero spectrum itself [[r69](task46), [r74](task14), [r55](task9)]. Current scaling-site/NCG frameworks satisfy (i) conceptually but lack a completed cohomology/trace proof of (ii), while existing arithmetic Riemann–Roch identities realize prime-sum log p weights but not the prime-power expansion driving the plateau; bridging these gaps is the critical next step to a geometric class K_geom that accommodates the long-range spectral rigidity of the Riemann zeros [[r55](task9), [r65](task62), [r76](task39)].
