## Broad overview: why geometric “\(K_{\mathrm{geom}}\)/SURF” matters

For curves over finite fields, RH for the zeta function is proved by importing geometry: zeta = characteristic polynomial of Frobenius on \(H^1\), and RH is a positivity consequence of Hard Lefschetz + Hodge index/Hodge–Riemann bilinear relations. In the number-field case \(\zeta(s)\), the Weil explicit formula already resembles a trace/Lefschetz formula. The core dream is that there exists an “arithmetic surface” \(S\) (morally \(\mathrm{Spec}\,\mathbb Z\times_{\mathbb F_1}\mathrm{Spec}\,\mathbb Z\)) with a diagonal class \([\Delta]\) acting as Lefschetz operator \(L\), such that:

- **G1**: \(H^1(S)\cong\Pi^\perp\) with Frobenius operator = your \(T\) (zeros ordinates); and \(L=[\Delta]\notin W^*(T)\) (independent geometry).
- **G2**: integer-graded lowest-weight \(\mathfrak{sl}_2\) (Hard Lefschetz).
- **G3**: Tate twist compatibility \([T,L]=cL\).
- **G4**: Hodge–Riemann positivity \(\omega\succ 0\) on primitives.

But your prompt emphasizes: (a) positivity statements derived from the Weil distribution are *equivalent to RH* and can be circular; (b) spectral models built from \(T\) alone cannot generate an independent Lefschetz operator; (c) continuous-spectrum settings naturally produce principal series, not lowest-weight Hodge modules.

The Connes program supplies a *rigorous spectral/trace-formula framework* for the explicit formula and attempts to build an “absolute geometry” (arithmetic site/scaling site) over characteristic one. The key question is whether this framework can supply G1–G4 without violating the gates.

## Route selected: Route C (Connes adèle class space / scaling site)

### Main themes and findings in the literature

1. **Zeros as an absorption spectrum and the explicit formula as a trace formula.**
   Connes develops a trace-formula interpretation of Weil’s explicit formula on the adèle class space, where the nontrivial zeros enter the spectral side as an “absorption spectrum” (missing spectral lines), and possible off-critical zeros appear as resonances. Moreover, he relates positivity of the Weil distribution / global trace formula to RH for Hecke \(L\)-functions with Grössencharakter. (connesUnknownyearformuledetrace pages 1-3, connes2000noncommutativegeometryyear pages 55-58)

2. **Noncompact scaling dynamics forces IR/UV cutoffs.**
   In the GL(1)/adèlic setting, Connes introduces cutoff projections such as \(Q_\Lambda\) (imposing simultaneous decay of a Schwartz vector and its Fourier transform beyond \(|x|>\Lambda\)), and trace expressions \(\mathrm{Tr}(Q_\Lambda\,\pi(f))\) carry divergent terms controlled by \(\log\Lambda\). This is not an incidental technicality: the trace formula in this framework *involves* both infrared and ultraviolet cutoffs and requires delicate renormalization/regularization. (connes2000noncommutativegeometryyear pages 55-58)

   Recent expository developments reiterate that the trace formula involves both IR and UV cutoffs and that an “ultraviolet model” is not yet genuinely available, motivating operator models (prolate/Sonin) aimed at capturing the high-frequency regime. (connes2602theriemannhypothesis pages 4-6, connes2602theriemannhypothesis pages 30-33)

3. **A characteristic-one geometric avatar: arithmetic site and scaling site.**
   Connes and Consani define the arithmetic site \(\mathscr A\) as the topos \(\mathbb N^\times\) equipped with structure sheaf \(\mathbb Z_{\max}\), and the scaling site \(\mathscr S\) as the semiringed topos \(([0,\infty)\rtimes\mathbb N^\times,\mathcal O)\) obtained by extension of scalars from the Boolean semifield \(\mathbb B\) to the tropical semifield \(\mathbb R_{\max}^+\), with \(\mathcal O\) modeled by convex piecewise-affine functions with integral slopes. Points recover the relevant adèle-class-space sector \(\mathbb Q^\times\backslash\mathbb A_\mathbb Q/\widehat{\mathbb Z}^*\). (connes2017geometryofthe pages 1-3, connes2017geometryofthe pages 6-8)

4. **Periodic orbits \(C_p\) and a Riemann–Roch theorem with real-valued (type II) dimensions.**
   Restricting the scaling flow to the periodic orbit associated to a prime \(p\) yields a compact quasi-tropical circle \(C_p=\mathbb R_+^*/p^\mathbb Z\) of length \(\log p\). On \(C_p\), the authors develop divisors and prove a Riemann–Roch formula where the “dimension” of \(H^0(D)\) is a **real number** \(\mathrm{Dim}_\mathbb R(H^0(D))\), defined as a limit of normalized topological dimensions; the divisor group modulo principal divisors has a real-degree component. (connes2017geometryofthe pages 3-6, connes2017geometryofthe pages 1-3)

5. **Positivity formulations are powerful but risk circularity.**
   Connes restates Weil’s criterion/RH as positivity/negativity of quadratic forms attached to the explicit formula (equivalently to the Weil distribution), e.g. conditions of the form \(W(g\star g^*)\ge 0\) for suitable test functions, and states equivalences to RH. These are rigorous reductions, but by your “positivity gate” they cannot be used as independent Hodge–Riemann positivity unless \(L\) is constructed independently from the spectrum. (connes2016anessayon pages 8-11, connes2000noncommutativegeometryyear pages 55-58)

## A precise obstruction theorem (proved) for Route C as currently built

### Theorem (Type-II dimension obstructs integer Hard Lefschetz on \(C_p\))

Let \(C_p=\mathbb R_+^*/p^\mathbb Z\) be the Connes–Consani periodic orbit quasi-tropical curve at prime \(p\), equipped with the divisor theory and Riemann–Roch theorem developed in *Geometry of the scaling site*. Assume a putative “cohomology theory” \(H^\bullet\) on \(C_p\) satisfies the following two conditions:

1. (**Compatibility with their Riemann–Roch**) For every divisor \(D\) on \(C_p\), the Euler characteristic \(\chi(D)\) computed from \(H^0(D),H^1(D)\) equals the real-valued Riemann–Roch index
\[
\chi(D)=\mathrm{Dim}_\mathbb R(H^0(D)) - \mathrm{Dim}_\mathbb R(H^0(-D)) = \deg(D),
\]
where \(\deg(D)\in\mathbb R\) is the (real) degree of \(D\). (This is exactly the theorem proved for \(C_p\) in their framework.)

2. (**Finite-type integer grading**) For each divisor \(D\), \(H^0(D)\) and \(H^1(D)\) are finite-dimensional *integer*-vector-space-like objects so that \(\dim H^i(D)\in\mathbb Z_{\ge 0}\) and hence \(\chi(D)\in\mathbb Z\).

Then these two conditions are incompatible: no such theory can exist.

#### Proof

By the divisor theory on \(C_p\), divisors can have **arbitrary real degree** (the degree map in \(\mathrm{Div}(C_p)/\mathcal P\) has image \(\mathbb R\)). (connes2017geometryofthe pages 3-6)

Their Riemann–Roch theorem states
\[
\mathrm{Dim}_\mathbb R(H^0(D)) - \mathrm{Dim}_\mathbb R(H^0(-D)) = \deg(D),
\]
with \(\deg(D)\in\mathbb R\) and \(\mathrm{Dim}_\mathbb R\) a real-valued “dimension.” (connes2017geometryofthe pages 3-6)

If a cohomology theory satisfied condition (1), then \(\chi(D)=\deg(D)\). Choosing a divisor \(D\) with \(\deg(D)=1/2\) (or any non-integer real), we obtain \(\chi(D)=1/2\).

But under condition (2), \(\chi(D)=\dim H^0(D)-\dim H^1(D)\in\mathbb Z\), a contradiction.

Therefore no cohomology theory on \(C_p\) can simultaneously (i) reproduce the proved Riemann–Roch index of Connes–Consani and (ii) be a finite-type integer-graded cohomology theory in the sense needed for lowest-weight Hard Lefschetz / Hodge–Riemann packages.

\(\square\)

#### Interpretation

- This does **not** prove that *no* future construction could yield integer Hard Lefschetz for \(\zeta\). It proves that **the currently built characteristic-one geometry on the periodic orbits \(C_p\)**, insofar as it is governed by the real-valued (type II) dimension theory that appears naturally there, cannot be upgraded to the requested integer-graded “surface cohomology” without changing the basic invariants.

- In the language of your gates: it shows a **hard integrality failure** for a major piece of Route C geometry, consistent with your warning that continuous/principal-series phenomena cannot yield a lowest-weight Hodge module.

### Adversarial self-audit (attempt to break the theorem)

- **Potential loophole:** one might argue that \(\mathrm{Dim}_\mathbb R\) is *not* intended to be an Euler characteristic of integer cohomology but rather a von Neumann dimension. Correct: the theorem’s hypothesis (2) explicitly assumes finite-dimensional/integer-graded cohomology. If one instead embraces von Neumann dimensions, then the conclusion is not a contradiction—but then **G2 (integer grading) fails**, which is exactly the point.

- **Potential loophole:** one could replace \(C_p\) by a different compactification or a different global object (e.g., a true “surface” \(S\)) where degrees become integral. This is not ruled out; it is an **OPEN escape route**, but it requires constructing new geometry beyond the current \(C_p\) framework.

Thus the obstruction is sharp but local: it blocks a direct path from the *existing* scaling-site periodic-orbit Riemann–Roch package to a classical Hard-Lefschetz/Hodge-index package.

## Gaps in the research and future directions (within the hard gates)

### Key gaps (OPEN)

The obstruction theorem shows this cannot be obtained merely by reinterpreting \(\mathrm{Dim}_\mathbb R\) as an integer dimension.

### Suggestions for future work

- **Construct a new compact/finite geometric object** whose invariants are integer-graded and which maps to the scaling-site world only after completion/realification. The obstruction suggests that the passage through type-II dimensions should be viewed as an analytic shadow, not the target cohomology itself.

- **Clarify the role of cutoffs vs geometry:** since trace formulas require IR/UV regularization (connes2000noncommutativegeometryyear pages 55-58, connes2602theriemannhypothesis pages 30-33), a successful “SURF” object would need a built-in compactness/renormalization mechanism that yields an honest trace without external cutoffs.

- **Bridge to \(\lambda\)-ring/F\(_1\) descent carefully:** Borger’s framework makes precise how Frobenius lifts serve as descent data and warns that finite-type descent is rare and cohomologically rigid (only very restricted motives descend). This suggests that any \(\mathbb F_1\)-surface supporting nontrivial \(H^1\) must live in a broader (likely derived/topos-theoretic) category than finite-type \(\mathbb F_1\)-schemes. ## Final classification

- The Connes adèle-class-space program **rigorously realizes** the explicit formula as a trace formula and provides a sophisticated characteristic-one geometric framework (arithmetic site/scaling site, periodic orbits) with deep analogies to Weil’s proof in finite characteristic. (connes2000noncommutativegeometryyear pages 55-58, connes2017geometryofthe pages 1-3)

- However, **within the presently built Route C geometry**, there is a **proved obstruction** to obtaining the required **integer-graded, lowest-weight Hard Lefschetz** package from the periodic-orbit \(C_p\) geometry, because the native Riemann–Roch theory uses real degrees and real (type-II) dimensions. (connes2017geometryofthe pages 3-6)

- The key remaining tasks to reach the prompt’s (G1–G4) are OPEN and coincide with Standard-Conjectures-type desiderata for an “absolute surface”: constructing a diagonal intersection theory with Hodge index positivity and producing an independent Lefschetz operator outside \(W^*(T)\), while also overcoming the cutoff/continuous-scaling nature of the current spectral setting. (connes2016anessayon pages 18-21, connes2000noncommutativegeometryyear pages 55-58)
