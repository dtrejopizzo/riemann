<conclusion>
The current literature provides strong local analogies between the p-adic Sen operator Θ_p and an archimedean “log-Frobenius”/scaling generator Θ_∞, but does not (in the retrieved sources) exhibit an established unified adelic/prismatic framework defining a single global operator Θ_global whose specializations recover Θ_v at all places.

<explanation>
Plan-driven comparative review was conducted by (i) retrieving and scanning foundational and modern sources on Sen theory and (φ,Γ)-modules, (ii) retrieving sources on archimedean cohomology in the style of Deninger–Consani–Marcolli/Connes, and (iii) searching for proposals of global/adelic Hodge-theoretic objects over Spec ℤ (adelic period rings, global prismatic or q-de Rham constructions, arithmetic-site structures). No statistical tests were applicable because the objective is a conceptual literature synthesis, not a quantitative meta-analysis.

1) p-adic Sen operator Θ_p (local, cyclotomic).
A precise operator-level definition appears in Sen theory as follows. For a p-adic Galois representation V, one forms a Sen module D_Sen(V) inside (C_p ⊗_{Q_p} V)^{H_K}, where H_K is the kernel of the cyclotomic character χ; D_Sen(V) is a K_∞-vector space of dimension dim(V) with C_p ⊗_{K_∞} D_Sen(V) ≅ C_p ⊗_{Q_p} V. For γ ∈ Γ (a sufficiently small open subgroup of Γ_p = Gal(K_∞/K)) close to 1, the ratio log(γ)/log(χ(γ)) is defined by a convergent power series in (1−γ), and the resulting operator converges to an endomorphism ∇^{(0)}: D_Sen(V) → D_Sen(V) independent of the choice of such γ. This operator ∇^{(0)} is the infinitesimal generator of the cyclotomic Γ-action, and is the (local) Sen operator Θ_p. (morita2007imperfectresiduefields pages 6-8)

The Sen endomorphism governs Hodge–Tate data: Shimizu recalls that the generalized Hodge–Tate weights are defined as eigenvalues of the Sen endomorphism φ_V on the decompleted module H(V)_fin (a finite-dimensional k_∞-vector space obtained from H(V) = (V ⊗ C_p)^{Gal(k/k_∞)} by Sen decompletion). (shimizu2018constancyofgeneralized pages 1-3)

In modern language, Porat also emphasizes that the eigenvalues of the canonical Sen operator encode (generalized) Hodge–Tate weights (up to sign conventions across sources) and that the Sen operator can be viewed Lie-theoretically (as an element associated to Lie(Γ) in Berger–Colmez-type generalizations). (porat2022topicsinsen pages 9-12)

2) Archimedean analogue Θ_∞ (scaling/log-Frobenius).
In the archimedean cohomology of Consani–Marcolli, an explicit infinitesimal scaling generator exists that plays the role of a “logarithm of Frobenius” at infinity. In “Archimedean cohomology revisited,” the operator Φ is given explicitly by
Φ = −U ∂/∂U,
acting on the archimedean cohomology complex T^• (and related variants such as the total complex T_u^• and its Hilbert completion). The same work describes a multiplicative scaling flow on a real parameter s by φ_t(s) = s e^{−t}; the induced infinitesimal generator on sections is −s d/ds, i.e. Υ = −s (d/ds) (up to sign convention). These operators act on global sections of a Rees sheaf interpretation of archimedean cohomology, and on the cohomology of higher inertia invariants/coinvariants complexes. The spectrum of Φ is integral in the cited model (Spec(Φ) = ℤ, with multiplicities given in the paper). (consani2006archimedeancohomologyrevisited pages 3-5, consani2006archimedeancohomologyrevisited pages 17-20, consani2006archimedeancohomologyrevisited pages 21-24)

This supplies the requested identification of Θ_∞ with a generator of an ℝ_+^* (scaling) action and, equivalently, an operator of the form s d/ds (up to sign). (consani2006archimedeancohomologyrevisited pages 17-20)

3) Search for a global/adelic object with Θ_v at all places.
The retrieved sources do contain several global-looking constructions, but none provides the specific all-places operator Θ_v on a single object that recovers both Θ_p and Θ_∞.

• “q-de Rham” / prismatic ideas over Spec ℤ: Scholze’s ICM account reports a q-de Rham cohomology theory for smooth schemes over Spec ℤ and explains its relation to A_inf-cohomology upon a specialization q ↦ [ε]. However, it also states that existing constructions are obtained by p-adic completion and then “patched together in some slightly artificial way,” and that “it is not clear how to combine them for varying p.” This directly undermines (at least at present) the existence of a clean, single global cohomology object carrying uniform Θ_v-operators for all p and for ∞. (scholze2019padicgeometry pages 25-27)

• Adelic “ring of periods” proposals: Weng proposes an adelic ring of periods B_A defined as a restricted product of local period rings (including B_dR-like objects), intended to host “adelic representations” subject to local admissibility constraints. But in the retrieved excerpt there is no operator-level construction of Θ_v, nor a specialization statement linking a global operator to Sen/log-Frobenius generators. (weng2010stabilityandarithmetic pages 3-4)

• Arithmetic site / Bost–Connes / λ-ring structures: Connes–Consani develop global arithmetic structures (sheaves of S-algebras on an Arakelov-type compactification; Γ-ring at the archimedean stalk) and describe explicit Frobenius/Verschiebung-type operators (F_n, V_n, σ_n, \tildeρ_n) with precise intertwining identities on a single algebraic object. These provide a genuine global operator calculus, but the operators are indexed by n (endomorphisms of a Witt/BC-type algebra) rather than by places v, and they are not identified with local Sen operators or with the archimedean scaling generator Φ = −U∂_U. (connes2021segalsgammarings pages 7-9, connes2021segalsgammarings pages 3-5)

Therefore, within the retrieved corpus, the hypothesis of an existing unified Θ_global whose local specializations reproduce Θ_p and Θ_∞ is not supported.

4) Objects acted upon and (lack of) compatibility/product formulas.
The place-by-place operator picture supported by evidence is:

• Finite p-adic places: Θ_p acts on a Sen module D_Sen(V) (or its geometric/family generalizations) built from the cyclotomic extension K_∞ and invariants under H_K = ker(χ). The defining formula is Θ_p = log(γ)/log(χ(γ)) (via convergent series), independent of γ near 1. (morita2007imperfectresiduefields pages 6-8)

• Relative/family p-adic setting: an arithmetic Sen endomorphism φ_L acts on the p-adic Simpson/Riemann–Hilbert output H(L) = ν′_* (\hat L ⊗ O_C), and is identified with the residue/graded-0 part of a de Rham endomorphism in a B_dR-type filtered setting (gr^0 φ_dR = φ_L). (shimizu2018constancyofgeneralized pages 3-6, shimizu2018constancyofgeneralized pages 15-18)

• Archimedean place: Θ_∞ acts on the archimedean cohomology complex/cohomology (T^•, T_u^•, Rees-sheaf global sections) as Φ = −U∂_U or equivalently Υ = −s d/ds for the scaling flow on s. (consani2006archimedeancohomologyrevisited pages 3-5, consani2006archimedeancohomologyrevisited pages 17-20)

No retrieved source states an adelic product formula tying Θ_v together (e.g., ∑_v Θ_v = 0 or ∏_v exp(…Θ_v…) = 1), nor a construction of a single global module with commuting specializations to these local operator actions. Instead, the literature emphasizes analogies (logarithmic infinitesimal generators of natural 1-parameter/group actions) and proposes global ambient structures (restricted products of period rings, arithmetic site operator families) that do not currently specialize to Sen/log-Frobenius simultaneously. (scholze2019padicgeometry pages 25-27, weng2010stabilityandarithmetic pages 3-4, connes2021segalsgammarings pages 7-9)

<discussion>
The evidence supports a robust local dictionary: Θ_p and Θ_∞ are both defined as logarithmic infinitesimal generators of canonical symmetry actions (cyclotomic Γ_p-action vs ℝ_+^* scaling) on place-specific cohomological or period-theoretic objects, and both control “weight” information (Hodge–Tate weights at p via eigenvalues of Θ_p; Γ-factors/spectral data at ∞ via Φ/Υ). (shimizu2018constancyofgeneralized pages 1-3, consani2006archimedeancohomologyrevisited pages 3-5)

However, limitations are significant. First, the tool-accessible corpus did not include some potentially relevant sources (e.g., full monographs or specific papers explicitly on “adelic (φ,Γ)-modules” if they exist under different terminology). Second, several global proposals in the literature are programmatic or heuristic: Scholze explicitly notes that current q-de Rham constructions are patched prime-by-prime and do not yet combine varying p in a natural way, which blocks a straightforward Θ_global at the present stage. (scholze2019padicgeometry pages 25-27)

Finally, Connes–Consani-type global operator formalisms (Frobenius/Verschiebung, λ-operations) are global and precise, but they do not match the Sen operator framework directly; any bridge would require new comparison functors identifying the relevant representations/cohomologies and showing that the infinitesimal generators correspond under those functors. (connes2021segalsgammarings pages 7-9, consani2006archimedeancohomologyrevisited pages 3-5)
