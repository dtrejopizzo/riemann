<conclusion>
The retrieved literature does not provide an explicit, published formula equating the archimedean Gillet–Soulé/Arakelov Green-function pairing \(\int g_y\,d\mu_x\) (or \(\int g_y\log|s|\)) on an arithmetic surface with a Wodzicki/noncommutative residue \(\mathrm{Res}(\Phi_x,\Phi_y)\) of divisor-associated pseudodifferential operators, although it does establish closely related residue–zeta/heat/log identities for Green-type operators.
</conclusion>
<explanation>
The hypothesis asks for a theorem identifying an Arakelov Green-current integral (archimedean contribution to arithmetic intersection) with a residue trace of pseudodifferential operators built from divisor/spectral triple data. In the retrieved corpus, the strongest rigorously explicit identities are instead of the following types.

1) Foundational residue trace identities (explicit and standard). Connes, Lesch, and Schrohe give equivalent characterizations of the Wodzicki residue (noncommutative residue) in terms of (i) the \(\log t\) coefficient in the heat-trace asymptotics and (ii) the residue at \(s=0\) of zeta-type traces \(\mathrm{Tr}(P A^{-s})\), together with the local cosphere-bundle integral formula for the order \(-n\) symbol term (connes1988theactionfunctional pages 1-4, lesch1997onthenoncommutative pages 1-3, schrohe1999noncommutativeresiduesdixmiers pages 3-6). Connes’ trace theorem identifies, for order \(-n\) classical pseudodifferential operators, the Dixmier trace with a dimensional constant times the Wodzicki residue, supplying the “NC integral” mechanism (connes1988theactionfunctional pages 1-4).

2) Residues of inverse powers of Laplacians/Dirac operators (Green-operator relevance, but not Arakelov). Ackermann states explicit formulas relating residues of powers of an elliptic operator to heat-kernel coefficients, e.g. \(a_k(P)\) expressed via \(\mathrm{res}(P^{-(n-k)/d})\) (ackermann1996anoteon pages 1-4). Kalau–Walze show for Dirac operators in even dimensions \(n\ge 4\) that \(\mathrm{Res}(D^{-n+2})\) captures the second heat coefficient and yields the Einstein–Hilbert action (kalau1995gravitynoncommutativegeometry pages 1-4). These results demonstrate that residues of inverse-type operators (close cousins of Green operators) encode geometric integrals, but they do not treat divisor Green currents or Arakelov intersection pairings.

3) Explicit “Green-type contribution = residue” formulas exist, but in a different sense of ‘Green’. Schrohe proves that in Boutet de Monvel’s boundary calculus the residue of an operator includes terms that are integrals of the traces of singular Green symbols and boundary symbol components; in particular, the residue (a trace) decomposes into an interior cosphere integral plus boundary integrals of Green-symbol traces (schrohe1999noncommutativeresiduesdixmiers pages 12-15). Moreover, Schrohe identifies the residue with the coefficient of \(\log t\) in a heat-trace expansion, again involving the Green-symbol trace contributions (schrohe1999noncommutativeresiduesdixmiers pages 24-26). This gives a rigorous “integral of Green-symbol trace equals residue” statement, but it concerns boundary value problems and singular Green operators in pseudodifferential calculus, not Arakelov Green functions \(g(x,y)\) on compact Riemann surfaces and their appearance in arithmetic intersection theory.

4) Green functions on Riemann surfaces and residues: partial analytic bridge. Estrada–Gracia-Bondía–Várilly analyze counting functionals and Green functions and state that diagonal asymptotic expansion coefficients can be computed in terms of noncommutative residues of suitable operator powers (estrada1998onsummabilityof pages 1-3). Okikiolu discusses canonical traces and determinant identities such as \(\det Q = \mathrm{TR}(\log Q)\) and situates these in the context of determinants and (in related literature) regular parts of Green functions (okikiolu2001criticalmetricsfor pages 1-4). These results support the plausibility of translating certain regularized Green-function invariants into residue/canonical-trace language, but they still do not present the specific Arakelov intersection functional \(\int g_y d\mu_x\) as a noncommutative residue pairing of divisor operators.

5) NCG–Arakelov interface: conceptual, not formula-level for residues of divisor operators. Consani–Marcolli construct spectral-triple-type data and express archimedean \(\Gamma\)-factors via regularized determinants/zeta functions of an operator \(\Phi\); they also recall that archimedean Arakelov contributions are described by Green functions on \(X(\mathbb C)\) (consani2004noncommutativegeometrydynamics pages 3-4, consani2004noncommutativegeometrydynamics pages 19-21). However, the retrieved segments do not supply a theorem that turns the Arakelov Green-current integral itself into a Wodzicki/Dixmier residue, nor a construction of divisor-associated pseudodifferential operators \(\Phi_x\) whose residue pairing equals the Gillet–Soulé archimedean term (consani2004noncommutativegeometrydynamics pages 3-4, consani2002newperspectivesin pages 18-19).

No statistical tests were performed, because the objective is to locate explicit theorems/formulas in the literature rather than to aggregate quantitative experimental results. The main scientific implication is that, within the retrieved sources, residue traces naturally capture (a) log-terms in heat expansions and (b) regularized spectral invariants (determinants, \(\Gamma\)-factors), and they also capture certain Green-operator symbol traces in boundary calculi, but an explicit identification with Arakelov intersection Green-current integrals is not present.
