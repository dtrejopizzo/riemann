<conclusion>
The available literature supports the hypothesis at the level of structural evidence: the Arakelov cup-product/Lefschetz operator L=∪ĉ₁ is defined from local geometric/metric data and satisfies operator relations and locality features that are not naturally determined by (or recoverable from) a zeta/explicit-formula spectral operator and its generated von Neumann algebra, although a definitive “L∉W*(T)” theorem was not found in the retrieved sources.

<explanation>
1) Operator-algebraic side (Weil explicit formula / “spectral operator” frameworks)

• Spectral-operator models encoding ζ-data can be made completely explicit. In the Herichi–Lapidus framework, the “spectral operator” is defined as a=ζ(∂c) using measurable functional calculus for an unbounded normal operator ∂c (“infinitesimal shift”) on a weighted Hilbert space Hc. They compute its spectrum via a spectral mapping theorem as σ(a)=cl ζ({λ:Re(λ)=c}), and characterize quasi-invertibility of a in terms of the absence of zeros of ζ on the vertical line Re(s)=c; thus the operator’s spectral properties are directly controlled by global analytic ζ-data (and hence, indirectly, by its zeros). (herichi2012riemannzerosand pages 21-25)

• Independently, Connes/Meyer-type approaches recast the Weil explicit formula as a trace/character formula for representations of the idèle class group CK. Meyer constructs a virtual representation π whose spectral multiplicities match the order of poles/zeros of a completed L-function LK, and whose character equals the (raw) Weil distribution from the explicit formula. This again makes the “spectral” object fundamentally global and encoded by the analytic behavior (zeros/poles) of LK. (meyer2005onarepresentation pages 1-2)

• Limitation: within retrieved texts, no source explicitly introduces an operator denoted T together with an explicitly named von Neumann algebra W*(T); therefore the discussion of W*(T) can only be abstract (the von Neumann algebra generated by a normal/self-adjoint operator via functional calculus) and cannot be tied to a single canonical “T” definition.

2) Arakelov / archimedean cohomology side (cup products with arithmetic Chern classes)

• The arithmetic first Chern class has an explicit decomposition into algebraic and archimedean metric/Green data. Iyengar–Soulé define the arithmetic Chern class of a Hermitian line bundle H by ĉ1(H)=[(div s,−log∥s∥2)] and define the Lefschetz operator L as intersection with ĉ1(H). The archimedean metric term −log∥s∥2 appears directly in the operator’s definition, evidencing dependence on local (archimedean) geometry rather than global ζ-zero data. (iyengar2017theclassicaland pages 47-47)

• In archimedean cohomology, L sits in a geometric operator package (L,N,Φ) with explicit commutation relations and adjoints depending on a chosen Kähler form. Consani–Marcolli define L as wedge with the Kähler form (up to an operator U−1), and obtain an sl(2) structure where L is a raising operator with commutator [Φ,L]=L; they also give an explicit adjoint formula L*=(·⌋ω)U, showing L* depends on the Kähler form ω. Φ (log Frobenius) has integer spectrum Spec(Φ)=Z. These features characterize L by local Hodge/Lefschetz geometry and (archimedean) spectral-triple data, rather than by global zero sets. (consani2006archimedeancohomologyrevisited pages 3-5, consani2006archimedeancohomologyrevisited pages 21-24)

• Zhang’s Lefschetz-module approach makes the “local correction” aspect explicit: admissible (Arakelov) classes are those whose curvature ω(x) is harmonic, and any cycle class can be modified by adding vertical cycles on special fibers to become admissible. Canonical liftings and decompositions often require smoothness/semistability hypotheses or standard conjectures. Under these conjectures, Zhang constructs splittings of filtrations and controls L-linearity only modulo lower pieces, with an adjoint Λ entering the formalism. This expresses L not as a single global spectral invariant but as an operator whose definition and canonicality require placewise (finite + infinite) geometric input and sometimes conjectural structure. (zhang2020standardconjecturesand pages 22-25, zhang2020standardconjecturesand pages 3-7, zhang2020standardconjecturesand pages 25-29)

3) “No-go” or distinguishing criteria found

• A concrete obstruction exists in arithmetic Chow theory: Bachmat proves that there is no arithmetic correspondence AF on arithmetic Chow groups of an abelian scheme satisfying key Fourier-transform-like properties (in particular those that would make it a ring isomorphism between intersection and Pontryagin products). The obstruction is structural: in arithmetic intersection theory the identity correspondence is not represented by a cycle in A×A, so there is no unit for the Pontryagin product, contradicting what an “arithmetic Fourier transform” would force. This is an explicit no-go theorem showing that arithmetic/Arakelov correspondences can fail to admit the same algebraic “spectral transform” structure that underlies many operator-algebraic spectral constructions. (bachmat1994onthearakelov pages 13-17, bachmat1994onthearakelova pages 13-17)

• Trace-formula/cyclic-homology frameworks exhibit nonuniqueness/degeneracy that complicates extracting geometric operators: Khalkhali–Marcolli describe a large subspace (radical) of correspondences with vanishing trace, allowing degrees/codegrees to be modified by adding radical elements (linked to a subtle failure of Fubini). This indicates that even if an operator algebra realizes the explicit formula, it can have a “huge radical” of trace-invisible elements, undermining uniqueness of recovering geometric correspondences from spectral trace data alone. (khalkhali2008aninvitationto pages 95-98)

4) Implications for the research hypothesis

• The hypothesis claims L=∪ĉ₁ on an Arakelov cohomology for Spec Z is independent of the spectral operator T from the Weil explicit formula, because L depends on local metric data while T depends on global zeros {γρ}. The evidence supports the key mechanism: ĉ1(H) explicitly contains the archimedean metric term −log∥s∥2, and the associated Lefschetz operators participate in geometric commutation packages (sl(2), adjoint formulas depending on ω, local harmonic/vertical corrections) that are not specified by the global ζ-zero set alone. (iyengar2017theclassicaland pages 47-47, consani2006archimedeancohomologyrevisited pages 21-24, zhang2020standardconjecturesand pages 22-25)

• Conversely, the available spectral-operator constructions encode ζ-data in a way that is explicitly governed by ζ(s) along vertical lines and thus by global analytic information about ζ (including its zeros), as in a=ζ(∂c) and in the idèle-class virtual representation whose multiplicities are ord(ω,LK). (herichi2012riemannzerosand pages 21-25, meyer2005onarepresentation pages 1-2)

• Because the retrieved sources do not provide an explicit, standard definition of “T” and W*(T) in the exact sense used in the prompt, no formal proof of independence (e.g., showing L is not a bounded Borel functional calculus of T, or does not lie in W*(T), or fails commutation relations required of W*(T)) could be extracted. Therefore, the conclusion is supported by structural and “incompatibility” evidence rather than a fully formal no-membership theorem.

This yields credible support for the intuition that “L is local-geometric while T is global-spectral.” However, three important limitations remain.

Second, while several operator-theoretic relations are available for L in archimedean cohomology (sl(2) action, [Φ,L]=L, explicit adjoint formula), the analytic properties suggested in the prompt (e.g., compactness/trace-class of L, fine restriction behavior between finite vs infinite places) were not found as explicit theorems for L itself in the retrieved pages. The compact-resolvent statement applies to the Dirac-type operator D in the spectral triple rather than to L. (consani2006archimedeancohomologyrevisited pages 21-24)

Third, much of the global Arakelov Lefschetz formalism is conditional on standard conjectures (Hard Lefschetz, positivity), which complicates turning “locality/metric dependence” into a rigorous operator-algebraic independence statement. (iyengar2017theclassicaland pages 47-47, zhang2020standardconjecturesand pages 25-29)

Despite these limitations, the presence of a concrete arithmetic no-go theorem (nonexistence of an arithmetic Fourier correspondence with classical properties) and the explicit dependence of ĉ1(H) on local metric data are strong indicators that the geometric operator L is not naturally generated solely from explicit-formula spectral data.
