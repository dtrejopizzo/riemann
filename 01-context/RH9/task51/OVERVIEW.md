Geometric representation theory provides a particularly direct match to the hypothesis via the relative/absolute Hard Lefschetz formalism of Hosono–Saito–Takahashi (HST). For a projective morphism \(f:X\to Y\), HST construct two Lefschetz actions coming from two Chern classes: a relative class \(\omega_L\) (fiber direction) and a class \(\omega_R\) pulled back from the base. Using the Decomposition Theorem and relative hard Lefschetz, they obtain commuting actions \((sl_2)_L\times (sl_2)_R\) on intersection cohomology, and they state that these two actions “commute” and thus define a joint \((sl_2)_L\times(sl_2)_R\)-action. This commuting pair yields a decomposition of the cohomology into a direct sum of tensor products \(IH^*(\widetilde M_\beta)\cong\bigoplus_h I_h\otimes R_h(\beta)\), where the left Lefschetz action is on \(I_h\) and the right action is on \(R_h(\beta)\). Concretely, their toy example \(H^*(\widehat{\mathbf P}^2)\simeq (0)_L\otimes[(1)_R\oplus(0)_R]\) exhibits a summand on which the left Lefschetz action is trivial while the right action remains nontrivial, i.e. a literal instance of “\(L\) acts as \(L_H\otimes id_V\)” on a decomposed factor. (hosono2001relativelefschetzaction pages 4-7, hosono2001relativelefschetzaction pages 7-10)

Crucially for objective (4), HST use this decoupled left/right structure to define integer enumerative/BPS invariants by inserting only the right-factor Cartan element in a graded trace: \(n_h(\beta):=\mathrm{Tr}_{R_h(\beta)}((-1)^{2H_R})\). Here the left \((sl_2)_L\) structure controls the decomposition into \(I_h\) (geometric “spin” multiplets), while the actual integer is extracted purely from the auxiliary/right factor \(R_h(\beta)\) by a trace weighted by \(H_R\). This is an explicit realization of the hypothesis’ pattern in which a primary Lefschetz \(sl_2\) is compatible with a second grading living on an auxiliary factor, with the compatibility \([L_H, T_V]=0\) being essentially built into the tensor-factor action, yet yielding meaningful invariants (linked to the Gopakumar–Vafa program and BPS counting). (hosono2001relativelefschetzaction pages 1-4, hosono2001relativelefschetzaction pages 7-10)

A second geometric-representation-theoretic near-match occurs in the quiver-variety approach to quantum affine algebras. Zegers proves that a Hard Lefschetz theorem for Chern classes of tautological sheaves yields a canonical \(U(sl_2)\)-module structure on each \(\ell\)-weight space (via primitive decomposition of the relevant cohomology). Independently, the Heisenberg subalgebra induces a Jordan filtration on these \(\ell\)-weight spaces, and the paper relates that filtration to the Lefschetz \(sl_2\)-module structure and connects both to refined \(q,t\)-character data and Poincaré polynomials. This is not always written as an explicit \(H\otimes V\) factorization with a displayed commutator, but it is a concrete instance of a Lefschetz \(sl_2\) action structurally separated from a second (Heisenberg/Jordan) “weight/grade” structure that encodes spectral/refined information. (zegers2015qtcharactersand pages 1-3)

In QFT/CFT, explicit tensor product decompositions and commuting actions appear in the chiral factorization of 2D conformal field theory. Kusuki writes the full CFT Hilbert space as a direct sum of tensor products of chiral (holomorphic) and anti-chiral (antiholomorphic) modules: \(\mathcal H_{\mathrm{CFT}}=\bigoplus_{(h,\bar h)\in S} M_{h,\bar h}\,V_h\otimes \bar V_{\bar h}\). In this setup, the Virasoro modes \(L_n\) generate descendants within \(V_h\), while \(\bar L_n\) act within \(\bar V_{\bar h}\), and spectral data like scaling dimension and spin are extracted from \(L_0\pm\bar L_0\). Although Kusuki’s excerpt does not explicitly print \([L_n,\bar L_m]=0\), the tensor-product structure makes such commutation a trivial consequence of factorized action (the archetype of \([L_H\otimes id, id\otimes T_V]=0\)). (kusuki2412modernapproachto pages 14-17, kusuki2412modernapproachto pages 20-22)

Kawai’s BCFT free-field formalism gives an even more explicit commutation statement across factors: holomorphic and antiholomorphic mode algebras are treated as independent copies, and cross-commutators between holomorphic and antiholomorphic zero-modes vanish, e.g. \([\phi_0,\bar a_0]=[\bar\phi_0,a_0]=0\). This is a direct, text-level example of the objective’s requested situation where the commutator vanishes “for domain reasons” (different sectors/factors) but is still structurally meaningful because boundary conditions glue the two sectors (e.g. via \((L_k-\bar L_{-k})|B\rangle=0\)). (kawai2002boundaryconformalfield pages 60-64)

Finally, in BPS/refined index physics, Galakhov presents the protected spin character/refined BPS index as a graded trace inserting commuting Cartan generators for different symmetry factors: \(\Omega(\gamma;u;q)=\mathrm{Tr}_{\mathcal H_{\mathrm{BPS}}} q^{2J_3}(-q)^{2I_3}\), where \(J_3\) is the Cartan of spatial spin \(so(3)\) and \(I_3\) is the Cartan of \(su(2)_R\). This illustrates the same conceptual pattern as HST (a decomposition/compatibility of distinct symmetry factors, with spectral/refined data extracted via a trace over inserted Cartan elements) in a clearly QFT setting. (galakhov2016interfacesinsupersymmetric pages 15-18)

<discussion>
The strongest direct confirmation of the research hypothesis is the HST geometric framework, where the commuting \((sl_2)_L\times(sl_2)_R\) action and the decomposition \(\bigoplus_h I_h\otimes R_h\) are explicitly asserted and then used to define integer BPS invariants by a trace over only the “auxiliary” right factor; this is essentially the precise mathematical incarnation of “Lefschetz on \(H\) + Tate/weight operator on \(V\), commuting by factorization,” with nontrivial consequences for enumerative geometry and BPS counting. (hosono2001relativelefschetzaction pages 1-4, hosono2001relativelefschetzaction pages 7-10)

Limitations and shortfalls:
1. Physical “Hilbert space = \(H\otimes V\)” with an explicit Lefschetz \(sl_2\) triple on \(H\) is less directly documented in the retrieved QFT/string sources than in HST; the CFT examples give very explicit factorization and commutation, but with Virasoro rather than Lefschetz \(sl_2\). (kawai2002boundaryconformalfield pages 60-64, kusuki2412modernapproachto pages 14-17)
2. Some geometric representation theory sources (e.g. Zegers) provide a compelling analogue—Lefschetz \(sl_2\) plus an auxiliary filtration/Heisenberg action used to encode refined data—but do not always present the structure literally as a strict tensor product with an explicit displayed commutator \([L_H, T_V]=0\). (zegers2015qtcharactersand pages 1-3)
3. The connection to arithmetic data in the narrow sense (e.g. Frobenius eigenvalues, \(\ell\)-adic weights, Galois actions) was not strongly evidenced in the retrieved corpus; the clearest “arithmetic-like” role is played by graded traces (indices) and refined generating functions (GV/BPS, \(q,t\)-characters), which are spectral/enumerative rather than directly Galois-arithmetic in the cited texts. (hosono2001relativelefschetzaction pages 1-4, zegers2015qtcharactersand pages 1-3)
</discussion>
