<conclusion>
The surveyed Bost–Connes and related number-theoretic QSM models do not (in the retrieved literature) exhibit an algebra-internal, integer-graded \(\mathfrak{sl}_2\)-action with a grading operator \(H\) of discrete integer spectrum by construction; instead, their canonical generators have discrete but logarithmic spectra realized in positive-energy representations.
</conclusion>
<explanation>
This survey focused on (i) algebraic definitions of the observable algebras, (ii) the presence of candidate operators resembling a Hard Lefschetz triple \((L,\Lambda,H)\), (iii) especially an operator \(H\) with discrete integer spectrum to induce an integer grading, and (iv) links to the Weil explicit formula.

Algebraic definitions (BC and key generalizations).  Modern expositions present the Bost–Connes (BC) observable algebra as the semigroup crossed product \(A = C^*(\mathbf{Q}/\mathbf{Z})\rtimes \mathbf{N}^\times\), equivalently as a groupoid/Hecke-type \(C^*\)-algebra, generated by elements \(e(r)\) (\(r\in \mathbf{Q}/\mathbf{Z}\)) and isometries \(\mu_n\) (\(n\in \mathbf{N}^\times\)) subject to explicit covariance relations; a dense arithmetic subalgebra is often taken to be \(\mathbf{Q}[\mathbf{Q}/\mathbf{Z}]\) or a rational subalgebra stable under the time evolution (shirbisheh2008quantumstatisticalmechanics pages 1-4, connes2006kmsstatesand pages 5-7).  Connes also describes an integral/arithmetic incarnation of the BC system in terms of Witt functors, matching BC endomorphisms to Frobenius/Verschiebung operations, and presents a Hecke-algebra viewpoint \(H_\mathbf{Q}(\Gamma,\Gamma_0)\) underlying the construction (connes2011thebcsystemand pages 1-3, connes2011thebcsystemand pages 3-6).

For the Connes–Marcolli \(\mathrm{GL}_2\) / \(\mathbf{Q}\)-lattice system, the observable algebra is given as the completion of a convolution algebra on the commensurability groupoid of \(\mathbf{Q}\)-lattices modulo scaling, with a rational arithmetic subalgebra of (typically unbounded) multipliers stable under the time evolution and encoding modular/Hecke data (connes2006kmsstatesand pages 5-7, shirbisheh2008quantumstatisticalmechanics pages 7-10, connes2006kmsstatesand pages 12-14).  Function-field analogues and other BC-type systems similarly use groupoid-style convolution algebras and arithmetic subalgebras over suitable base fields, with time evolution defined by ideal/lattice covolume data and symmetries recovering class field theory actions (consani2007quantumstatisticalmechanics pages 1-3).

Canonical time evolutions and the spectrum of the implementing Hamiltonian.  In the BC system the time evolution is explicit on generators: \(\sigma_t(\mu_n)=n^{it}\mu_n\) and \(\sigma_t(e(r))=e(r)\) (shirbisheh2008quantumstatisticalmechanics pages 1-4, connes2004fromphysicsto pages 23-26).  In standard positive-energy representations, the self-adjoint generator \(H\) implementing \(\sigma_t\) therefore has eigenvalues \(\log n\) (because \(n^{it}=e^{it\log n}\)), and the partition function becomes \(Z(\beta)=\zeta(\beta)\) (connes2004fromphysicsto pages 19-23, connes2004fromphysicsto pages 23-26).  Importantly for the hypothesis, the evidence places this \(H\) primarily as an unbounded operator implementing the dynamics in representations (or in a multiplier/von Neumann context), rather than as a bounded element explicitly inside the \(C^*\)-algebra of observables (connes2004fromphysicsto pages 26-30, connes2004fromphysicsto pages 19-23).

In the \(\mathrm{GL}_2\) \(\mathbf{Q}\)-lattice system, the time evolution is \(\sigma_t(f)(g,\rho,z)=\det(g)^{it} f(g,\rho,z)\) on the dense convolution algebra (shirbisheh2008quantumstatisticalmechanics pages 7-10).  In positive-energy representations attached to invertible \(\mathbf{Q}\)-lattices, the Hamiltonian spectrum is described as \(\{\log\det(m)\mid m\in \Gamma\backslash M_2(\mathbf{Z})^+\}\), again discrete but logarithmic rather than integer-valued; the partition function is \(\zeta(\beta)\zeta(\beta-1)\) (connes2006kmsstatesand pages 12-14).

Assessment of the integrality gate and Hard Lefschetz triple criterion.  The research hypothesis requires an algebraic structure supporting a discrete, integer-graded \(\mathfrak{sl}_2\)-action with a grading operator \(H\) of integer spectrum (independent of analytic/thermodynamic realizations).  Across the retrieved BC and \(\mathrm{GL}_2\) sources, the only canonical grading-like generator is the Hamiltonian implementing the time evolution, whose spectrum is built from \(\log n\), \(\log\det(m)\), or log-norm data (connes2004fromphysicsto pages 19-23, connes2006kmsstatesand pages 12-14, connes2004fromphysicsto pages 23-26).  No retrieved text provides an explicit \(\mathfrak{sl}_2\) or Hard Lefschetz triple \((L,\Lambda,H)\) acting on the observable algebra or its dense arithmetic subalgebra, nor any claim of an intrinsic grading operator \(H\) with discrete integer spectrum “by construction.” Targeted searches for explicit occurrences of “sl2”, “Hard Lefschetz”, or a Lefschetz operator yielded no relevant evidence in the retrieved corpus.

Connections to the Weil explicit formula and Lefschetz trace formulas.  While an internal Hard Lefschetz triple was not found, there is substantial evidence that BC/endomotive/adèle-class-space constructions connect to Weil explicit formulas through trace formulas and Lefschetz-type fixed-point formulas.  In Connes–Consani–Marcolli, the geometric side of the trace formula for the action of the idèle class group on the adèle class space is described as coming from an Atiyah–Bott (and related) Lefschetz formula, and the Riemann hypothesis is reformulated as positivity of an induced trace pairing (connes2007noncommutativegeometryand pages 41-44, khalkhali2008aninvitationto pages 95-98).  A parallel cohomological mechanism uses cyclic modules/homology: a “cooling and distillation” map produces a cyclic cokernel \(D(A,\varphi)\) with an \(\mathbf{R}_+^*\) scaling action whose cyclic homology is proposed as a Frobenius analogue; a Lefschetz trace formula for this scaling action is asserted to yield a cohomological interpretation of the spectral realization of zeta zeros (connes2010theweilproof pages 13-16, connes2010theweilproof pages 1-3).  The retrieved visual “tentative dictionary” makes explicit the intended correspondence between Weil’s proof ingredients (Frobenius correspondences, Riemann–Roch, Lefschetz formula) and noncommutative-geometric counterparts (scaling correspondences, index theorem, bivariant Chern character localization) (connes2010theweilproof media 36fa0e4f).

Statistical tests performed.  No inferential statistical tests (e.g., hypothesis tests, regression) were applicable, because the objective is a structural/mathematical literature survey rather than an analysis of numerical experimental data. The evaluation instead used an evidence-based “existence test”: we searched for explicit constructions or statements of (1) algebra-internal \(\mathfrak{sl}_2\)-actions, (2) Hard Lefschetz triples, and (3) integer-spectrum grading operators \(H\). The retrieved evidence consistently supported logarithmic-energy generators in representations and Lefschetz-type trace formulas at the level of cyclic homology/cohomological constructions, but did not provide the required integer-spectrum grading operator within the observable algebras themselves (connes2004fromphysicsto pages 19-23, connes2006kmsstatesand pages 12-14, connes2007noncommutativegeometryand pages 41-44, connes2010theweilproof pages 13-16).

Scientific implications.  The surveyed literature suggests that (a) the “dynamics side” of BC-type QSM systems naturally produces discrete spectra tied to arithmetic multiplicative data through logarithms, and (b) the “explicit formula side” is realized via Lefschetz-type trace formulas and cyclic-homology/cohomological constructions on objects derived from QSM systems (cooling/distillation, restriction cokernels) rather than via an explicit Hard Lefschetz \(\mathfrak{sl}_2\)-action internal to the observable algebra. Therefore, if an integrality gate is required (integer grading by construction), it likely demands an additional structure (e.g., a new cohomological grading or categorical/motivic enhancement) beyond the standard Hamiltonian/time-evolution generator.

<discussion>
Scope and limitations.  The negative conclusion is limited to the retrieved corpus and to evidence explicitly present in the examined sections. Many foundational papers (including the original Bost–Connes paper and some monographs) were not directly retrieved in full here, and some later developments (e.g., deeper treatments of “primitive cohomology” alluded to by Connes–Consani–Marcolli) may contain additional structures not captured in the sampled pages (connes2010theweilproof pages 1-3). Nonetheless, the core structural features of BC/\(\mathrm{GL}_2\) systems—crossed-product/Hecke algebra descriptions and logarithmic time evolutions—are consistent across multiple independent sources, making it unlikely that an integer-spectrum grading operator exists already at the level of the standard observable algebra without being prominently discussed.

Interpretational caveat about ‘integer spectrum’.  The canonical Hamiltonians in BC-type systems have discrete spectra indexed by arithmetic objects (positive integers, determinants, ideal norms), but the eigenvalues themselves are logarithms (\(\log n\), \(\log\det\)), not integers (connes2004fromphysicsto pages 23-26, connes2006kmsstatesand pages 12-14). One can formally obtain integers by composing with an exponential or choosing a different generator, but the generator of the \(\mathbf{R}\)-time evolution is fixed by \(\sigma_t\) and yields logarithmic eigenvalues; moreover, the implementing operator is typically unbounded and representation-dependent (connes2004fromphysicsto pages 26-30, connes2004fromphysicsto pages 19-23). Thus, “integrality by construction” is not achieved in the straightforward operator-algebraic formulation.

Where the Hard Lefschetz analogy does appear.  The strongest Lefschetz-type statements occur in the trace-formula program for the adèle class space and in cyclic-homology realizations of explicit formulas, where correspondences have degrees/codegrees and RH becomes positivity of a trace pairing (connes2007noncommutativegeometryand pages 41-44, khalkhali2008aninvitationto pages 95-98). This is compatible with a future Hard Lefschetz-like structure, but the retrieved literature does not currently identify operators \(L,\Lambda,H\) with integer grading on these cohomological objects.
