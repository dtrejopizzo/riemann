1) Precise ARR statement and constituents (Gillet–Soulé 1992).

Gillet–Soulé’s Arithmetic Riemann–Roch (their Theorem 7) is stated for a projective morphism \(f:X\to Y\) of arithmetic varieties with \(f\) smooth on the generic fiber and a Kähler metric on the relative tangent bundle \(T_f\). For a hermitian coherent sheaf \(\bar{\mathcal F}\) (case (i), \(Y\) regular) they obtain an identity in \(\widehat{CH}^1(Y)_\mathbb Q\) (their eq. (11)), expressing the arithmetic class of the determinant of cohomology \(\lambda(\mathcal F)=\det Rf_*(\mathcal F)\) equipped with the Quillen metric \(h_Q\) in terms of an arithmetic pushforward \(f_*\) applied to a Riemann–Roch transform \(\widehat r(\mathcal F)\) plus correction terms built from \(\operatorname{ch}(\mathcal F_\mathbb C)\), the Todd class \(\operatorname{Td}(f_\mathbb C)\), and secondary (Bott–Chern) data (gillet1992anarithmeticriemannroch pages 53-56, gillet1992anarithmeticriemannroch pages 49-53).

Key constituents explicitly identified in the paper include:

• Arithmetic degree / pushforward to \(\widehat{CH}^1(\mathrm{Spec}\,\mathbb Z)\cong\mathbb R\): Gillet–Soulé explain that codimension-one cycles on \(\mathrm{Spec}\,\mathbb Z\) correspond to \(\sum_p n_p\log p + \lambda/2\) under this identification (gillet1992anarithmeticriemannroch pages 53-56).

• Analytic torsion \(T(X,E)\) and Quillen metric: they define Ray–Singer analytic torsion using the zeta function \(\zeta_q(s)=\mathrm{Tr}(\Delta_q^{-s}|_{\ker\Delta_q^\perp})\) and set \(T(X,E)=\sum_{q\ge 0}(-1)^{q+1}q\,\zeta_q'(0)\), with Quillen metric \(h_Q=h_{L^2}\exp(T(X,E))\) on the determinant line (gillet1992anarithmeticriemannroch pages 49-53).

• Bott–Chern secondary Todd class: they introduce a Bott–Chern secondary Todd class \(\mathrm{Td}'(f)\) attached to the exact sequence \(0\to T_f\to T_X\to f^*T_Y\to 0\) (pqac-00000006; see also the Bott–Chern/Todd discussion used in their RR construction (gillet1992anarithmeticriemannroch pages 42-46)).

• \(R\)-genus term: they define an additive characteristic class \(R(T_f)\) (contravariant, additive on exact sequences) whose expression for a line bundle involves a power series with coefficients built from \(\zeta'(-m)\) and related constants (gillet1992anarithmeticriemannroch pages 49-53). This \(R\)-term appears in the Spec\((\mathbb Z)\) specialization.

Specialization to \(Y=\mathrm{Spec}\,\mathbb Z\). Gillet–Soulé explicitly note that Theorem 7(i) becomes an “arithmetic Hirzebruch–Riemann–Roch” equality of real numbers. Writing \(Z_Q(\mathcal F)\) for the arithmetic Euler characteristic (the arithmetic first Chern class of \((\lambda(\mathcal F),h_Q)\)), their eq. (15) expresses \(Z_Q(\mathcal F)\) as a pushforward of \(\widehat r(\mathcal F)\) minus an archimedean correction term of the form \(a(\operatorname{ch}(\mathcal F_\mathbb C)\operatorname{Td}(f_\mathbb C)R(f_\mathbb C))\) (gillet1992anarithmeticriemannroch pages 53-56). This is the cleanest decomposition into “finite arithmetic pushforward” + “archimedean analytic correction” that could plausibly be compared to the finite-prime + gamma-factor splitting in Weil’s explicit formula.

2) Finite-prime contributions vs. von Mangoldt weights.

On the ARR side, pushforward/arithmetic degree to \(\widehat{CH}^1(\mathrm{Spec}\,\mathbb Z)\) weights finite codimension-one data by \(\log p\) (more generally \(\log\#k(x)\) for a closed point \(x\)) because \(\widehat{CH}^1(\mathrm{Spec}\,\mathbb Z)\cong\mathbb R\) identifies a cycle \(\sum n_p[p]\) with \(\sum n_p\log p\) (gillet1992anarithmeticriemannroch pages 53-56). For a closed point with residue field \(k(x)=\mathbb F_{p^m}\), \(\log\#k(x)=m\log p\), which is closely related to (but not equal to) the von Mangoldt weight \(\Lambda(p^m)=\log p\).

On the explicit-formula side, Connes’ presentation of Weil’s explicit formula shows that the local non-archimedean contribution can be written as a principal-value integral over \(k_v^*\) with kernel \(1/|1-u|\) (connes1999traceformulain pages 69-73). His local analysis decomposes the integral into annuli \(\pi^nR^*\) and produces \(\log q\) factors (with \(q\) the residue field size), i.e. the same logarithmic local size that underlies prime(-power) weights (connes1999traceformulain pages 69-73).

Assessment of the finite-prime match: the evidence supports the hypothesis at the level of “logarithmic weighting at finite places” and “prime-power structure emerging from local decompositions” (gillet1992anarithmeticriemannroch pages 53-56, connes1999traceformulain pages 69-73). However, it does not (in the retrieved text) provide an explicit derivation that Gillet–Soulé’s finite intersection contributions for a specific \(\bar L\) reorganize exactly into the Weil explicit formula’s \(\sum_{n\ge 1}\Lambda(n)\,\phi(\log n)\)-type sum. The main technical mismatch visible from the evidence is the appearance of \(m\log p\) for degree-\(m\) residue fields on the ARR side versus \(\log p\) in \(\Lambda(p^m)\) (gillet1992anarithmeticriemannroch pages 53-56).

3) Archimedean contributions: torsion/determinants, gamma factors, and \(\Psi\)-type distributions.

ARR archimedean input. In Gillet–Soulé, the archimedean contribution enters through the Quillen metric \(h_Q\), i.e. through analytic torsion defined from zeta-regularized determinants of Laplacians on \((0,q)\)-forms (gillet1992anarithmeticriemannroch pages 49-53). In the Spec\((\mathbb Z)\) specialization, an additional archimedean correction is encoded by the \(R\)-genus characteristic class \(R(T_f)\), whose coefficients involve special values/derivatives of the Riemann zeta function (gillet1992anarithmeticriemannroch pages 49-53).

Explicit-formula archimedean term. Connes writes Weil’s explicit formula as a distributional identity \(\Delta=\log|d|^{-1}\delta_1 + D - \sum_v D_v\) where the archimedean components are explicit principal-value distributions plus constants involving \(\log(2\pi)\) and Euler’s constant (connes1999traceformulain pages 69-73, connes1999traceformulain pages 73-77). This provides a concrete realization of the “gamma-factor/\(\Psi\)” distributional side in terms of Fourier transforms and Pfw prescriptions.

Bridge literature. Deninger’s “Arithmetic geometry and analysis on foliated spaces” develops a trace-formula framework where primes correspond to closed orbits (length \(\log N\mathfrak p\)) and archimedean places correspond to fixed points; he writes explicit-formula-type distributional traces as sums over spectral data and periodic orbit/fixed-point terms (deninger2005arithmeticgeometryand pages 25-28, deninger2005arithmeticgeometryand pages 21-25, deninger2005arithmeticgeometryand pages 41-45). While this supports the conceptual mechanism needed to reinterpret arithmetic data in explicit-formula form, the retrieved excerpts do not compute Quillen torsion determinants into the exact gamma-factor distributions appearing in Connes/Weil.

Assessment of the archimedean match: the hypothesis is strongly supported conceptually—analytic torsion is literally a zeta-regularized determinant (gillet1992anarithmeticriemannroch pages 49-53), and Weil’s archimedean term is literally a distribution built from gamma-factor analysis (connes1999traceformulain pages 73-77). Moreover, Gillet–Soulé’s \(R\)-class contains zeta-derivative constants (gillet1992anarithmeticriemannroch pages 49-53) that live in the same “special-value/gamma” ecosystem. But the evidence gathered here stops short of an explicit computation that identifies \(T(\bar L)\) and/or the \(R\)-term with Connes’ \(\Psi\)-distribution for a test function canonically determined by \(\bar L\).

4) Synthesis: can full ARR be transformed into Weil explicit formula?

The Spec\((\mathbb Z)\) specialization of ARR already has the right global architecture: a single global identity whose right-hand side splits into (i) a pushforward term aggregating finite arithmetic data with \(\log\#k(x)\) weights, and (ii) an archimedean correction involving analytic torsion and the \(R\)-genus (gillet1992anarithmeticriemannroch pages 53-56, gillet1992anarithmeticriemannroch pages 49-53). Weil’s explicit formula similarly decomposes into (i) finite local terms (non-archimedean distributions which, after expansion, yield prime(-power) sums with logarithmic coefficients), and (ii) archimedean distributions/constants (gamma-factor/\(\Psi\)-type terms) (connes1999traceformulain pages 69-73, connes1999traceformulain pages 73-77).

However, turning ARR into Weil’s explicit formula would require at least two additional steps not supplied by the current evidence:

• A test-function formalism: Weil’s explicit formula is an identity of distributions evaluated on test functions, whereas ARR is an equality in arithmetic Chow/degree language. One needs a mechanism associating a test function to \(\bar L\) (or to a family of metrics on \(\bar L\)) such that intersection-theoretic contributions become the explicit-formula weights.

• A reindexing/normalization converting closed-point degrees \(m\log p\) into von Mangoldt coefficients \(\log p\) while producing the correct test-function weight at \(p^m\). Deninger’s orbit picture suggests one natural place where multiplicities \(m\) arise (iterates of a primitive orbit) and can be normalized, but this normalization is not carried out within Gillet–Soulé’s ARR statement itself (deninger2005arithmeticgeometryand pages 25-28, deninger2005arithmeticgeometryand pages 21-25).

Thus, the evidence supports the research hypothesis as a plausible program (structural matching and shared analytic objects), but it does not validate it as an established theorem for a concrete choice of \(\bar L\) in the ARR setting.
