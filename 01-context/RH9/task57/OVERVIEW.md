<conclusion>
The literature supports a rational weight/eigenspace decomposition for the commuting Adams operations {ψ^n} on (pieces of) K-theory/motivic cohomology, but it does not currently provide an established identification of their joint spectrum on a global cohomology of Spec ℤ with the nontrivial zeros of ζ(s).
</conclusion>
<explanation>
This survey separated three intertwined strands: (i) λ-ring/Adams-operation representation theory on K-theory and motivic cohomology, (ii) special-value formulas for zeta/L-functions in terms of these “weight” pieces, and (iii) trace-formula/spectral realizations of zeta zeros coming from scaling/Frobenius actions.

(1) Spectrum/eigenvalues for the family {ψ^n}.  In standard λ-ring settings, the operators ψ^k commute and are determined by the λ-operations (Newton identities). Under nilpotence/filtration hypotheses one can rationally split into simultaneous eigenspaces. Concretely, Hiller proves that for a suitable augmented special λ-ring A with locally nilpotent filtration, A⊗ℚ decomposes as a direct sum of ψ^k-eigenspaces V_i with ψ^k acting by multiplication by k^i, and these V_i are independent of k (so the whole monoid action is simultaneously diagonalizable over ℚ) (hiller1981λringsandalgebraic pages 8-12). In algebraic K-theory with supports, the Gillet–Soulé framework (as summarized by Walker) gives an explicit internal direct-sum “weight” decomposition K_0^Z(R)⊗ℚ = ⊕_j K_0^Z(R)(j)_ℚ such that ψ_k acts diagonally on weight j by eigenvalue k^j, equivalently ∏_j(ψ_k−k^j)=0 on K_0^Z(R)⊗ℚ (walker2020adamsoperationsin pages 113-116). Levine develops a general “Adams decomposition” formalism in which ψ^k−k^i·id is only required to be nilpotent on summands M_i, capturing the common practical situation that one obtains generalized eigenspaces (diagonalizable up to nilpotents) (levine2005lambdaoperations pages 33-37). In motivic contexts, Riou shows that classical operations on K_0 (including λ^n and ψ^k) lift uniquely to endomorphisms of the A^1-representing object for higher K-theory, thereby placing ψ^k in the stable motivic setting (riou2010algebraicktheory pages 1-3).

For motivic cohomology, the most explicit “spectrum statement” is that motivic cohomology groups are defined as Adams weight pieces of K-theory: Goncharov uses H^i_M(X,ℚ(n)) := K^{(n)}_{2n−i}(X)⊗ℚ, where K^{(n)} denotes the weight-n eigenspace for the Adams operations (goncharov2005polylogarithmsregulatorsand pages 1-4). Taken together, these sources justify a joint-spectrum description in the following limited sense: after rationalization (and sometimes modulo nilpotent error) the family {ψ^n} acts through characters n↦n^j on weight-j components (hiller1981λringsandalgebraic pages 8-12, walker2020adamsoperationsin pages 113-116, levine2005lambdaoperations pages 33-37, goncharov2005polylogarithmsregulatorsand pages 1-4). However, within the retrieved evidence there is no explicit computation of the resulting multiplicities/eigenspaces for the specific groups K_i(ℤ), beyond this abstract weight formalism.

(2) Eigenvalues/weights vs special values and (orders of) zeros of L-functions.  The best-developed relationship in the literature is between Adams-weighted motivic/K-theoretic pieces and special values (leading terms) of L-functions, rather than individual nontrivial zeros. Goncharov states Beilinson’s conjectural framework: for integers n > 1 + i/2, the special value L(h^i(X), n) should be described (up to a rational factor) by the Beilinson regulator from motivic cohomology H^*_M(X,ℚ(n)) (identified as Adams weight-n K-theory) to real Deligne cohomology; and in the case X=Spec(F) for a number field F this regulator coincides with the Borel regulator and connects to special values of the Dedekind zeta function via Borel’s theorem (goncharov2005polylogarithmsregulatorsand pages 1-4). Grayson’s discussion of weight filtrations and the motivic Atiyah–Hirzebruch spectral sequence highlights a more “zero-sensitive” conjectural bridge: the rank of E_2-terms is “conjecturally associated with the order of zero of the L-function” of an étale cohomology group at a specified point (grayson1994weightfiltrationsin pages 22-25). This addresses objective (2) partially: Adams weights are tightly integrated into conjectures for special values/leading terms and, more indirectly, to orders of vanishing; but none of the cited K-theoretic λ/ψ papers provide a formula linking individual eigenvalues of ψ^n to locations of nontrivial zeros.

(3) Trace formula / spectral interpretation of zeros via a monoid action.  The trace-formula-type relation requested in objective (3) does appear in the Connes (and Connes–Consani) noncommutative-geometry program, but with the relevant operator being a scaling/Frobenius action on an adèlic/noncommutative space rather than the Adams operations on K_i(ℤ). Connes describes that the zeros of ζ(s) occur as an “absorption spectrum” for the scaling action on an L^2-space built from commensurability classes of ℚ-lattices, and that the Riemann–Weil explicit formulas arise from a trace formula in this setting (connes2010aviewof pages 24-26). In later expositions he states explicitly that his trace formula “recovers Weil’s explicit formula” and has a spectral side with contributions from zeros and a geometric side with contributions from places (connes2602theriemannhypothesis pages 16-19). Connes–Consani propose an “arithmetic site” equipped with an action of the monoid ℕ^× (and scaling group ℝ^×_+), with Frobenius correspondences Ψ(λ) satisfying Ψ(λ)∘Ψ(λ′)=Ψ(λλ′), and construct a counting distribution whose associated Hasse–Weil zeta is the complete Riemann zeta function (connes2014thearithmeticsite pages 3-6, connes2014thearithmeticsite pages 1-3). Deninger outlines a dynamical/cohomological program to interpret explicit formulas via index-theorem analogues, but notes that no cohomology theory for Spec ℤ has yet been developed in his framework (deninger2007dynamicalsystemsand pages 1-3).

Statistical tests performed.  No statistical hypothesis tests were applicable: the objective is a mathematical-literature synthesis rather than an empirical dataset analysis. The “tests” in this context were consistency checks across sources: (a) whether commuting relations ψ^kψ^l=ψ^{kl} and rational weight decompositions are explicitly stated (yes in Hiller and Walker/GS summaries) (hiller1981λringsandalgebraic pages 8-12, walker2020adamsoperationsin pages 113-116); (b) whether eigenvalues are explicitly of the form k^j on weight-j pieces (yes) (hiller1981λringsandalgebraic pages 8-12, walker2020adamsoperationsin pages 113-116); (c) whether any cited source states an equality of the form Trace(F({ψ^n})|H^1)=∑_ρ G({γ_ρ}) with ρ running over zeta zeros (no in the ψ^n/K-theory literature retrieved; trace-side statements about zeros appear instead in Connes’ scaling-action trace formula discussions) (connes2602theriemannhypothesis pages 16-19, connes2010aviewof pages 24-26).

Scientific implications.  The evidence strongly supports the “diagonalizable monoid action” portion of the hypothesis, but only in the rationalized/weight-graded sense: ψ^n acts by n^j on weight-j pieces (often up to nilpotents) (hiller1981λringsandalgebraic pages 8-12, levine2005lambdaoperations pages 33-37, walker2020adamsoperationsin pages 113-116). The evidence does not support (at present) the stronger claim that the joint spectrum of {ψ^n} on a specific global cohomology group of Spec ℤ matches the set of nontrivial zeta zeros. The closest “spectral zeros” results arise in noncommutative/scaling-site frameworks whose operator is a scaling/Frobenius action engineered to produce Weil explicit formulas (connes2014thearithmeticsite pages 3-6, connes2602theriemannhypothesis pages 16-19, connes2010aviewof pages 24-26).
</explanation>
<discussion>
The main limitation is a mismatch between two mature theories:

• In λ-ring/K-theoretic representation theory, the eigenvalues of ψ^n are rigid (n^j on weight-j components), and the decomposition is algebraic, often requiring rational coefficients and sometimes yielding generalized eigenspaces (nilpotent error terms) rather than honest diagonalization on integral groups (levine2005lambdaoperations pages 33-37, walker2020adamsoperationsin pages 113-116, hiller1981λringsandalgebraic pages 8-12).

• In spectral realizations of zeta zeros (Connes, Connes–Consani, Deninger), the relevant “monoid action” is scaling/Frobenius on adèlic or topos-theoretic objects; while these are sometimes philosophically compared to Frobenius lifts/Adams operations, the retrieved sources do not construct a direct bridge identifying this scaling action with ψ^n on algebraic K-theory or TC of Spec ℤ, nor do they compute an Adams-spectrum on K_i(ℤ) whose eigenparameters are the nontrivial zeros (connes2014thearithmeticsite pages 3-6, connes2602theriemannhypothesis pages 16-19, deninger2007dynamicalsystemsand pages 1-3).

A further limitation is evidentiary: key classical papers that might contain more explicit computations (e.g., Gillet–Soulé “Intersection theory using Adams operations”, and detailed K-theory-of-integers computations) were not obtainable in this run, so we cannot rule out more explicit trace identities appearing there. Nonetheless, within the gathered corpus, the only explicit-formula/trace identities involving zeros are attached to scaling/Frobenius actions in noncommutative geometry, not to Adams operations acting on K_i(ℤ) (connes2602theriemannhypothesis pages 16-19, connes2010aviewof pages 24-26).
