The evidence splits into two complementary strands.

(1) Arakelov theory: global invariants expressed as algebraic (finite-prime) terms plus archimedean analytic torsion terms.

Gillet–Soulé’s arithmetic Riemann–Roch theorem (ARR) provides a canonical formula for a global invariant over arithmetic schemes: the arithmetic class/degree of the determinant of cohomology equipped with the Quillen metric is expressed in terms of the pushforward of arithmetic Chern character and Todd classes plus explicit correction terms built from Bott–Chern secondary classes and analytic torsion (Ray–Singer torsion defined from zeta-regularized Laplacians). This is an explicit “global = algebraic pushforward + analytic torsion/differential-form correction” identity, where the algebraic/arithmetic Chow pushforward packages non-archimedean (finite prime) intersection-theoretic data and the Quillen/analytic torsion terms package the archimedean (spectral) contributions (gillet1992anarithmeticriemannroch pages 49-53, gillet1992anarithmeticriemannroch pages 46-49, gillet1992anarithmeticriemannroch pages 60-63). The higher-degree ARR of Gillet–Rössler–Soulé makes the same structure explicit: the GRR correction is modified by an “exotic” class involving R(T_gℂ), and the analytic input is explicitly tied to local index theory/analytic torsion forms, i.e. an archimedean correction living in differential forms on the complex manifold of points (gillet2008anarithmeticriemannroch pages 1-3).

A very explicit “sum over places” RR-type formula appears in Borek’s Arakelov theory for noncommutative arithmetic curves: the degree map for divisors includes an overt decomposition
Σ_p κ_p log N(p) − log N_∞(D_∞)
(i.e. a sum over finite primes plus an archimedean log-norm term), and the Riemann–Roch identity χ(a)=deg div(a)+χ(𝒪) uses this decomposition directly (borek2011arakelovtheoryof pages 7-10, borek2011arakelovtheoryof pages 10-14). While this does not contain von Mangoldt weights or test-function transforms, it matches the hypothesis’s structural requirement “global invariant = sum over p≤∞ of local contributions.”

(2) Determinants det′Δ, Quillen metrics, and links to zeta/L-values.

Several sources make explicit the role of zeta-regularized determinants and analytic torsion. Montplet proves an arithmetic Riemann–Roch theorem for pointed stable curves with singular hyperbolic metrics, relating arithmetic intersection invariants and determinant-line data with Quillen metrics (defined via Selberg zeta on hyperbolic components) and deriving explicit special-value relations on modular curves; an example formula ties an arithmetic intersection quantity to the constant (2ζ′(−1)+ζ(−1)) (modulo log|ℚ×|), indicating a separation of archimedean analytic constants from arithmetic contributions (montplet2009anarithmeticriemannroch pages 1-4, montplet2009anarithmeticriemannroch pages 38-42). Chinburg defines Arakelov Euler characteristics as degrees of determinants of total cohomology with Quillen metrics, with finite-prime dependence through local lattice/ε-constant data and archimedean dependence through analytic torsion/determinants of Laplacians; in particular, a theorem identifies the Arakelov Euler characteristic with a rational class built from local ε-constants attached to p-adic étale cohomology and to Hodge cohomology, i.e. an explicit “local factor” decomposition principle (chinburg2001arakeloveulercharacteristics pages 3-5).

On the analytic side (relevant because Quillen metrics are defined using det′Δ), there are explicit determinant factorizations via Selberg zeta and Kronecker limit formulas: for compact hyperbolic surfaces, det′Δ can be expressed using Selberg zeta derivatives (e.g., det′Δ0 = e^{c0(2g−2)} Z′(1)) and also admits holomorphic factorization into exp(−const·Liouville action) times |holomorphic product|^2 (mcintyre2006holomorphicfactorizationof pages 3-6, mcintyre2006holomorphicfactorizationof pages 1-3). These formulas exhibit the kind of “global spectral invariant = explicit analytic factors” that can serve as the archimedean piece of an arithmetic Arakelov identity, but they do not themselves incorporate the finite primes.

(3) Explicit Weil-formula matching: adelic principal value local terms and the archimedean Ψ-term.

The strongest positive evidence for an explicit-formula-like trace identity starting from a “geometric object over all places” is in Connes–Consani–Marcolli’s noncommutative geometry of the adèle class space. They define a cyclic-homology/cohomology object H1(A_K/K*, C_K) and prove a Lefschetz-type trace formula: for a test function f, Tr(ϑ_m(f)|H1) equals a spectral sum over zeros together with explicit local terms given by principal value integrals over the local multiplicative groups at all places and a global logarithmic correction involving the differential idèle (connes2010theweilproof pages 26-30, connes2010theweilproof pages 23-26, connes2010theweilproof pages 1-3). This is essentially a geometric trace formula whose “geometric side” is already in the Weil explicit formula’s native language (sum of local distributions W_v).

For the archimedean contribution, Connes–Consani isolate and compute an archimedean distribution built from the kernel −log|t|; they show its Fourier transform yields the Weil distribution used in the archimedean Ψ/Gamma-factor term, giving a direct identification of the archimedean local piece at the distributional level (connes2021weilpositivityand pages 10-12, connes2021weilpositivityand pages 1-3). The displayed equations are visible in the retrieved figure (connes2021weilpositivityand media e3405e22). Independently, Burnol gives an explicit decomposition of Weil’s explicit formula into local terms W_ν(g) expressed as finite-part/principal-value integrals over ℚ_ν× and shows the archimedean term contains explicit constants like (log π + γ)g(1) together with principal value integrals, matching the standard Ψ-type archimedean contribution structure (burnol1998theexplicitformula pages 13-16).

Identification with the user’s specific Weil explicit formula terms.

• Archimedean term Ψ(r): Connes–Consani’s formulas show the archimedean distribution arises from −log|t| and yields the Weil distribution after Fourier transform, which is exactly the kind of Γ-factor/Ψ-term contribution in the explicit formula (connes2021weilpositivityand pages 10-12, connes2021weilpositivityand media e3405e22). Burnol’s explicit constants (log π + γ)g(1) and principal-value integrals are also standard representatives of the archimedean Ψ-term in Weil’s formulation (burnol1998theexplicitformula pages 13-16).

• Finite primes term −2Λ(n)n^{−1/2} g(log n): In Connes–Consani–Marcolli, the finite places appear as local principal-value terms W_v on K_v× that recombine to the global explicit formula; this is the level at which the von Mangoldt-weighted prime sums are usually recovered after expanding local Euler factors/log-derivatives, but the evidence snippets retrieved here do not explicitly print the von Mangoldt sum itself—rather they give the local-integral representation which is known to be equivalent to the von Mangoldt side upon standard manipulations (connes2010theweilproof pages 26-30, connes2016anessayon pages 3-6). Thus, the match is strongest for the archimedean term and structural (via local distributions) for finite primes.

Statistical tests.

No statistical hypothesis tests were performed because the objective is a literature synthesis of explicit formulas in pure mathematics rather than an empirical dataset analysis. Instead, the “test” applied was an evidence-matching criterion: (i) presence of a displayed or explicit global identity; (ii) explicit decomposition into contributions indexed by all places; (iii) explicit identification of the archimedean distribution with the Weil Ψ/Gamma-factor term; and (iv) explicit attempt to realize the explicit formula as a trace/Lefschetz formula from geometry. Criteria (i) and (ii) are satisfied by Arakelov RR/degree formulas (gillet1992anarithmeticriemannroch pages 49-53, borek2011arakelovtheoryof pages 7-10). Criteria (iii) and (iv) are satisfied most strongly by Connes–Consani(-Marcolli) and Burnol (connes2010theweilproof pages 26-30, connes2021weilpositivityand pages 10-12, burnol1998theexplicitformula pages 13-16, connes2021weilpositivityand media e3405e22).

<discussion>
The research hypothesis is only partially confirmed in the strict sense “an arithmetic Riemann–Roch theorem for Spec ℤ whose local terms can be directly identified with Weil’s Ψ(r) and −2Λ(n)n^{−1/2}g(log n).” Classical Arakelov Riemann–Roch theorems do produce global invariants (arithmetic degrees, determinants of cohomology with Quillen metrics) expressed as algebraic pushforwards plus explicit archimedean analytic torsion/differential-form terms, which is exactly a global-to-local decomposition across finite vs. archimedean places (gillet1992anarithmeticriemannroch pages 49-53, gillet2008anarithmeticriemannroch pages 1-3). However, in the evidence retrieved here these formulas remain in the language of arithmetic Chow theory and analytic torsion rather than in the explicit-formula language of test functions and von Mangoldt-weighted prime sums.

By contrast, Connes–Consani–Marcolli’s framework yields a direct Weil-explicit-formula-as-Lefschetz-trace formula with local principal-value distributions at every place and an explicit spectral side, hence matching the desired structure very closely, but it is formulated on the adèle class space (a noncommutative space) rather than a standard compactification of Spec ℤ in Arakelov geometry (connes2010theweilproof pages 26-30, connes2010theweilproof pages 1-3). The archimedean term matching is particularly explicit: the −log|t| kernel yields the Weil distribution (connes2021weilpositivityand pages 10-12, connes2021weilpositivityand media e3405e22), and Burnol’s exposition makes the local integral terms at each completion completely explicit (burnol1998theexplicitformula pages 13-16).

Limitations: (i) Image extraction failed for Gillet–Soulé’s displayed ARR formula, so citations rely on text evidence rather than figure crops (gillet1992anarithmeticriemannroch pages 49-53). (ii) The retrieved Connes–Consani–Marcolli snippets establish local principal-value terms but did not, in the excerpted evidence, explicitly rewrite the finite-place side as a von Mangoldt sum with a specific test-function g; that equivalence is standard but was not re-derived here from a fully quoted passage (connes2010theweilproof pages 26-30, connes2016anessayon pages 3-6). (iii) A fully explicit “Spec ℤ compactification + Laplacian determinant = Weil explicit formula” in purely Arakelov terms was not located in the retrieved texts; the closest currently explicit derivations are noncommutative/adelic.
