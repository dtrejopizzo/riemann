<conclusion>
The current literature does not support the existence (yet) of a global Sen operator Θ_Z on integral prismatic cohomology of Spec ℤ whose base change to Spf ℤ_p recovers the classical p-adic Sen operator Θ_p, and no evidenced link between such a Θ_Z-spectrum and the Riemann zeros is presently established.
</conclusion>
<explanation>
<p-adic Sen operator: definition and functoriality.>  For a p-adic Galois representation V of a p-adic field k, Sen theory constructs a decompleted module H(V)_fin inside H(V):=(V⊗_{\Q_p}C_p)^{Gal(k/k_\infty)}, where k_\infty=k(\mu_{p^\infty}) and Γ=Gal(k_\infty/k) acts; the infinitesimal Γ-action defines a k_\infty-linear endomorphism φ_V on H(V)_fin, whose eigenvalues are the generalized Hodge–Tate weights of V (shimizu2018constancyofgeneralized pages 1-3).  A representation is Hodge–Tate exactly when φ_V is semisimple with integer eigenvalues (shimizu2018constancyofgeneralized pages 1-3).  Sen’s original work also emphasizes functoriality of the attached canonical operator for Banach-algebra valued Galois homomorphisms (sen1993aninfinitedimensional pages 1-5).  Modern “arithmetic Sen operator” constructions package this infinitesimal Γ-action as a canonical Lie(Γ)-action on a functor Darith(M), which is lax symmetric monoidal; when the decompletion map is an isomorphism, the chosen generator Θ∈Lie(Γ) uniquely extends to a C_p-linear endomorphism of M called the arithmetic Sen operator (jiang2310thetaoperatorequals pages 14-17).  Relative/geometric Sen theory further recasts Sen data as an integrable Higgs field θ_F:F→F⊗Ω^1_X(log)(−1), functorial in F and compatible with pullback, with cohomological control Riν_*F via Higgs cohomology (camargo2026geometricsentheory pages 1-3).

<Prismatic analogues.>  In the prismatic/prismatization setting, Sen-type operators do appear, but in local and/or truncated form.  Liu constructs a Sen operator Θ_E on the pullback ρ_*E of quasi-coherent complexes on truncated Cartier–Witt/prismatized stacks WCart_n (under n≤p) by producing an infinitesimal δ-base-change isomorphism γ_b≡Id mod ε, written as Id+εΘ_E; Θ_E satisfies a Leibniz rule and acts explicitly by Θ(λ^i)=iλ^i in Breuil–Kisin coordinates (liu2409ontheprismatization pages 12-15).  This Θ_E packages objects E into pairs (ρ_*E,Θ_E) landing fully faithfully in a category of modules with a connection-like structure and imposes a “local nilpotence of Θ^p−Θ mod p” condition on the essential image (liu2409ontheprismatization pages 1-3).  The same framework gives a cohomological description of global sections as a fiber of Θ_E (liu2024prismaticcrystalsover pages 104-109).  Separately, Gao–Min–Wang relate Hodge–Tate prismatic crystals on the absolute (log-)prismatic site of O_K to Sen theory by classifying crystals via linear algebra data (M,φ_M) that controls Sen weights and yields a comparison between prismatic cohomology of a crystal and Galois cohomology of an associated representation (gao2311hodgetateprismaticcrystals pages 3-4).  However, in the evidence retrieved here, these constructions are (i) p-adic/local (over O_K or ℤ_p[[λ]]-type prisms), (ii) sometimes truncated (n≤p), and (iii) do not provide a cohomology theory over Spec ℤ equipped with a single operator Θ_Z whose p-adic base changes recover the classical Θ_p.

<Behavior as p varies.>  No explicit compatibility relation between Θ_p for different primes p was located in the retrieved sources.  The strongest available statement is indirect: in geometric situations the eigenvalues of Θ (Hodge–Tate weights) are expected to match algebro-geometric invariants such as Hodge numbers (hence p-independent in motivic contexts) (porat2022topicsinsen pages 9-12).  There are strong rigidity/constancy results inside a fixed p-adic framework (e.g., generalized Hodge–Tate weights constant in rigid families at fixed p), but these do not compare different primes (shimizu2018constancyofgeneralized pages 1-3).

<Archimedean/spectral connections.>  There is a concrete archimedean analogue of a Hodge–Tate infinitesimal operator: Morin defines an operator Θ as the infinitesimal generator of an ℝ-action associated with a Hodge–Tate structure on the cohomology of the archimedean local Weil group, computes its spectrum, and expresses the archimedean Γ-factor as a zeta-regularized determinant det_∞(2π(Θ−s·Id)) (morin2015aremarkon pages 1-3).  This provides a rigorous bridge between a “Θ-operator” and archimedean local factors, but it is not presented as a base change of the p-adic Sen operator (morin2015aremarkon pages 1-3).  In Deninger’s global program for zeta functions over Spec ℤ, the candidate global operator is an infinitesimal generator θ of a continuous ℝ-flow acting on reduced foliation cohomology; Deninger explicitly defines θ=lim_{t→0}t^{−1}(φ_t^*−id) and states it plays a role similar to Frobenius in characteristic p (deninger2007analogiesbetweenanalysis pages 3-6).  At the same time, Deninger stresses that a suitable cohomology theory for schemes over Spec ℤ “has yet been developed” and uses the dynamical/foliation model as a conjectural substitute (deninger2007dynamicalsystemsand pages 1-3, deninger2007analogiesbetweenanalysis pages 1-3).  None of the retrieved sources provides a theorem identifying θ with (a limit or interpolation of) p-adic Sen operators, nor a derivation of Riemann zeros as the spectrum of a Sen operator.

<Statistical tests.>  No statistical hypothesis tests are applicable: the question is structural/theoretical, and the evidence consists of definitions, comparison theorems, and existence/nonexistence statements from the mathematical literature rather than numerical datasets.

<Scientific implications.>  The evidence points to a coherent pattern: (i) at each fixed prime p, Sen operators are highly functorial “infinitesimal Galois” invariants, and (ii) prismatic cohomology has begun to admit Sen-type infinitesimal operators in certain local/truncated contexts.  But (iii) globalization over Spec ℤ in the precise sense of a single Θ_Z acting on integral prismatic cohomology of Spec ℤ, with compatibility across all p and with spectral meaning for ζ(s), remains open and is not currently established in the sourced literature.
</explanation>
<discussion>
The research hypothesis asks for a specific global object—an integral prismatic cohomology H_{Δℤ}(Spec ℤ) with an operator Θ_ℤ whose base change to Spf ℤ_p recovers Θ_p and whose complexified spectrum relates to Riemann zeros.  Within the retrieved literature, prismatic Sen-type operators exist primarily in (a) absolute prismatic settings over O_K and (b) truncated Cartier–Witt/prismatization settings (n≤p), where the operator is built from an infinitesimal δ-base-change and has an explicit “Euler derivation” flavor Θ(λ^i)=iλ^i (liu2409ontheprismatization pages 12-15, liu2409ontheprismatization pages 1-3).  These are promising analogues, but they fall short of the demanded global Spec ℤ construction.

A second limitation is cross-prime compatibility.  Even if one defines Θ_p on each p-adic realization, nothing in the retrieved evidence supplies a mechanism to compare these operators as p varies; the only plausible pathway is via motivic/Hodge invariants (Hodge numbers) that should govern all p-adic Hodge–Tate weights, but this is not formulated here as an operator-level compatibility statement (porat2022topicsinsen pages 9-12).  This is a substantial conceptual gap for any attempted Θ_ℤ.

Finally, regarding Riemann zeros: the closest rigorous link to spectral determinants is at the archimedean place, where Morin’s operator Θ yields Γ-factors as regularized determinants (morin2015aremarkon pages 1-3).  Deninger’s θ is the archetype of a “global infinitesimal generator” intended to replace Frobenius over Spec ℤ, but his construction occurs in a dynamical/foliation framework and explicitly notes the absence of a satisfactory cohomology theory over Spec ℤ in the usual algebro-geometric sense (deninger2007dynamicalsystemsand pages 1-3, deninger2007analogiesbetweenanalysis pages 3-6).  Thus, while the philosophical direction supports “infinitesimal generator” operators as global candidates, the specific identification of a prismatic/Sen Θ_ℤ with Deninger’s θ (and any connection to the Riemann spectrum) remains speculative.
