<conclusion>
The reviewed literature supports the hypothesis that, in Arakelov motivic cohomology, cup product with the arithmetic first Chern class ĉ₁(\overline{\mathcal L}) acts by raising cohomological degree by +2 and shifting the Adams (twist) weight by +1, i.e. \(H^p_{M,ar}(X,\n\mathbf{Q}(q))\xrightarrow{\,\cup\,\hat c_1\,}H^{p+2}_{M,ar}(X,\n\mathbf{Q}(q+1))\). (scholbach2012arakelovmotiviccohomology pages 17-21, scholbach2012arakelovmotiviccohomology pages 14-17)
</conclusion>
<explanation>
1) Definition of arithmetic/Arakelov motivic cohomology groups.  Holmström–Scholbach define the Arakelov (arithmetic) motivic cohomology spectrum \(\widehat{A}\) as a homotopy fiber of the regulator map into the Deligne cohomology spectrum: \(\widehat{A}:=\mathrm{hofib}(A\wedge S^0\to A\wedge \eta_*H_D)\), in particular for \(A=H_B\) (Beilinson motivic cohomology spectrum) or \(A=BGL\) (algebraic K-theory spectrum). (holmstrom2010arakelovmotiviccohomology media e4a7ea04)  The associated Arakelov motivic cohomology groups are then represented by this spectrum, e.g. \(\widehat{H}^n(M,p)=\mathrm{Hom}_{SH(S)_\mathbf Q}(M,\widehat{H}_B(p)[n])\), with a bi-grading \((n,p)\) in cohomological degree and Tate twist/weight. (holmstrom2010arakelovmotiviccohomology pages 19-22, holmstrom2010arakelovmotiviccohomology media e4a7ea04)  This yields a long exact sequence relating Arakelov motivic cohomology, motivic cohomology, and Deligne cohomology (regulator): \(\cdots\to \widehat H^n(M,p)\to H^n(M,p)\xrightarrow{\rho}\mathrm{Hom}(M,\eta_*H_D(p)[n])\to \widehat H^{n+1}(M,p)\to\cdots\). (holmstrom2010arakelovmotiviccohomology media e4a7ea04)

For l.c.i. schemes \(X\), the same long exact sequence specializes to an exact sequence connecting Arakelov motivic cohomology, Adams-eigenspaces of algebraic K-theory, and Deligne cohomology: \(\cdots\to \widehat H^n(X,p)\to K_{2p-n}(X)^{(p)}_{\mathbf Q}\to H^n_D(X,p)\to \widehat H^{n+1}(X,p)\to\cdots\). (holmstrom2010arakelovmotiviccohomology media e4a7ea04)  For an arithmetic scheme such as \(X=\mathrm{Spec}\,\mathbf Z\), one applies the same definition with the motive of \(\mathrm{Spec}\,\mathbf Z\) (or the corresponding object in the motivic stable homotopy category), producing \(H^p_{M,ar}(\mathrm{Spec}\,\mathbf Z,\mathbf Q(q))\) as the corresponding Hom group into the Arakelov fiber spectrum. (holmstrom2010arakelovmotiviccohomology pages 19-22, holmstrom2010arakelovmotiviccohomology media e4a7ea04)

A parallel, more explicit (complex-level) model is given by Goncharov: the weight-\(n\) Arakelov motivic complex is defined as a cone (shifted by −1) of a regulator map from Bloch’s weight-\(n\) higher Chow complex to the weight-\(n\) Deligne complex, and the Arakelov motivic cohomology groups are the cohomology of this cone. (goncharov2005polylogarithmsregulatorsand pages 1-4)

2) Cup product and arithmetic first Chern class.  On the realization/Deligne side, the Deligne complex \(D(p)\) is a unital differential (bi)graded algebra whose induced product on Deligne cohomology agrees with the classical cup product \(\cup\); moreover there is a functorial first Chern class \(c_1:\mathrm{Pic}(X)\to H^2_D(X,1)\). (holmstrom2010arakelovmotiviccohomology pages 12-16)  These multiplicative structures are promoted to the spectrum level: HD is a commutative ring spectrum, and the “hat” (Arakelov) construction preserves module structures (if \(A\) is a ring spectrum, \(\widehat A\) is canonically an \(A\)-module). (holmstrom2010arakelovmotiviccohomology pages 19-22)

In Scholbach’s motivic-stable formulation over \(\mathbf Z\), Arakelov motivic cohomology groups are defined by \(\widehat H^i(M,m)=\mathrm{Hom}_{DM(\mathbf Z)}(M,\widehat{\mathbf 1}(m)[i])\) for a canonically defined Arakelov motivic cohomology spectrum \(\widehat{\mathbf 1}\), together with a canonical “Chern class/regulator” map \(ch:\widehat{\mathbf 1}\to \eta_*H_D\). (scholbach2017specialvaluesofa pages 13-16)  This places the arithmetic first Chern class of a metrized (hermitian) line bundle naturally in bidegree \((2,1)\): \(\hat c_1(\overline{\mathcal L})\in \widehat H^2(X,1)=\mathrm{Hom}(M(X),\widehat{\mathbf 1}(1)[2])\). (scholbach2017specialvaluesof pages 13-16)

Scholbach also relates these classes to classical Gillet–Soulé arithmetic Chow theory via arithmetic Chern classes, i.e. arithmetic Chern classes identify arithmetic K-theory in degree 0 with a direct sum of arithmetic Chow pieces; under the comparison between Arakelov motivic cohomology and arithmetic K-/Chow-theory, the arithmetic first Chern class corresponds to the usual Gillet–Soulé arithmetic \(\hat c_1\) and hence again lives in degree 2, weight 1. (scholbach2012arakelovmotiviccohomology pages 21-24)

3) Existence of the degree/weight shift map induced by cup product with \(\hat c_1\).  Scholbach constructs canonical pairings compatible with the K-theory and Chow actions, giving (in even-degree notation) pairings of the form
\[H^{2n}(X,n)\times \widehat H^{2m}(X,m)\to \widehat H^{2(n+m)}(X,n+m).\]
(scholbach2012arakelovmotiviccohomology pages 17-21)  Taking the element \(\hat c_1(\overline{\mathcal L})\in \widehat H^2(X,1)\) and using additivity of the bi-grading in this pairing yields precisely an operation
\[\cup\hat c_1(\overline{\mathcal L}):\ \widehat H^p(X,q)\to \widehat H^{p+2}(X,q+1),\]
interpreting the pairings as cup products / module actions in the Arakelov theory. (scholbach2012arakelovmotiviccohomology pages 17-21, scholbach2017specialvaluesof pages 13-16)

4) Identification of the weight \(q\) with Adams eigenspace weight.  The long exact sequence for l.c.i. schemes explicitly identifies the twist index \(p\) with the Adams eigenspace label in algebraic K-theory: \(\widehat H^n(X,p)\to K_{2p-n}(X)^{(p)}_{\mathbf Q}\to H^n_D(X,p)\). (holmstrom2010arakelovmotiviccohomology media e4a7ea04)  Moreover, Scholbach’s decomposition statements show that the summands indexed by \(p\) are characterized by Adams operations acting by \(k^p\) on the \(p\)-summand, i.e. the twist/weight is exactly the Adams-eigenspace weight. (scholbach2012arakelovmotiviccohomology pages 14-17)  Therefore, cup product with a first Chern class in weight 1 shifts the Adams weight by +1.

</explanation>
<discussion>
The evidence for the degree and weight shift is strong at the level of formalism: (i) Arakelov motivic cohomology is explicitly bigraded \((p,q)\) via Tate twists and is constructed as a homotopy fiber of a regulator map (holmstrom2010arakelovmotiviccohomology media e4a7ea04); (ii) multiplicative structures exist via ring/module spectra and via K- and Chow-actions giving explicit graded pairings (scholbach2012arakelovmotiviccohomology pages 17-21, holmstrom2010arakelovmotiviccohomology pages 19-22); (iii) the twist index is identified with the Adams eigenspace weight both through exact sequences involving \(K_{2p-n}(X)^{(p)}\) and through explicit Adams-operation eigenvalues on the relevant summands (holmstrom2010arakelovmotiviccohomology media e4a7ea04, scholbach2012arakelovmotiviccohomology pages 14-17). Together these imply that \(\cup\hat c_1\) maps \((p,q)\mapsto (p+2,q+1)\) whenever \(\hat c_1\in\widehat H^2(X,1)\).

A limitation of the present excerpt set is that it does not include an explicit, cochain-level definition of \(\hat c_1(\overline{\mathcal L})\) from a hermitian metric (e.g. as a pair consisting of an algebraic divisor class plus a Green current) in the classical Gillet–Soulé style; instead, the evidence places \(\hat c_1\) via the Arakelov motivic spectrum and via comparison with arithmetic Chow/K-theory. (scholbach2017specialvaluesof pages 13-16, scholbach2012arakelovmotiviccohomology pages 21-24) For users needing explicit calculations on \(\mathrm{Spec}\,\mathbf Z\), further extraction from texts specifically treating arithmetic Chow groups of \(\mathrm{Spec}\,\mathbf Z\) and their metrics would be needed, but the structural claim about degree/weight shifting is already determined by the bi-grading and multiplicativity.
