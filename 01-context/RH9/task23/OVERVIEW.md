1. Derived Hecke algebras give explicit degree-shifting correspondence operators on arithmetic cohomology.
Venkatesh constructs a graded (Ext-defined) “derived Hecke algebra” acting on cohomology of arithmetic groups/manifolds, with the \(\mathrm{Ext}^i\) component shifting cohomological degree by \(+i\) (venkatesh2019derivedheckealgebra pages 1-3). In a concrete \(\mathrm{PGL}_2\) setting, he defines a degree-\(+1\) derived Hecke operator \(T_{q,\alpha}\) by an explicit pull–cup–push construction: pull back cohomology along the Hecke correspondence to a \(\Gamma_0(q)\)-level object, cup with a cohomology class \(x_\alpha\in H^1\), and push forward back to the original level (venkatesh2019derivedheckealgebra pages 5-8). This is exactly a degree-shifting operator built from pull–push along correspondences, with the shift governed by the inserted cohomology class.

Harris–Venkatesh provide an explicit derived Hecke action in the weight-one modular setting by identifying the derived Hecke algebra as the cohomology of a Hecke DG endomorphism algebra, and showing that an element \(h_x\in H^*(K_x,S)\) acts on cohomology by an explicit composite: restriction/conjugation (pull), cup with \(h_x\), and corestriction (push), with degree shift equal to \(\deg(h_x)\) (harris2019derivedheckealgebra pages 30-33). They further give an explicit derived Hecke element \(T_{q,z}\) coming from a degree-1 class at the double coset \(x=\mathrm{diag}(q,1)\) (harris2019derivedheckealgebra pages 30-33).

Ronchetti’s groupoid-cohomology model makes the pull–cup–push nature of derived Hecke convolution completely explicit: the derived Hecke algebra is compactly supported cohomology on a Hecke groupoid, and the product is defined by pulling back along projections, cupping, then pushing forward by summing corestrictions over finite fibers (ronchetti2016satakemapfor pages 9-12). Khare–Ronchetti likewise define derived Hecke algebras as Ext-algebras and equivalently as compactly supported equivariant cohomology classes on \((G/K)\times (G/K)\) with a convolution product; they highlight explicit degree-1 “derived diamond operators” realized via pullback from torus cohomology followed by cup product, giving a concrete degree-shifting mechanism in the \(p\)-adic/ordinary setting (khare2023derivedheckeaction pages 13-16, khare2023derivedheckeaction pages 11-13).

2. Motivic/K-theoretic sources propose (or partially realize) degree-shifting motivic actions on arithmetic/coherent cohomology, often mediated by derived Hecke operators.
A central theme in Venkatesh is that derived Hecke operators provide a \(p\)-adic realization of conjectural operations coming from motivic cohomology / algebraic \(K\)-theory; he formulates the conjecture that the “motivic \(\mathbf{Q}\)-lattice” inside the relevant \(p\)-adic Galois cohomology groups preserves rational cohomology (venkatesh2019derivedheckealgebra pages 1-3, venkatesh2019derivedheckealgebra pages 3-5).

Prasanna–Venkatesh explicitly hypothesize that a “hidden, degree-shifting action” of a motivic cohomology group (tied to adjoint \(L\)-values) explains spectral degeneracy in arithmetic cohomology; they provide an explicit description of the action after tensoring with \(\mathbf{C}\) via regulators and conjecture that it respects \(\mathbf{Q}\)-structures (prasanna2021automorphiccohomologymotivic pages 1-3). They also describe how motivic classes can produce distinguished \(\mathbf{Q}\)-lines in shifted cohomological degree (prasanna2021automorphiccohomologymotivic pages 8-10, prasanna2021automorphiccohomologymotivic pages 42-45).

Horawa’s conjectures for Hilbert modular varieties propose that a Stark/motivic group acts (via its exterior algebra) in a degree-shifting way on coherent cohomology Hecke-eigenspaces, with a mod-\(p\) realization “by derived Hecke operators” and a predicted lift to a single rational/integral motivic action (horawa2023motivicactionon pages 1-3). Horawa–Prasanna formulate an analogous motivic-action conjecture for Siegel modular forms in which a motivic cohomology group acts via the Deligne regulator to send a degree-0 class to a degree-1 class, and conjecture preservation of rational/integral structures (horawa2025motivicactionfor pages 1-3).

Finally, Khare–Ronchetti record a concrete arithmetic instance where integral motivic classes (identified inside a Tate module) control derived diamond operators acting by pullback along an explicit map from an “integral motivic lattice” \(\Delta\) to a local torus, i.e. an explicit motivic-to-derived-Hecke bridge (khare2023derivedheckeaction pages 45-48).

3. Explicit \(\mathfrak{sl}_2\)-triples with \([e,f]=h\) do appear in correspondence algebras acting on cohomology/Chow, but not (in the retrieved sources here) as a theorem inside derived Hecke actions on arithmetic cohomology.
On the “correspondence algebra” side, explicit Lefschetz \(\mathfrak{sl}_2\)-triples are constructed and satisfy the commutator identity \([e,f]=h\) with \(h\) a diagonal grading operator. Ramón-Marí constructs operators \(L\) (cup by hyperplane class), \(\Lambda\) (Lefschetz dual), and \(H\) (grading) and states commutator relations identifying \((\Lambda,L,H)\) as an \(\mathfrak{sl}_2\)-triple, including \([\Lambda,L]=H\) (mari2010selfcorrespondencesofthe pages 1-4). Kretschmer states existence/uniqueness of a lowering operator \(f_a\) given a hard-Lefschetz class \(a\), with \([e_a,f_a]=h\), and discusses a correspondence-induced grading operator on Chow groups in hyperkähler-type settings (kretschmer2020thechowring pages 7-11). Neguţ–Oberdieck–Yin lift an LLV Lie algebra action to Chow for \(\mathrm{Hilb}^n(K3)\) and produce an explicit grading correspondence \(h\) that appears in every \((e_\alpha,f_\alpha,h)\) \(\mathfrak{sl}_2\)-triple and acts diagonally by \(i-n\) on \(H^{2i}\) (negut2019motivicdecompositionsfor pages 1-4). Polishchuk constructs explicit pull–push correspondence operators acting on Chow groups of symmetric powers/Jacobians with explicit commutator formulas and remarks that, for a single curve, they induce a \(\mathbf{Z}\)-form of a Lefschetz \(\mathfrak{sl}_2\)-action (polishchuk2008algebraiccycleson pages 1-3).

However, within the derived Hecke literature retrieved and scanned here (Venkatesh; Harris–Venkatesh; Khare–Ronchetti; Ronchetti), while there are explicit degree-shifting operators and graded(-commutative) algebra structures, I did not find an explicit theorem/conjecture phrased as: two derived Hecke operators \(e\) and \(f\) (both realized as pull–push correspondences on arithmetic cohomology) satisfy \([e,f]=h\) where \(h\) is a diagonal grading operator on cohomology. The derived Hecke algebra is typically graded-commutative in the relevant degree-1 exterior-algebra parts (so the naive commutator often vanishes), and the key conjectural structure is instead an exterior-algebra action of a motivic/Galois cohomology space on arithmetic cohomology (venkatesh2019derivedheckealgebra pages 31-34, venkatesh2019derivedheckealgebra pages 1-3), rather than a Lefschetz-type \(\mathfrak{sl}_2\)-action.

Scientific implications.
The evidence supports a bifurcation: (i) arithmetic/automorphic settings supply explicit correspondence algebras (derived Hecke) that naturally contain degree-shifting operators predicted to arise from motivic cohomology and possibly preserve rational/integral structures (venkatesh2019derivedheckealgebra pages 1-3, prasanna2021automorphiccohomologymotivic pages 1-3, horawa2023motivicactionon pages 1-3, horawa2025motivicactionfor pages 1-3); (ii) explicit \(\mathfrak{sl}_2\)-triples with \([e,f]=h\) are well-established in Lefschetz/motivic-Chow contexts via correspondences (mari2010selfcorrespondencesofthe pages 1-4, kretschmer2020thechowring pages 7-11, negut2019motivicdecompositionsfor pages 1-4). The missing link, in the material retrieved here, is an explicit construction identifying arithmetic derived-Hecke degree-shifters with the raising/lowering operators of an \(\mathfrak{sl}_2\) acting on (motivic) cohomology of an arithmetic scheme.

Limitations of the survey.
(i) Retrieval limits: several potentially relevant works (e.g., broader expositions/ICM lectures) were unobtainable in this run, and some later sections of the retrieved papers may contain additional structure not captured by the targeted page ranges. (ii) Image extraction failed for key displayed formulas (e.g. Venkatesh’s equation defining \(T_{q,\alpha}\), and Ramón-Marí’s displayed commutator formulas), so the report relies on text-extracted evidence rather than figure/table crops. (iii) Conceptual mismatch: derived Hecke actions in the Venkatesh/Harris/Khare–Ronchetti tradition are often modeled as (graded-commutative) exterior-algebra actions of degree-1 classes, whereas an \(\mathfrak{sl}_2\)-triple requires both raising and lowering operators; the latter are naturally provided by Hard Lefschetz duality and are typically constructed using an inverse Lefschetz operator \(\Lambda\), whose algebraicity is closely tied to standard conjectures (mari2010selfcorrespondencesofthe pages 1-4). This suggests that “finding an \(\mathfrak{sl}_2\)-triple in derived Hecke” might require enlarging the algebra beyond the exterior part or incorporating additional non-commutative correspondences (e.g. geometric Satake/Hecke categories), which were not explicitly connected to the arithmetic derived Hecke constructions in the retrieved evidence.

Interpretation relative to the hypothesis.
The hypothesis that the triple is “contained within a larger correspondence algebra” is partially supported: degree-shifting operators are indeed naturally packaged in derived Hecke/correspondence algebras acting on arithmetic cohomology, and motivic cohomology is conjecturally responsible for these operators (venkatesh2019derivedheckealgebra pages 1-3, prasanna2021automorphiccohomologymotivic pages 1-3, horawa2023motivicactionon pages 1-3). But the more specific claim that there exist two such correspondence operators \(e\) and \(f\) in this setting with commutator \([e,f]=h\) is not supported by explicit theorems/conjectures in the retrieved derived Hecke sources; rather, \([e,f]=h\) appears explicitly in Lefschetz-type correspondence actions on cohomology/Chow of algebraic varieties (mari2010selfcorrespondencesofthe pages 1-4, kretschmer2020thechowring pages 7-11).
