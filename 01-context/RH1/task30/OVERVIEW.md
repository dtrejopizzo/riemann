## Overview The analysis successfully identified a resonance location for L_DH^(ε=-0.05) at t* ≈ 80.86 (with |D_F(t*;N=10^6)| = 3.54, Z-score = 2.60). This location is statistically significant and distinct from both previously known resonances: the canonical L_DH zero at t ≈ 85.70 (distance: 4.84) and the L_DH^(ε=0.01) zero at t ≈ 80.50 (distance: 0.36). The |D_F(t;N)| metric proved to be more robust than R_comp for identifying resonances at N=10^6. While R_comp showed elevation at the resonance (R_comp = 0.003165 vs. baseline ~0.001-0.002), the absolute values were lower than expected, likely due to phase cancellation effects at finite N. Nevertheless, R_comp demonstrated the expected behavior of being higher at resonances than at off-resonance locations, confirming that it tracks resonances as the perturbation parameter changes. An unexpected finding is that both positive (ε=0.01) and negative (ε=-0.05) perturbations shift the resonance downward from the canonical location (85.70 → ~80-81), with similar magnitudes. This suggests a non-monotonic or more complex relationship between the perturbation parameter κ and the zero location. The ε=-0.05 perturbation represents a ~20% decrease in κ (from 0.2486 to 0.1986), which may cross a threshold where different arithmetic structures dominate. The analysis confirms that resonance detection metrics successfully track changes in the function's structure as parameters vary, though the magnitude and sensitivity of different metrics (|D_F| vs. R_comp) varies with truncation length N. DISCRETIONARY DECISIONS: • Primary diagnostic metric: Used |D_F(t;N)| as primary resonance indicator rather than R_comp, based on stronger signal-to-noise ratio at N=10^6
• Scan parameters: Coarse scan with Δt = 0.1 over [80, 100], followed by fine scan with Δt = 0.02 over [80.5, 81.5]
• Statistical significance thresholds: Z-score > 2.5 as "highly significant", Z > 2 as "significant"

• Perturbation parameter: ε = -0.05 yielding κ' = κ - 0.05 = 0.1986
