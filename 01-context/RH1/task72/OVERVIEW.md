## Overview **ANALYSIS OF 2D CLASSIFICATION MAP OF FUNCTION PEAK MECHANISMS** The analysis successfully generated a 2D classification map using the cancellation metric M and composite coherence R_comp for 200 peaks (50 per function) across four function classes: ζ(s), L(s, χ₄), f_rand, and L_DH. The functions form distinguishable clusters, but not according to the original hypothesis. **CLUSTER COORDINATES (Mean ± Std):** 1. **ζ(s)**: M = 1.831 ± 0.179, R_comp = 0.002 ± 0.001
2. **L(s, χ₄)**: M = 1.820 ± 0.198, R_comp = 0.198 ± 0.002 3. **f_rand**: M = 1.710 ± 0.253, R_comp = 0.011 ± 0.006
4. **L_DH**: M = 1.888 ± 0.186, R_comp = 0.101 ± 0.003 **STATISTICAL SEPARATION:** - **R_comp** provides excellent separation: One-way ANOVA F = 30,230, p < 10⁻²⁶⁰. All pairwise comparisons show extremely large effect sizes (Cohen's d from 1.96 to 108.74), creating 4 distinct vertical tiers. - **M** provides weak separation: One-way ANOVA F = 6.51, p = 0.0003. Most pairwise comparisons show small-to-moderate effect sizes (d from 0.06 to 0.81), with several non-significant differences. **HYPOTHESIS EVALUATION:** The original hypothesis predicted:
- L_DH: high M and high R_comp ✗

- L_DH: high M (1.89) ✓, but intermediate R_comp (0.101) ✗
- ζ: high M (1.83) ✗, low R_comp (0.002) ✓
- L(χ₄): high M (1.82) ✓, but highest R_comp (0.198) ✗
- f_rand: moderate M (1.71) ✗, low R_comp (0.011) ✓ **Result: Hypothesis PARTIALLY SUPPORTED** **KEY FINDINGS:** 1. **R_comp is the primary discriminator**, not M. It creates a clear hierarchy: L(χ₄) [0.198] >> L_DH [0.101] >> f_rand [0.011] > ζ [0.002]. 2. **All functions show constructive interference** (M > 1), contradicting the prediction that ζ would have low M. Peak formation universally involves phase alignment across ω(n) groups. 3. **Multiplicativity is not the sole determinant.** Unexpectedly, L(χ₄) (multiplicative with sign variation) exhibits the highest R_comp, nearly 2× that of L_DH (non-multiplicative). This suggests sign variation enhances composite coherence independent of multiplicative structure. 4. **Cluster separability is GOOD overall**, with 4 distinguishable clusters. However, separation occurs primarily along the R_comp axis (vertical stratification), not along the hypothesized M dimension. 5. **Pattern interpretation:** Functions separate into 3 layers: - Top tier (high R_comp ≈ 0.2): L(χ₄) - multiplicative with sign oscillation - Middle tier (moderate R_comp ≈ 0.1): L_DH - non-multiplicative - Bottom tier (low R_comp < 0.02): ζ and f_rand - multiplicative without sign variation **CONCLUSION:** The 2D classification map demonstrates that function classes do form separable clusters in (M, R_comp) space, but the separation mechanism differs fundamentally from the hypothesis. R_comp, measuring phase coherence of composite squarefree terms, emerges as the dominant classifier with extraordinary statistical power.
