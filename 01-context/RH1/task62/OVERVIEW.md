## Overview ## Analysis Summary The hypothesis that L_DH resonances correspond to a "balanced" state of interference is **partially supported** by the data. The analysis reveals that M_coh values at R_comp peaks (median = 1.551) are statistically lower than M_coh values at |D_DH| peaks (median = 1.619), with p = 0.020 (one-sided Mann-Whitney U test). However, the effect size is small (rank-biserial r = 0.238), and both distributions show M_coh values significantly greater than 1 (90% and 98% respectively), indicating constructive interference in both cases. ### Key Quantitative Findings: 1. **Distribution A (R_comp peaks)**: Mean M_coh = 1.436 ± 0.269, Median = 1.551
2. **Distribution B (|D_DH| peaks)**: Mean M_coh = 1.565 ± 0.161, Median = 1.619
3. **Statistical significance**: - Two-sided Mann-Whitney U: p = 0.040 (significant) - One-sided test (A < B): p = 0.020 (significant) - Effect size: r_rb = 0.238 (small)
4. **Both distributions exceed M_coh = 1**: - Distribution A: 90% of peaks have M_coh > 1 - Distribution B: 98% of peaks have M_coh > 1
5. **High peak overlap**: 76% (38/50) of peaks appear in both distributions ### Interpretation: The results provide evidence for a **nuanced** trade-off mechanism rather than a simple "balanced" state: - R_comp peaks (best resonance indicators) do occur at slightly lower M_coh values than |D_DH| peaks, supporting the hypothesis of a trade-off between term-class coherence and composite coherence
- However, the difference is modest (Δmedian = -0.068, ~4% relative difference), and both types of peaks occur in a regime of **strong constructive interference** (M_coh >> 1)
- The 76% overlap between peak sets suggests that R_comp and |D_DH| are largely detecting the same resonance events, with R_comp providing slightly refined localization
- The hypothesis that R_comp peaks would show "significantly lower" M_coh than |D_DH| peaks is technically supported but overstates the magnitude of the difference ### Conclusion: L_DH resonances are characterized by **elevated term-class coherence** (M_coh > 1), not by a reduction to neutral or "balanced" interference. The data suggest that maximal composite coherence (R_comp) occurs when term-class coherence is high but not necessarily maximal, consistent with a subtle optimization trade-off. The resonance mechanism appears to require constructive interference across omega classes, but with a specific configuration that maximizes composite squarefree coherence rather than simply maximizing the vector sum of omega-class contributions. --- ## Discretionary Decisions - **Time sampling resolution**: Used dt = 5.0 for computational feasibility, resulting in 1801 time points over [1000, 10000]. Higher resolution (dt = 1.0) would provide finer peak detection but was computationally prohibitive.
- **Truncation length**: Used N = 10^5 as specified in the dataset description as a practical limit for Python computation, though N = 10^6 is the target for high-resolution analysis.
- **Vectorized computation method**: Used chunked vectorization with chunk_size = 50 to balance memory constraints and computational efficiency. Alternative: Kahan summation (too slow) or full vectorization (memory overflow).
- **Peak identification**: Used simple ranking (np.argsort) to identify top 50 peaks rather than local maxima detection. This captures global peaks but may miss significant local features.
- **Statistical test selection**: Used Mann-Whitney U test (non-parametric) rather than t-test due to non-normal distribution shapes visible in histograms and unequal variances (Levene's test F = 5.47, p = 0.021).
- **Significance threshold**: Set α = 0.05 for statistical tests (conventional threshold).
- **Effect size metric**: Used rank-biserial correlation as the effect size for Mann-Whitney U test, interpreted using conventional thresholds (small: 0.1-0.3, medium: 0.3-0.5, large: >0.5).
- **Composite squarefree definition**: Defined composite squarefree as ω(n) ≥ 2, consistent with the dataset description. This excludes primes (ω(n) = 1) and 1 (ω(n) = 0).
- **R_comp computation**: Computed as mean resultant length (|Σ phasors| / N_phasors) rather than RMS or other measures of coherence.
