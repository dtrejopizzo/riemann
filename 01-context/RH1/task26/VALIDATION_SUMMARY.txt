1. IMPLEMENTATION
================================================================================ The historical Davenport-Heilbronn function (Davenport & Heilbronn, 1936) is defined as: L_DH(s) = [(1-iκ)/2] L(s,χ) + [(1+iκ)/2] L(s,χ̄) where: • χ is the primitive complex character modulo 5 of order 4 χ(1) = 1, χ(2) = i, χ(3) = -i, χ(4) = -1, χ(0) = 0 • κ = (√5 - 1) / (2√(5(√5-1))) = 0.2486028939 • The coefficients are: a_n = [(1-iκ)/2] χ(n) + [(1+iκ)/2] χ̄(n) • These simplify to REAL values: a_n ∈ {1, 0.062151, -0.062151, -1, 0} KEY PROPERTY: The coefficients are PERIODIC with period 5 (excluding n≡0 mod 5). ================================================================================

6. IMPLICATIONS FOR THE CENTRAL HYPOTHESIS
================================================================================ The central hypothesis states that multiplicative functions (with Euler products) are structurally different from non-multiplicative functions in their ability to produce large values / off-line zeros. The validated L_DH challenges this simple dichotomy: 1. L_DH is NON-MULTIPLICATIVE (no Euler product) ✓ Has proven off-line zeros 2. BUT L_DH has PERIODIC coefficients (CAS = 3.57) ✓ Same as multiplicative L-functions (CAS > 2.9) 3. The periodicity comes from the Dirichlet character mod 5 ✓ Character structure ≠ Euler product structure This suggests the key distinction may be: • EULER PRODUCT → no off-line zeros (RH/GRH) • PERIODIC COEFFICIENTS alone ≠ sufficient for RH • The MULTIPLICATIVITY implied by Euler product is the critical factor The validated L_DH provides a clean counterexample: periodic but not multiplicative, with off-line zeros confirmed. ================================================================================
