## Overview ## Analysis of Extreme Metric Values for L_DH^(ε=0) ### Main Finding At the resonance peak t* = 1054.94, the L_DH^(ε=0) function exhibits |D(t*)| = 19.55, which is 69.9× larger than the Riemann zeta function (|D(t*)| = 0.28). This extreme magnitude difference arises from **constructive alignment of term-class vectors** caused by the alternating coefficient structure a_n = (-1)^ω(n). ### Part 1: High Magnitude Mechanism (M_coh) - CONFIRMED ✓ **Hypothesis:** The alternating signs (-1)^k cause term-class vectors S_k to align constructively, minimizing cancellation. **Evidence:**
- **L_DH^(ε=0):** The signed vectors (-1)^k S_k align with angles of only 4°-17° between consecutive vectors, yielding a **coherence ratio of 98.8%** (|Σ(-1)^k S_k| / Σ|S_k| = 0.988). Almost all potential magnitude is retained through constructive interference.
- **ζ (baseline):** The vectors S_k alternate in phase with angles of ~163°-179° between consecutive vectors, yielding a **coherence ratio of only 4.9%** (0.049). Massive destructive cancellation occurs.
- **Quantitative comparison:** - L_DH: S_1 through S_4 have magnitudes [3.89, 6.39, 5.40, 2.59] and align to sum to |D| = 19.55 - ζ: S_1 through S_4 have magnitudes [3.83, 6.03, 4.79, 2.06] but cancel to sum to |D| = 0.28 **Conclusion:** The hypothesis is **confirmed**. The alternating coefficient structure creates phase alignment that produces the characteristic high |D(t*)| values. ### Part 2: Composite Term Contribution (R_comp) - PARTIALLY CONTRADICTED ✗ **Hypothesis:** The low R_comp is caused by highly oscillatory a_n for composite squarefree numbers, leading to maximal phase incoherence. **Evidence:**
- **Phase distributions:** Both functions show **highly uniform** phase distributions for composite terms: - ζ: mean resultant vector magnitude = 0.0023 - L_DH^(ε=0): mean resultant vector magnitude = 0.025 - Both are close to 0, indicating near-perfect uniformity across [-π, π] - **R_comp at resonances:** Contrary to expectation, L_DH has **higher** R_comp than ζ at their respective peaks: - L_DH peaks: mean R_comp = 0.756 (composite terms are 76% of total) - ζ peaks: mean R_comp = 0.203 (composite terms are 20% of total) - **Mechanism clarification:** The difference is not in phase incoherence among composite terms, but in the **relative contribution** of composite vs. prime terms: - L_DH: Composite sum magnitude = 14.75, Prime sum = 3.89 → composite dominates - ζ: Composite sum magnitude = 2.86, Prime sum = 3.83 → more balanced **Conclusion:** The hypothesis is **contradicted** by the data. Both functions exhibit uniform phase distributions for composite terms. The key difference lies in constructive vs. destructive interference between term-class groups (primes vs. composites), not in incoherence within the composite class. The "low R_comp" mentioned in the dataset description likely refers to a different metric definition or normalization than |composite sum|/|total sum|. ### Mechanistic Summary The coefficient structure a_n = (-1)^ω(n) produces large |D(t*)| values through:
