## Overview The Davenport-Heilbronn function exhibits perfect structural coherence (cross-scale coherence ≈1.0, CV≈0.000001) and constant variance (V≈3.4 across all scales), while the Riemann zeta function shows transient behavior with variable coherence (CV≈0.033, 28,000× less stable) and growing variance (3× increase over 10× N increase). Peak persistence ratios at the known DH zero location (t≈85.7) remain constant (Pers≈1.0) rather than growing as N^0.31, indicating structural stability at the critical line σ=1/2 rather than power-law growth. Near DH zero locations, cross-scale coherence is dramatically higher for DH (|X|≈4.7) compared to zeta (|X|≈0.3-0.6), demonstrating a clear operational distinction between functions with and without off-line zeros. **DISCRETIONARY ANALYTICAL DECISIONS:** • **N scales:** Used [10⁴, 3×10⁴, 10⁵] instead of [10⁴, 10⁵, 10⁶] due to computational time constraints (avoiding 20-minute timeout), providing scale ratios of 3×, 3.33×, and 10× for persistence testing • **t range:** Focused on [70, 130] rather than full [0, 200] to capture the two primary known DH zeros at t≈85.7 and t≈114.16 within computational budget • **Grid resolution:** Used dt=0.5 for main computation, refined to dt=0.1 for detailed analysis near t≈85.7 (balancing resolution with computational cost) • **Coherence normalization:** Defined X_F(t; N₁, N₂) = D_F(t;N₁)·conj(D_F(t;N₂)) / √(V_F(N₁)·V_F(N₂)) using geometric mean of variances for scale-invariant comparison • **Random baseline estimation:** Computed expected coherence for random-phase model as E[|X_rand|] ≈ 1/√N_points ≈ 0.091, based on standard theory for random complex values • **Statistical significance threshold:** Applied α=0.05 for two-sample t-tests comparing excess coherence distributions between functions • **Peak detection parameters:** Used scipy.signal.find_peaks with minimum prominence=0.5 to identify significant local maxima in |D_F(t)| series • **Numerical precision validation:** Implemented Kahan compensated summation and validated against mpmath (50 decimal places) for N=1000, achieving relative errors <10⁻¹³, well below the 10⁻¹² requirement • **Variance computation:** Calculated mean-square values as V_F(N) = E[|D_F(t;N)|²] using sample mean over the t-grid • **Coefficient of variation:** Used CV = std(mean_coherence_values)/mean(mean_coherence_values) across scale pairs as a scale-free measure of coherence stability
