- Composites (non-PP): 0.18× — strong suppression ## Key Observations 1. **Prime Amplification in L_DH**: The primes-only subset contributes the largest coherence signal for L_DH (|X| = 2.424), which is 22× larger than the corresponding ζ signal. 2. **Composite Suppression**: Composites contribute LESS to L_DH coherence than to ζ (ratio = 0.18×), directly contradicting the hypothesis. 3. **Pattern Stability**: Verification across t ∈ [80, 90] confirms the pattern, though with variation. At certain peaks (e.g., t ≈ 87.5, 88.0), prime contributions are >100× larger than composites. 4. **Mechanistic Insight**: The non-multiplicative coefficients a_p in L_DH (which depend on p mod 5 via the character χ) create constructive interference among primes that is absent in the uniform a_p = 1 structure of multiplicative functions. ## Interpretation The resonance signature in L_DH arises from **selective amplification of prime-phase relationships**, not from composite-number interactions. The non-multiplicativity matters specifically at **prime numbers**, where it breaks the uniform phase structure of the Euler product. This finding redirects attention to the role of primes in generating L_DH's off-line zeros, suggesting that understanding the character-induced phase structure at primes (rather than composite multiplicative structure) is key to explaining why L_DH fails the Riemann Hypothesis. --- ## Discretionary Analytical Decisions - **Cross-scale coherence metric**: Used unnormalized coherence X_F(t; N₁, N₂) = D_F(t; N₁) · conj(D_F(t; N₂)) to allow magnitude comparisons across subsets.
