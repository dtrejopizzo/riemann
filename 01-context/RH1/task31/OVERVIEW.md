The zeta function demonstrates approximately 5.4-fold stronger phase coherence than DH, directly opposite to the predicted "phase alignment mechanism." ## Quantitative Evidence **Rayleigh Test Results for Top 20 Peaks:** - **Zeta function**: Mean resultant length R = 0.009015 ± 0.003025, with 13/20 peaks showing significant non-uniformity (p < 0.05) and 10/20 showing highly significant non-uniformity (p < 0.001). However, the function with proven off-line zeros (DH) shows perfectly uniform phases, while the function conjectured to have no off-line zeros (ζ) shows significant phase alignment. 2. **Alternative Explanations**: - The weak (R ≈ 0.01) but statistically significant phase coherence in zeta may reflect **log-correlated Gaussian field structure** or extreme value statistics (Gaussian Multiplicative Chaos), not zero-related phenomena - DH peaks may arise from **composite number contributions** rather than prime phase alignment, since a_n^{DH} are non-multiplicative - The relevant phase structure for off-line zeros may operate at a different scale or through different Dirichlet series terms than analyzed here 3. **Methodological Robustness**: Testing with ALL terms (not just primes) and at different truncation levels (N = 10⁴, 10⁵, 10⁶) confirms the finding. The multiplicative structure appears to generate weak but detectable phase coherence even without off-line zeros. ## Discretionary Analytical Decisions - **Significance threshold**: Set α = 0.05 for Rayleigh test, with additional reporting at α = 0.001 for highly significant results
