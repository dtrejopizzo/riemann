## Overview # ANALYSIS RESULTS: Resonance Detection in L-Functions ## Main Finding **The research hypothesis is REJECTED.** Contrary to the prediction, non-multiplicative functions (Davenport-Heilbronn family) do NOT show significantly higher kurtosis and resonance scores than multiplicative functions. Instead, the opposite pattern is observed: **multiplicative functions, particularly the random multiplicative function f_rand, exhibit substantially higher kurtosis and resonance scores than the non-multiplicative DH function.** ## Quantitative Evidence ### Kurtosis (K_F)
- **Riemann ζ** (multiplicative): K_F ≈ 1.8 - 2.6
- **L(s, χ₄)** (multiplicative real character): K_F ≈ 5.1 - 5.2
- **f_rand** (random multiplicative): K_F ≈ 9.4 - 11.4 (**HIGHEST**)
- **L_DH** (non-multiplicative): K_F ≈ 2.5 (**LOWEST among main comparisons**) Mann-Whitney U test: Multiplicative > Non-multiplicative (p = 0.032468, **significant**)
Effect size (Cohen's d): 1.28 (large effect) ### Resonance Score (RS = M_F / √V_F)
- **Riemann ζ**: RS ≈ 2.1 - 2.4
- **L(s, χ₄)**: RS ≈ 3.8 - 3.9
- **f_rand**: RS ≈ 4.9 - 5.8 (**HIGHEST**)
- **L_DH**: RS ≈ 2.4 (comparable to ζ) Mann-Whitney U test: Multiplicative > Non-multiplicative (p = 0.197, not significant at α = 0.05)
Effect size (Cohen's d): 1.39 (large effect) ### Scaling Behavior (Critical Test)
The research program predicts that DH peaks should grow as N^(β₀ - 1/2) = N^0.3085, giving:
- **Expected ratio** M_F(10⁵) / M_F(10⁴): **2.03**
- **Observed ratio for L_DH**: **1.00** (NO growth) For comparison:
- Riemann ζ: ratio = 0.97
- L(s, χ₄): ratio = 0.97
- f_rand: ratio = 1.34 **The DH function shows NO evidence of power-law peak growth, contradicting the theoretical prediction.** ### Tail Probabilities
Fraction of samples with |D_F| > λ√V_F: | Function | P_2 | P_3 | P_4 | P_5 |
|----------|-----|-----|-----|-----|
| ζ | 3% | 0% | 0% | 0% |
| χ₄ | 6% | 2% | 0% | 0% |
| f_rand | 5% | 2% | 1% | 0.5% |
| L_DH | 5% | 0% | 0% | 0% | The random multiplicative function f_rand shows the heaviest tails, with detectable probability even at λ = 5. ## Interpretation ### Why This Result Contradicts the Hypothesis 1. **Expected**: The DH function's known off-line zeros at (σ, t) = (0.8085, 85.7), (0.6508, 114.2), (0.5744, 166.5), (0.7243, 176.7) should produce resonance peaks in D_F(t; N) that grow with N and manifest as heavy-tailed distributions with high kurtosis. 2. **Observed**: The DH function shows: - Moderate kurtosis (K ≈ 2.5, comparable to ζ) - Moderate resonance scores (RS ≈ 2.4, comparable to ζ) - No power-law peak growth with increasing N - No elevated tail probabilities 3. **Unexpected**: The random multiplicative function shows: - Highest kurtosis (K ≈ 10-11) - Highest resonance scores (RS ≈ 5-6) - Substantial peak growth with N (ratio = 1.34) - Heaviest tails ### Possible Explanations 1. **Limited height range**: The sampled range t ∈ [80, 190] may not capture the resonance signature optimally, though it includes known zero locations. 2. **Finite N effects**: At N ~ 10⁵, the expected resonance signature may still be too weak to dominate over statistical fluctuations. 3. **Random multiplicativity effect**: Random multiplicative functions may exhibit stronger heavy-tailed behavior than structured functions (ζ, χ₄, L_DH) due to the absence of arithmetic cancellations that occur in structured coefficient sequences. 4. **Theoretical reconsideration needed**: The premise that off-line zeros manifest as heavy-tailed distributions in D_F(t; N) partial sums may require re-examination. The signature may be more subtle than kurtosis/resonance scores at these scales. ## Validation Against Research Program Criteria The research program (page 4) specifies mandatory validation conditions: | Condition | Expected | Observed | Status |

The detector is invalid." ## Statistical Significance - **Kurtosis comparison**: p = 0.032 (significant), but in the **opposite direction** (multiplicative > non-multiplicative)
- **Resonance score comparison**: p = 0.197 (not significant)
- **Effect sizes**: Large (Cohen's d > 1.2), indicating substantial differences between groups ## Conclusion The core premise that kurtosis and resonance scores can distinguish multiplicative from non-multiplicative functions is **falsified** by this computational experiment. The random multiplicative function exhibits the strongest signatures that the hypothesis attributed to non-multiplicative functions. The Davenport-Heilbronn function, despite having proven off-line zeros, does not show the expected heavy-tailed distribution or elevated resonance scores in the D_F(t; N) partial sum at N ~ 10⁴-10⁵ over the sampled height range. --- ## DISCRETIONARY ANALYTICAL DECISIONS • **Truncation lengths N**: Selected N ∈ {10⁴, 10⁵} rather than including 10⁶ due to computational time constraints (pattern already clear at 10⁵) • **Height range t**: Selected t ∈ [80, 190] to cover known DH zeros at t ≈ 85.7, 114.2, 166.5, 176.7 • **Sampling resolution**: Used Δt = 2π/log(N) as specified (Nyquist-like resolution): Δt ≈ 0.68 for N=10⁴, Δt ≈ 0.55 for N=10⁵ • **Statistical test**: Mann-Whitney U test (non-parametric) chosen over t-test due to small sample size (n=6 per group), no normality assumption required, robustness to heavy-tailed distributions • **Significance threshold**: Set α = 0.05 (standard scientific convention) • **DH perturbations**: Used ε ∈ {-0.01, 0, +0.01} to test robustness beyond single DH counterexample • **Random seed**: seed=42 for reproducibility of random multiplicative function f_rand • **Numerical algorithm**: Kahan compensated summation implemented for numerical stability with N ~ 10⁵ terms • **Effect size**: Cohen's d with pooled standard deviation for comparing independent groups • **Figure design**: Two-panel vertical layout (stacked subfigures) showing kurtosis and resonance score comparisons
