## Overview The strong resonance observed in L(s, λ(n)) at t* = 7563.5 with |D(t*)| = 46.88 is caused by constructive alignment of term-class vectors S_k, but the mechanism differs fundamentally from the hypothesis. The hypothesis proposed that vectors S_k, when signed by (-1)^k, would align in the complex plane. However, the analysis reveals that the S_k vectors are ALREADY optimally aligned without any additional correction. **Key Findings:** 1. **Peak Identification**: The highest magnitude peak occurs at t* = 7563.5 with |D(t*)| = 46.875785. 2. **Term-Class Decomposition**: At t*, the function decomposes as D(t*) = Σ S_k where S_k = Σ_{Ω(n)=k} λ(n)/n^(1/2+it*). The first six term-classes (k=1-6) contribute |Σ S_k| = 51.55, accounting for 110% of the total magnitude (the slight excess is canceled by higher-k contributions). 3. **Intrinsic Alignment Mechanism**: The multiplicative structure λ(n) = (-1)^Ω(n) means that S_k = (-1)^k T_k, where T_k = Σ_{Ω(n)=k} n^(-1/2-it*) are unsigned sums. The critical finding is: - Unsigned vectors T_k have nearly random phases (std = 109.2°) with destructive interference (R = 0.070) - The (-1)^k factors in λ(n) act as built-in phase correctors that align the vectors - The resulting S_k vectors have coherent phases (std = 40.4°) with strong constructive interference (R = 0.822) 4. **Hypothesis Refutation**: Applying an ADDITIONAL phase correction (-1)^k to the already-aligned S_k vectors DESTROYS the alignment, reducing R from 0.822 to 0.070. This is the opposite of the hypothesized behavior. 5. **Phase Analysis**: The S_k vectors have phases progressing smoothly from 16.9° (k=1) to 134.4° (k=6), forming a "fan" pattern that sums constructively. When double-corrected by (-1)^k, the phases become scattered (std increases from 40.4° to 109.2°). 6. **Quantitative Alignment**: The ratio |Σ S_k| / Σ |S_k| = 0.822 indicates that 82.2% of the maximum possible magnitude is achieved, demonstrating strong constructive interference. **Conclusion:** The constructive interference mechanism IS responsible for the high peak magnitude of L(s, λ(n)), confirming that resonance arises from term-class vector alignment. However, the phase correction by (-1)^k is NOT an external mechanism that must be applied—it is INTRINSIC to the function definition λ(n) = (-1)^Ω(n). The multiplicative structure creates the alignment automatically, distinguishing L(s, λ(n)) from functions where post-hoc correction might be needed.
