<conclusion>
At the resonant peak of L_DH (t=84.21, N=10⁶), the phase distribution of all terms is non-uniform (p=0.0034) while the prime-only subset is uniform (p=0.70), confirming that composite terms break phase uniformity; in contrast, for the Riemann zeta function at its peak (t=102.46, N=10⁶), both all terms (p<10⁻⁴⁰) and prime-only terms (p=4.4×10⁻⁴) exhibit non-uniform phase distributions.
</conclusion> <methods>
The analysis computed Davenport-Heilbronn coefficients a_n = ((1-iκ)/2)·χ(n) + ((1+iκ)/2)·χ̄(n) for n=1 to 10⁶, where χ is the primitive complex character mod 5 with χ(1)=1, χ(2)=i, χ(3)=-i, χ(4)=-1, and κ=(√5-1)/(2√5·√(√5-1))≈0.249. Peak locations were identified by scanning D_F(t;N) = Σ_{n≤N} a_n n^(-1/2-it) over ranges [80,90] for L_DH and [100,200] for Zeta, locating maxima at t_DH=84.208 (|D|=4.079) and t_zeta=102.460 (|D|=11.885). At these peaks, phases θ_n = arg(a_n n^(-1/2-it)) mod 2π were extracted for (1) all n=1 to 10⁶ and (2) the 78,498 primes ≤10⁶ using the Sieve of Eratosthenes. The Rayleigh test for circular uniformity was performed, computing mean resultant length R = |Σexp(iθ_n)|/n and test statistic z=n·R², with p-values calculated using the asymptotic exponential approximation p≈exp(-z). Libraries used: NumPy 1.26.4 for numerical computation, SciPy for statistics, pandas for data handling, and matplotlib for visualization.

The results confirm the research hypothesis and reveal the mechanism of resonance in non-multiplicative L-functions. For L_DH, the non-uniformity of all-term phases (p=0.0034) combined with uniformity of prime-only phases (p=0.70) demonstrates that **composite numbers are responsible for phase correlation at resonant peaks**. This occurs because L_DH coefficients are non-multiplicative: a_mn ≠ a_m·a_n, so composite terms acquire unique phases not determined by their prime factors. At the resonant height t≈84.21, these composite phases align constructively while prime phases remain random—a phenomenon impossible in multiplicative functions like ζ where a_n is completely determined by a_p for primes p|n. The contrasting behavior of Zeta, where both all-terms and prime-only phases are non-uniform, indicates that Zeta's peaks arise from a fundamentally different mechanism: coherent alignment of **prime** phases that propagates multiplicatively to composites. The much stronger non-uniformity for Zeta (p<10⁻⁴⁰ vs p=0.0034) reflects the larger peak magnitude (|D|=11.88 vs 4.08) and tighter phase concentration (R=0.0098 vs 0.0024). This analysis extends Report r6's prime-only findings, showing that the prime-phase analysis was "too narrow" for detecting L_DH resonance precisely because L_DH's resonance operates through composite terms. The result provides computational evidence that the Euler product's multiplicative structure prevents the kind of composite-driven phase alignment that produces off-critical-line zeros in L_DH, supporting the theoretical framework that multiplicativity is a protective mechanism against RH violation.
