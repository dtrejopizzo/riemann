This indicates the peak at t ≈ 84.2 is **not associated with an off-line zero** but represents general fluctuation dynamics of the partial sum. The analysis reveals that:
1. We are in a **pre-asymptotic regime** for N ≤ 10^6

3. The hypothesis that tracking the shifting peak would reveal growth closer to theory is **not supported** by the data ## Conclusion Tracking the shifting peak location as N increases does not reveal a growth exponent closer to the theoretical value. The observed exponent (α = 0.00336) remains orders of magnitude smaller than theory (α = 0.31), and the peak itself is not located at the known zero position.
