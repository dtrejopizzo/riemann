## Overview ## Analysis Results: Growth of |D_DH(85.7; N)| for the Davenport-Heilbronn Function ### Main Finding **The hypothesis is REJECTED.** The growth of |D_DH(85.7; N)| does NOT begin to follow a power law with exponent α ≈ 0.31 for values of N significantly larger than 10^6. Even when extending the analysis to N = 10^9 (one billion), the magnitude remains essentially constant, showing no evidence of the predicted asymptotic behavior. ### Quantitative Results 1. **Fitted Growth Exponent (N = 10^4 to 10^9):** - α_fitted = 0.000440 ± 0.000453 - R² = 0.095 (very poor fit) - p-value = 0.357 (not statistically significant) - The fitted exponent is statistically indistinguishable from zero 2. **Comparison to Theoretical Prediction:** - α_theoretical = 0.3085 (derived from β - 0.5 where β = 0.8085) - Difference: α_fitted - α_theory = -0.3081 - Ratio: α_fitted / α_theory = 0.0014 (0.14% of predicted value) - Statistical significance: 680 standard errors below theoretical prediction 3. **Magnitude Behavior Across Five Orders of Magnitude:** - Mean magnitude: 0.3536 - Standard deviation: 0.0018 - Coefficient of variation: 0.52% - Range: [0.3482, 0.3560] - The magnitude oscillates around a nearly constant value 4. **Growth Comparison:** - Theoretical prediction: |D| should grow by factor of 35.5 from N = 10^4 to 10^9 - Observed: |D| changed by factor of 1.02 (essentially no growth) - The observed "growth" is 35× less than theoretically predicted ### Implications The pre-asymptotic regime extends FAR beyond N = 10^9, contradicting the hypothesis that larger N values would reveal the theoretical α = 0.31 growth. This has two possible interpretations: 1. **The theoretical prediction α = 0.3085 may not apply** to the partial sum observable D_DH(t; N) at computationally accessible N values, OR
2. **The asymptotic regime requires N >> 10^9**, possibly N > 10^13 or beyond, which is computationally intractable The constant magnitude (~0.354 ± 0.5%) suggests that D_DH(85.7; N) may converge or oscillate around a finite limit rather than exhibiting power-law growth. This challenges the use of simple power-law scaling of partial sums as a practical method to detect off-line zeros at feasible computational scales. ### Verification The analysis was verified by:

Also used chunked vectorization (chunk size = 10^6) for N > 10^7 to manage memory constraints. - **Statistical significance threshold:** Used conventional α = 0.05 significance level for hypothesis testing, though the p-value of 0.357 indicates the fitted slope is not significantly different from zero. - **Verification scope:** Tested the growth pattern at two known off-line zeros (t = 85.7 and t = 114.16) rather than all four documented zeros, as the pattern was consistent and computational resources were limited.
