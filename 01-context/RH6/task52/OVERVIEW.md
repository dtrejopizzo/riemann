| `/workspace/downloads/omar_2025.pdf` | Zhao, "The positivity technique and low-lying zeros of Dirichlet L-functions" (2025) | PDF | 686 KB | --- # The Guinand-Weil Explicit Formula for Dirichlet L-functions ## Conventions (matching the user's notation) Let **h(t)** be an even Schwartz function on ℝ, with Fourier transform: > **g(u) = (1/(2π)) ∫₋∞^∞ h(t) e^{-iut} dt** Inverse: h(t) = ∫₋∞^∞ g(u) e^{iut} du. --- ## The ζ formula (for reference) > **∑_ρ h(γ_ρ) = h(i/2) + h(−i/2) − g(0) log π + (1/(2π)) ∫₋∞^∞ h(t) Re ψ(1/4 + it/2) dt − 2 ∑_{n≥1} Λ(n) n^{−1/2} g(log n)** where the LHS runs over ALL non-trivial zeros (both positive and negative γ). --- ## The L(s,χ) formula — χ primitive non-principal mod q **Source:** Chirre–Pereira–de Laat (2020), arXiv:2005.02393, equation (2.1), page 3; verified against Carneiro–Finder (2015), Lemma 5, eq. (2.4); and Molteni (2017), Theorem 7, eq. (3.15). Define:
- **a_χ = 0** if χ(−1) = +1 (χ **even**; gamma factor π^{−s/2} Γ(s/2))
- **a_χ = 1** if χ(−1) = −1 (χ **odd**; gamma factor π^{−(s+1)/2} Γ((s+1)/2)) Then: > ### **∑_{ρ: L(ρ,χ)=0} h((ρ − 1/2)/i) = g(0) · log(q/π) + (1/(2π)) ∫₋∞^∞ h(t) Re ψ(1/4 + a_χ/2 + it/2) dt − 2 ∑_{n≥2} Λ(n) Re(χ(n)) n^{−1/2} g(log n)** Under GRH, ρ = 1/2 + iγ, so h((ρ−1/2)/i) = h(γ), and the formula becomes: > **∑_{γ∈ℝ} h(γ) = g(0) log(q/π) + (1/(2π)) ∫₋∞^∞ h(t) Re ψ(1/4 + a_χ/2 + it/2) dt − 2 ∑_{n≥2} Λ(n) Re(χ(n)) n^{−1/2} g(log n)** --- ### Explicit breakdown by parity **χ EVEN (χ(−1) = +1, a_χ = 0):** > ∑_ρ h(γ_ρ) = g(0) log(q/π) + **(1/(2π)) ∫ h(t) Re ψ(1/4 + it/2) dt** − 2 ∑ Λ(n) Re(χ(n)) n^{−1/2} g(log n) The digamma argument **ψ(1/4 + it/2)** is identical to the ζ case. **χ ODD (χ(−1) = −1, a_χ = 1):** > ∑_ρ h(γ_ρ) = g(0) log(q/π) + **(1/(2π)) ∫ h(t) Re ψ(3/4 + it/2) dt** − 2 ∑ Λ(n) Re(χ(n)) n^{−1/2} g(log n) The digamma argument shifts to **ψ(3/4 + it/2)**. --- ## Answers to the five specific questions **(1) Are there h(i/2) + h(−i/2) terms?** **No.** These terms arise from the pole of ζ at s = 1 (and its image at s = 0). Since L(s,χ) is entire for non-principal χ (r(π) = 0), these terms are absent. **(2) The exact conductor/log term:** **g(0) · log(q/π)**, where q is the modulus (= conductor for primitive χ). This replaces −g(0) log π from the ζ formula; equivalently, the log(q/π) = log q − log π accounts for both the conductor q and the π from the gamma factor. **(3) The archimedean digamma integral:** The coefficient is **1/(2π)** (same as for ζ). - χ even: **(1/(2π)) ∫₋∞^∞ h(t) Re ψ(1/4 + it/2) dt** - χ odd: **(1/(2π)) ∫₋∞^∞ h(t) Re ψ(3/4 + it/2) dt** **(4) The prime sum:** **−2 ∑_{n≥2} Λ(n) · Re(χ(n)) · n^{−1/2} · g(log n)** - The overall factor is **−2** (same as ζ). - The character enters as **Re(χ(n))**, not χ(n) directly, and not 2 Re(χ(n)). - For real characters: Re(χ(n)) = χ(n), recovering the form with χ(n) directly. - For complex characters: Re(χ(n)) = (χ(n) + χ̄(n))/2, which arises because h and g are even, so g(log n) = g(−log n), and the two terms χ(n)g(log n) + χ̄(n)g(−log n) combine to 2 Re(χ(n)) g(log n). **(5) The LHS sum:** The sum runs over **all γ ∈ ℝ (both signs)** for L(s,χ) alone. Each non-trivial zero ρ of L(s,χ) with 0 < Re ρ < 1 contributes h((ρ−1/2)/i). Under GRH, this becomes h(γ) with γ ranging over all real imaginary parts (both positive and negative). For real χ, the zeros are symmetric (γ ↔ −γ), so the sum can be written as 2∑_{γ>0} h(γ). For complex χ, the zeros of L(s,χ) need not be symmetric about γ = 0 (the symmetry relates zeros of L(s,χ) to zeros of L(s,χ̄)), so one must sum over all γ without assuming pairing. --- ## Derivation from source equations The formula was derived by converting Chirre–Pereira–de Laat (2020), eq. (2.1), which uses the Fourier convention ĥ(ξ) = ∫ h(x) e^{−2πixξ} dx, to the user's convention g(u) = (1/(2π)) ∫ h(t) e^{−iut} dt via the identity **ĥ(ξ) = 2π g(2πξ)**, and specializing the general Carneiro–Finder (2015) formula to degree-1 Dirichlet L-functions (m=1, N=q, μ₁ = a_χ ∈ {0,1}). --- ## Numerical verification The formula was verified numerically to **14–17 significant digits** for: 1. **Riemann ζ**: h(t)=e^{−t²/2}, RHS − LHS ≈ 4×10⁻¹⁵ ✓
