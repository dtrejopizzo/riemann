</challenges> <discussion>
The toy resonance model — the simplest possible one with a flat resonator — already captures two of the three qualitative features the empirical M_jk is known to display: (i) the leading eigenvector is sign-uniform (all-positive after a single global sign choice), and (ii) the spectrum is decidedly low-rank with eigenvalues spanning ~6 orders of magnitude. The cosine similarity 0.85 between theoretical and empirical PC1 indicates the model identifies the correct dominant direction in stratum-space, with both peaking at ω=3 (the most populous stratum near 10⁶, ~3.8·10⁵ integers). However, the toy model under-concentrates spectral mass in PC1 (56% vs 87% empirical) and fails the >90% threshold that the hypothesis predicted. Inspection of the correlation matrices shows why: the empirical peak-selection induces strong long-range coupling across all ω-strata (correlations 0.5–0.93), whereas a flat resonator-weighted MVT integral only induces local (mostly nearest-neighbor) coupling. This is consistent with the literature reference (r39, r54) stating that off-diagonal structure in M_jk is dominantly produced by the peak-conditioning event itself, not by the unweighted (or flatly-weighted) bulk. Even the empirical matrix (86.6%) does not reach 90%; the hypothesis statement that this matches an empirical "f12" >90% observation appears to overstate the empirical zeta result at N=10⁶. The toy model is therefore best viewed as a successful first-order qualitative reconstruction (correct sign-coherent, low-rank direction) but quantitatively insufficient — a richer resonator design (e.g., R(t) with multiplicative coefficients tuned to amplify zeta, or explicit peak-truncation reweighting) would be needed to push PC1 concentration above 90%.
