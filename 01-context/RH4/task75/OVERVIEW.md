</conclusion> <methods>
1. Loaded the 11 (200, 8) complex S_k(t_p) arrays Sk_F{1,2,4,5p,5m,6,7,9,10,11,12}.npy from the unified range-matched dataset (description.npy confirms peaks of |D(t;N=10⁶)| in t∈[10⁴,2·10⁴], finufft-found, Kahan-summed S_k).
2. For each class, formed the 8×8 Hermitian PSD cross-term matrix M_jk = E_p[S_j(t_p)·conj(S_k(t_p))] = (Sᵀ·S*)/200 (Hermitian residual ≤ 1.1·10⁻¹⁶; trace equal to mean Σ_k|S_k|², verified).
3. Computed eigenvalues with numpy.linalg.eigvalsh, clipped negatives to 0, normalized p_i = λ_i/Σλ, and computed pc1_complex = λ_max/Σλ and H_complex = −Σ p_i log p_i (natural log).
4. Labeled classes: GRH-true = {F1, F2, F6, F7, F9, F10, F11}; GRH-false = {F4, F5p, F5m, F12} (F4 = Davenport-Heilbronn and F5p/F5m are its κ±0.05 perturbations, all DH-family RH-violators per the binding context; F12 is an explicit constructed violator).
5. Trained Logistic Regression and SVM (linear, RBF) on the 11 standardized 2-D points; evaluated leave-one-out cross-validation with sklearn's LeaveOneOut. Also evaluated a 1-D classifier on H_complex alone.
6. Produced final 2-panel figure (scatter + ranked bar) and saved features as class_spectral_features_unified.csv.
